## Bitcoin's Initial Reaction to the U.S.-Iran War
The recent conflict between the United States and Iran has sent shockwaves across global markets, with various assets experiencing significant volatility. Bitcoin, often regarded as a safe-haven asset, initially sold off when the war began. This initial reaction was likely due to the sudden increase in uncertainty and risk aversion among investors. However, two weeks later, Bitcoin has not only recovered but is also outperforming nearly every other asset class.

### Historical Context
To understand Bitcoin's recent outperformance, it's essential to consider the historical context of the cryptocurrency's behavior during times of geopolitical uncertainty. In the past, Bitcoin has often acted as a safe-haven asset, with its price increasing during periods of heightened uncertainty. This is due to its decentralized nature, limited supply, and perceived store of value.
