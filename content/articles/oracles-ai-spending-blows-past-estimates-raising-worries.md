## Oracle's AI Spending Spree: A New Era for the Tech Giant
Oracle's recent announcement that its AI spending has blown past estimates has sent shockwaves throughout the tech industry. The company's aggressive investment in artificial intelligence has raised concerns over its growing debt, leaving investors and analysts wondering if the gamble will pay off.

### Historical Context: Oracle's AI Journey
Oracle's foray into AI began several years ago, with the company making strategic acquisitions and investments in AI startups. The acquisition of Dyn in 2016 marked a significant turning point in Oracle's AI journey, as it gained access to advanced AI-powered cloud infrastructure. Since then, Oracle has made numerous AI-related acquisitions, including DataScience.com and CrowdTwist.

## Fundamentals: Oracle's Financial Health
To understand the implications of Oracle's AI spending spree, it's essential to examine the company's financial health. Oracle's revenue has been steadily increasing over the years, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.5%. However, the company's debt has also been rising, with a current debt-to-equity ratio of 3.45.

#### Risk Factors: Debt and Competition
Oracle's growing debt and increasing competition in the AI space pose significant risks to the company's financial health. The tech giant faces intense competition from established players like Microsoft, Amazon, and Google, as well as newer entrants like Snowflake and Databricks.
