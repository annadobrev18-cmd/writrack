### Historical Context: The Dominance of FICO

For decades, FICO has been the gold standard for credit scoring, with its algorithms and models widely accepted by lenders, creditors, and financial institutions. The company's dominance in the credit scoring market has been unparalleled, with its scores influencing lending decisions across various sectors, including mortgages, credit cards, and personal loans. However, the emergence of alternative credit scoring models has disrupted this status quo, posing a significant threat to FICO's market share.

### The Rise of Alternative Credit Scores

Alternative credit scoring models, such as those offered by VantageScore, TransUnion, and Equifax, have gained traction in recent years. These models often incorporate non-traditional data sources, such as rent payments, utility bills, and social media activity, to provide a more comprehensive picture of an individual's creditworthiness. The adoption of these alternative models by Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, and the FHA marks a significant shift in the credit scoring landscape, as it provides lenders with more options and flexibility in evaluating creditworthiness.
