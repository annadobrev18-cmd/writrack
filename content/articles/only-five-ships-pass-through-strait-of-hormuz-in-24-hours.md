The Strait of Hormuz, a narrow waterway connecting the Persian Gulf to the Arabian Sea, has long been a critical chokepoint in global oil trade. ### Historical Context
The Strait of Hormuz has been a focal point of geopolitical tensions in the Middle East for decades. The waterway is approximately 90 nautical miles long and 21-25 miles wide, making it a narrow and congested shipping route. In 2019, the Strait accounted for approximately 21% of the world's total petroleum liquids production, with an average of 17-20 million barrels per day passing through it. The Strait's strategic importance is further underscored by the fact that it is the only route for oil exports from Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates.
