The U.S. government has imposed sanctions on Venezuela's oil industry in response to the country's political crisis and human rights concerns. The sanctions, which were first introduced in 2019, prohibit U.S. companies from engaging in transactions with the Venezuelan state-owned oil company, Petroleos de Venezuela (PdVSA).

#### Impact on U.S. Companies

The sanctions have had a significant impact on U.S. companies operating in the Venezuelan oil industry. Many companies have been forced to cease operations or scale back their activities in the country. The clarified payment rules are expected to provide greater clarity and certainty for U.S. companies, allowing them to navigate the complex regulatory landscape.

### Clarified Payment Rules

The U.S. Treasury has clarified that U.S. companies can make payments to PdVSA for the purchase of Venezuelan oil, as long as the payments are made through a third-party bank and are subject to certain conditions. The clarified rules also provide guidance on the types of transactions that are permitted, including the purchase of oil and petroleum products.

#### Permitted Transactions

The following transactions are permitted under the clarified rules:
* The purchase of oil and petroleum products from PdVSA
* The payment of fees and royalties to PdVSA
* The provision of goods and services to PdVSA

#### Prohibited Transactions

The following transactions are prohibited under the clarified rules:
* The provision of financing or loans to PdVSA
* The purchase of debt or equity securities issued by PdVSA
* The engagement in any transaction that involves the transfer of funds to PdVSA
