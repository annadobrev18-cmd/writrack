Tungsten, also known as wolfram, has been a crucial metal in the global economy for decades. Its unique properties make it an essential component in the production of high-speed steel, which is used in the manufacture of cutting tools, wear-resistant parts, and other industrial applications. The demand for tungsten has been steadily increasing, driven by the growth of the global steel industry, as well as the expanding electronics and aerospace sectors.
