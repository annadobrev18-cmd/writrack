### Historical Context: Iran's Role in Global Oil Supply

Iran has long been a crucial player in the global oil market, with the country's oil exports accounting for a substantial portion of the world's total oil supply. The Strait of Hormuz, which connects the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman, is a vital waterway that allows oil tankers to transport crude oil from Iran and other Middle Eastern countries to destinations around the world. Any disruption to the security of the Strait of Hormuz would have far-reaching consequences for the global economy, including higher oil prices, reduced trade, and potential shortages of essential goods.
