## XRP's Recent Price Action
XRP, the native cryptocurrency of the Ripple protocol, has experienced a significant decline in its price over the past 24 hours. After attempting to break out above the $1.15 resistance level, XRP's price has fallen by 3%, leaving investors wondering what's next for the cryptocurrency.

### Technical Analysis
From a technical perspective, XRP's breakout attempt was expected to be met with resistance, given the strong $1.15 support level that had been established over the past few weeks. However, the failure to sustain a price above this level has led to a sharp decline, with XRP's price now trading below $1.10.

#### Chart Patterns
Looking at the chart patterns, it's clear that XRP's price action has been dictated by a series of lower highs and lower lows, indicating a clear downtrend. The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is also indicating oversold conditions, which could lead to a bounce in the short term. However, the Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) is still indicating a bearish trend, suggesting that the downward momentum may continue.

## Fundamental Analysis
From a fundamental perspective, XRP's price is heavily influenced by the overall sentiment in the cryptocurrency market. With the recent downturn in the market, XRP's price has been affected, leading to a decline in its value.

### Adoption and Partnerships
Despite the recent price decline, XRP has been making significant strides in terms of adoption and partnerships. Ripple, the company behind XRP, has been actively working with banks and financial institutions to integrate XRP into their systems, which could lead to increased demand and, subsequently, a higher price.

#### Use Cases
XRP has several use cases, including cross-border payments, liquidity provision, and decentralized finance (DeFi). These use cases have the potential to drive adoption and increase the demand for XRP, which could lead to a higher price in the long term.
