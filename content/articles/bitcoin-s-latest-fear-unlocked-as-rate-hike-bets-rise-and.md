### Historical Context

To understand the current market sentiment, it's essential to look at the historical context of Bitcoin's price movement in relation to interest rates. In the past, Bitcoin has shown a strong inverse correlation with the 10-year Treasury yield. When interest rates rise, the appeal of high-yielding assets like bonds increases, making Bitcoin less attractive to investors. Conversely, when interest rates fall, the demand for Bitcoin increases, driving up its price.

#### Rate Hike Bets Rise

The recent rise in rate hike bets has been driven by the Federal Reserve's hawkish stance on inflation. The central bank has signaled its intention to continue raising interest rates to combat high inflation, which has led to a surge in bond yields. The 10-year Treasury yield has risen significantly, making bonds more attractive to investors and reducing the appeal of Bitcoin.

### Market Impact

The crumbling of bond markets has had a significant impact on the cryptocurrency market. The rise in bond yields has led to a decrease in demand for Bitcoin, causing its price to fall. The market capitalization of Bitcoin has also decreased, making it less attractive to institutional investors.
