Michael Burry, the renowned investor of 'Big Short' fame, has once again made headlines with his contrarian stance on Palantir, a leading artificial intelligence software firm. Despite a recent surge in Palantir's stock price, triggered by a post from former US President Donald Trump, Burry has reaffirmed his bet against the company. ### Palantir's Rise to Prominence
Palantir, founded in 2004 by Peter Thiel, Nathan Gettings, Joe Lonsdale, Garry Tan, and Stephen Cohen, has evolved into a leading provider of artificial intelligence and data analytics software. The company's platform, which enables organizations to integrate and analyze large datasets, has been adopted by a diverse range of clients, including government agencies, financial institutions, and healthcare providers. Palantir's innovative approach and impressive client base have contributed to its significant growth, with the company's market capitalization exceeding $20 billion.

### Market Impact: Trump's Post and Palantir's Stock Surge
On April 10, 2026, former US President Donald Trump posted a message on his social media platform, expressing his admiration for Palantir's technology and its potential to support national security initiatives. The post sparked a rapid surge in Palantir's stock price, with the company's shares rising by over 10% in a single trading session. This sudden increase in stock price has been attributed to Trump's endorsement, which has potentially expanded Palantir's visibility and appeal among investors.
