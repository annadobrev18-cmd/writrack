## US-Iran Conflict Sparks Travel Chaos
The recent US strikes on Iran have sent shockwaves through the global airline industry, sparking travel chaos as airlines scramble to cancel and reroute flights. The conflict has raised concerns over safety and geopolitical risk, prompting investors to reassess their portfolios and brace for potential losses.

### Historical Context: Airline Industry and Geopolitical Risk
The airline industry has long been vulnerable to geopolitical risk, with past conflicts and tensions impacting travel demand and airline operations. The 1990-1991 Gulf War, for example, led to a significant decline in air travel, while the 2010-2012 Arab Spring uprisings disrupted flights and affected airline profitability. The current US-Iran conflict is no exception, with airlines taking precautionary measures to ensure passenger safety.

## Expert Opinions: Assessing the Impact of US-Iran Conflict
The US-Iran conflict has sparked a range of opinions from industry experts, with many predicting a significant impact on the airline industry and global economy.

Geopolitical risk experts predict that the conflict will lead to a rise in tensions between the US and Iran, potentially disrupting global supply chains and impacting trade.
