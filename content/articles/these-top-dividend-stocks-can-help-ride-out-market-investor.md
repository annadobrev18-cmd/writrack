## Riding Out Market Volatility with Dividend Stocks
The current market landscape is marked by increased volatility, making it challenging for investors to navigate. However, according to Michael Clarfeld of ClearBridge Investments, certain underappreciated dividend stocks can provide a safe haven. Clarfeld recently highlighted three such stocks that he believes have solid dividends and can help investors weather the market storm.

### Understanding Dividend Stocks
Dividend stocks are companies that distribute a portion of their earnings to shareholders in the form of dividends. These stocks are often attractive to investors seeking regular income and relatively lower volatility. The dividend yield, which is the ratio of the annual dividend payment per share to the stock's current price, is a key metric for evaluating dividend stocks.

#### Historical Performance of Dividend Stocks
Historically, dividend stocks have performed well during periods of market volatility. According to a study by Morningstar, dividend-paying stocks in the S&P 500 index have outperformed non-dividend payers during market downturns. This is because dividend stocks tend to be more stable and less volatile, providing a relatively safer investment option.

### ExxonMobil (XOM)
ExxonMobil is one of the largest oil and gas companies in the world, with a diverse portfolio of energy assets. The company has a long history of paying consistent dividends, with a current dividend yield of 5.1%. ExxonMobil's dividend payout ratio is around 50%, indicating a sustainable dividend policy.

#### Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)
Johnson & Johnson is a multinational healthcare company with a diverse portfolio of pharmaceutical, medical device, and consumer products. The company has increased its dividend payout for 59 consecutive years, with a current dividend yield of 2.7%. Johnson & Johnson's dividend payout ratio is around 40%, indicating a strong ability to sustain its dividend payments.

#### Procter & Gamble (PG)
Procter & Gamble is a multinational consumer goods company with a diverse portfolio of brands, including Tide, Pampers, and Gillette. The company has increased its dividend payout for 65 consecutive years, with a current dividend yield of 2.5%. Procter & Gamble's dividend payout ratio is around 50%, indicating a sustainable dividend policy.

#### Risk Factors
The main risk factors for these dividend stocks include:
* Market volatility: A decline in the overall market can negatively impact the stock prices of these companies.
* Interest rate changes: An increase in interest rates can make dividend stocks less attractive, leading to a decline in their stock prices.
* Industry disruption: The energy, healthcare, and consumer goods industries are subject to disruption from new technologies and changing consumer preferences.
