## Fundamentals of DoorDash
DoorDash, a leading food delivery and logistics company, has been making waves in the stock market despite its recent fourth-quarter earnings miss. The company's strong fundamentals and growth potential have led many Wall Street analysts to retain their conviction, viewing DoorDash as a 'core holding' for internet investors.

### Historical Performance
Since its initial public offering (IPO) in 2020, DoorDash has demonstrated impressive growth, with its revenue increasing from $1.9 billion in 2020 to $6.8 billion in 2022. This significant growth can be attributed to the company's strategic expansion into new markets, both domestically and internationally, as well as its ability to adapt to changing consumer preferences.

### Business Model
DoorDash's business model is centered around its platform, which connects consumers with local restaurants and stores. The company generates revenue through commission fees on orders, as well as delivery fees. This model has proven to be highly scalable, allowing DoorDash to expand its operations rapidly while maintaining a strong profit margin.

## Valuation
Despite the recent earnings miss, DoorDash's valuation remains attractive to many investors. The company's price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio is currently around 10, which is lower than that of its peers in the food delivery industry.

### Peer Comparison

## Risk Factors
While DoorDash has a strong growth potential, there are several risk factors that investors should be aware of. These include:

### Regulatory Risks
The food delivery industry is subject to various regulations, including those related to labor laws, consumer protection, and data privacy. Any changes to these regulations could negatively impact DoorDash's business.

### Competition
The food delivery industry is highly competitive, with many players competing for market share. DoorDash faces competition from established players such as Uber Eats and GrubHub, as well as new entrants.

### Economic Risks
Economic downturns could negatively impact consumer spending on food delivery, which could in turn affect DoorDash's revenue and profitability.

## Competitive Landscape
The competitive landscape of the food delivery industry is constantly evolving, with new players entering the market and existing players expanding their services.

## Future Outlook
Despite the recent earnings miss, DoorDash's strong fundamentals and growth potential make it an attractive investment opportunity for internet investors. The company's ability to adapt to changing consumer preferences and its strategic expansion into new markets position it well for long-term success.

### Growth Drivers
Several growth drivers are expected to contribute to DoorDash's future success, including:

#### Expansion into New Markets
DoorDash is expected to continue expanding into new markets, both domestically and internationally, which will provide a significant growth opportunity.

#### Increasing Demand for Food Delivery
The demand for food delivery is expected to continue growing, driven by changing consumer preferences and the increasing popularity of online ordering.

#### Diversification of Services
DoorDash is expected to continue diversifying its services, including the introduction of new features such as grocery delivery and pick-up, which will provide additional revenue streams.
