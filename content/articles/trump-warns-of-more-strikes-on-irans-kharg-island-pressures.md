## Geopolitical Tensions and Oil Markets
The recent warning by Trump of potential strikes on Iran's Kharg Island has sent shockwaves through the oil markets, highlighting the delicate balance between geopolitical tensions and global energy supply. As the world's most critical oil chokepoint, the Strait of Hormuz, which is located near Kharg Island, is a vital passage for international oil trade. Any disruption to this region could have far-reaching consequences for the global economy.

### Historical Context: Iran-US Relations and Oil Embargoes
The history of Iran-US relations is complex and tumultuous, with periods of heightened tensions and conflict. The 1979 Iranian Revolution marked a significant turning point, leading to the imposition of economic sanctions and an oil embargo by the US. The current tensions between the two nations are rooted in disagreements over Iran's nuclear program, regional influence, and support for militant groups.

#### Impact of Sanctions on Iran's Oil Exports
The reinstatement of US sanctions on Iran in 2018 has significantly impacted the country's oil exports, with production declining by over 1 million barrels per day. This reduction in supply has been partially offset by increased production from other OPEC members, such as Saudi Arabia and the UAE. However, the potential for further disruptions to Iranian oil exports poses a significant risk to global energy markets.
