## Defensive Investing Strategy
In today's volatile market, investors are looking for ways to play defense while still generating returns. One strategy that has gained attention is investing in dividend-paying stocks that consistently buy back their own shares. Wolfe Research has highlighted companies with at least a 10-year track record of repurchasing shares, and certain names also pay dividends.

### Historical Context
Share buybacks have been a popular way for companies to return value to shareholders, especially when interest rates are low. By repurchasing their own shares, companies can reduce the number of outstanding shares, increase earnings per share, and boost stock prices. Over the past decade, many companies have taken advantage of low interest rates to buy back their shares, and some have even increased their dividend payouts.

### Market Impact
The impact of share buybacks on the market has been significant. According to a report by S&P Dow Jones Indices, share buybacks have accounted for a significant portion of the total return of the S&P 500 index over the past decade. In fact, the report found that buybacks have contributed to an average annual return of 2.5% to the S&P 500 index since 2010.

### Technical Analysis
From a technical perspective, stocks with a proven track record of share buybacks and dividend payments tend to outperform the market during times of volatility. These stocks often have a lower beta, which means they are less volatile than the overall market. Additionally, the consistent return of capital to shareholders through buybacks and dividends can provide a floor for the stock price, reducing the risk of significant declines.

### Market Trends
The current market trends suggest that investors are looking for safe-haven assets that can provide a steady stream of income. The yield on the 10-year Treasury note has been declining, making dividend-paying stocks more attractive to income-seeking investors. Additionally, the ongoing trade tensions and global economic uncertainty have increased the appeal of defensive stocks with a proven track record of returning value to shareholders.

### Economic Indicators
The current economic indicators suggest that the US economy is still growing, albeit at a slower pace. The GDP growth rate has been declining, and the labor market is showing signs of slowing down. In this environment, investors are looking for stocks that can provide a steady stream of income and have a proven track record of returning value to shareholders.

### Risk Management
While investing in dividend-paying stocks with a proven track record of share buybacks can be a defensive strategy, it's essential to manage risk. Investors should consider the following factors when investing in these stocks:

* Dividend sustainability: Can the company continue to pay its dividend in the future?
* Share buyback history: Has the company consistently repurchased its shares over the past decade?
* Industry trends: Are the industry trends favorable for the company's growth?
* Valuation: Is the stock overvalued or undervalued compared to its peers?
