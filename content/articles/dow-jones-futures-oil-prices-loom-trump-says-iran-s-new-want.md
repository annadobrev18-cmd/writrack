### Historical Context: The Impact of Oil Prices on the Dow Jones

The relationship between oil prices and the Dow Jones is not new. In the past, we have seen that when oil prices rise, the Dow Jones tends to fall. This is because higher oil prices can lead to higher inflation, which can reduce consumer spending and, in turn, negatively impact the economy. For example, in 2008, when oil prices reached a record high of $147 per barrel, the Dow Jones fell by over 30%. Similarly, in 2014, when oil prices fell by over 50%, the Dow Jones rose by over 10%.
