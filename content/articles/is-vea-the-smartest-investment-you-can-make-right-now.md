## Fundamentals of VEA
The Vanguard FTSE Developed Markets ETF (VEA) is an exchange-traded fund that tracks the FTSE Developed All Cap ex US Index, which includes approximately 4,000 stocks of companies located in developed markets around the world, excluding the United States. VEA offers investors a diversified portfolio of international stocks, providing exposure to various sectors and geographies.

### Historical Performance
Historically, VEA has demonstrated a strong track record of performance, with its net asset value (NAV) steadily increasing over the years. Since its inception in 2007, VEA has provided investors with a return of around 5-6% per annum, making it an attractive option for those seeking long-term growth.

### Diversification Benefits
One of the key benefits of investing in VEA is its ability to provide diversification to a portfolio. By investing in a broad range of international stocks, VEA helps to reduce exposure to any one particular market or sector, thereby minimizing risk. This is particularly important for investors who are heavily invested in the US market, as VEA offers a way to gain exposure to other developed markets around the world.

## Valuation and Fees
VEA is known for its low expense ratio, which currently stands at 0.05%. This makes it one of the most cost-effective ways to gain exposure to international stocks. In comparison, many actively managed international mutual funds have expense ratios that are significantly higher, often ranging from 1-2%.

## Risk Factors
While VEA offers a diversified portfolio of international stocks, there are still several risk factors that investors should be aware of. These include:

### Market Risk
As with any investment in the stock market, there is a risk that the value of VEA could decline if the overall market experiences a downturn. This risk is exacerbated by the fact that VEA is invested in international stocks, which can be more volatile than US stocks.

### Currency Risk
VEA is denominated in US dollars, which means that investors are exposed to currency risk. If the value of the US dollar increases relative to other currencies, the value of VEA could decline, even if the underlying stocks perform well.

### Sector Risk
VEA is invested in a broad range of sectors, but some sectors may be more heavily represented than others. For example, the ETF has a significant weighting in the financials and industrials sectors, which could make it more vulnerable to downturns in these areas.

## Competitive Landscape
The international ETF space is highly competitive, with many different providers offering a range of products. Some of the key competitors to VEA include:

### iShares MSCI EAFE ETF (EFA)
The iShares MSCI EAFE ETF is one of the most popular international ETFs, with over $50 billion in assets under management. It tracks the MSCI EAFE Index, which includes stocks from developed markets in Europe, Australasia, and the Far East.

### iShares Core MSCI EAFE ETF (IEFA)
The iShares Core MSCI EAFE ETF is a lower-cost version of the iShares MSCI EAFE ETF, with an expense ratio of 0.11%. It tracks the MSCI EAFE IMI Index, which includes small-cap stocks in addition to large- and mid-cap stocks.

### SPDR Portfolio Developed World ETF (SPDW)
The SPDR Portfolio Developed World ETF is a low-cost ETF that tracks the S&P Developed Ex US BMI Index. It has an expense ratio of 0.30% and is designed to provide broad exposure to developed markets around the world.
