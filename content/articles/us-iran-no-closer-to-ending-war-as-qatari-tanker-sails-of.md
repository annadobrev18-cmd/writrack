### Historical Context

The US-Iran conflict has its roots in the 1979 Iranian Revolution, which led to the overthrow of the US-backed Shah of Iran. Since then, the two countries have had a tumultuous relationship, with periods of heightened tensions and conflict. The current escalation began in 2018, when the US withdrew from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), also known as the Iran nuclear deal.

### Economic Implications

The conflict has significant economic implications, particularly for the global oil market. Iran is the second-largest oil producer in the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC), and any disruption to its oil exports could lead to a significant increase in oil prices. The recent surge in oil prices has already had a ripple effect on the global economy, with many countries experiencing higher fuel costs and increased inflation.

#### Oil Price Volatility

The oil price volatility caused by the US-Iran conflict has been significant. The price of Brent crude oil has increased by over 10% in the past month, reaching a high of over $70 per barrel. ### Market Analysis

The US-Iran conflict has also had a significant impact on the global stock market.
