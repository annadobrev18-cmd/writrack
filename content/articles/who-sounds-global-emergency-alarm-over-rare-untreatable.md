### Historical Context: Previous Ebola Outbreaks

To understand the potential economic impact of the current outbreak, it is essential to examine the historical context of previous Ebola outbreaks. The 2014-2016 Ebola outbreak in West Africa, which affected Guinea, Liberia, and Sierra Leone, had a devastating economic impact on the region. The outbreak resulted in a significant decline in economic activity, with the three affected countries experiencing a combined loss of over $2.2 billion in GDP.

| Country | GDP Loss (2014-2016) | Economic Impact |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Guinea | $530 million | 10% decline in economic activity |
| Liberia | $770 million | 15% decline in economic activity |
| Sierra Leone | $920 million | 20% decline in economic activity |

The economic impact of the outbreak was not limited to the affected countries. The outbreak also had a significant impact on the global economy, with trade and investment flows to the region declining significantly.
