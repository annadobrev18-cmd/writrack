### Historical Context
The concept of leveraged ETFs is not new, but its introduction in South Korea marks a significant milestone. Leveraged ETFs are designed to provide investors with amplified exposure to the performance of an underlying asset, in this case, Samsung and SK Hynix stocks. The idea is to offer investors a chance to capitalize on the potential growth of these companies while managing risk.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What are leveraged stock ETFs, and how do they work?
Leveraged stock ETFs are designed to provide investors with amplified exposure to the performance of an underlying asset. They work by using debt to amplify the returns of the underlying asset, which can lead to higher potential returns, but also higher potential losses.
2. What are the risks associated with investing in leveraged stock ETFs?
The risks associated with investing in leveraged stock ETFs include the potential for significant losses, as the amplified exposure to the performance of the underlying asset can work against investors if the market moves in the opposite direction. Additionally, leveraged ETFs can be highly volatile, which can make them unsuitable for investors who are not comfortable with high levels of risk.
3. How can investors capitalize on the launch of leveraged stock ETFs on Samsung and SK Hynix?
Investors can capitalize on the launch of leveraged stock ETFs on Samsung and SK Hynix by investing in these ETFs and taking advantage of the potential growth of the underlying assets. However, it is essential to carefully consider the risks associated with leveraged investing and to develop a strategy that takes into account the potential for significant losses.
