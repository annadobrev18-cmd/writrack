### Historical Context: Retail Investors and Market Volatility

Retail investors, also known as mom-and-pop investors, have historically been prone to making emotional decisions during periods of market turmoil. The 2020 COVID-19 pandemic and the subsequent market crash are a prime example of this phenomenon. As the markets plummeted, many retail investors panicked, selling their holdings and exacerbating the decline. However, a subset of retail investors took a contrarian approach, buying the dip and positioning themselves for the eventual rebound.

### The Two Favorite Trades

According to recent data, retail investors have been flocking to two primary trades amidst the current market uncertainty:

1. **Gold and Other Safe-Haven Assets**: As tensions between the US and Iran escalate, retail investors have been seeking refuge in traditional safe-haven assets such as gold, silver, and Treasury bonds. This is a classic risk-off trade, as investors seek to protect their portfolios from potential losses.
2. **Defense and Aerospace Stocks**: The other favored trade among retail investors is defense and aerospace stocks, such as Lockheed Martin, Boeing, and Raytheon Technologies. This trade is likely driven by the expectation of increased military spending and potential conflict in the region.
