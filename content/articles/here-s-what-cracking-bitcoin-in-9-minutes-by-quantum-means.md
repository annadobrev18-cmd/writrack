### Technical Analysis of Quantum Computing Power
The technical capability to crack bitcoin in 9 minutes implies a significant quantum computing power. Currently, the most powerful quantum computers are those developed by tech giants like Google and IBM. However, the specifics of the quantum computer in question, such as the number of qubits and the quantum volume, are crucial for understanding its capabilities.

#### Qubit Count and Quantum Volume
A higher number of qubits and a larger quantum volume indicate a more powerful quantum computer. For a quantum computer to pose a significant threat to bitcoin, it would need to have a substantial number of qubits, likely in the hundreds or thousands, and a high quantum volume to maintain coherence and perform complex calculations.

#### Error Correction and Quantum Algorithms
Another critical aspect of quantum computing is error correction. Quantum computers are prone to errors due to the fragile nature of quantum states. Developing robust error correction techniques is essential for large-scale quantum computations. Additionally, the choice of quantum algorithm, such as Shor's algorithm for factorization and discrete logarithm problems, plays a crucial role in the efficiency of the quantum computer in cracking cryptographic codes.

### Expert Opinions and Mitigation Strategies
Experts in the field of cryptography and quantum computing have weighed in on the potential threat to bitcoin. Some believe that the development of quantum-resistant cryptographic algorithms and protocols is essential for the long-term security of bitcoin. Others suggest that the implementation of quantum key distribution (QKD) could provide an additional layer of security.

#### Post-Quantum Cryptography
The transition to post-quantum cryptography involves replacing current cryptographic algorithms with quantum-resistant ones. This could include lattice-based cryptography, code-based cryptography, or multivariate cryptography. Implementing these new algorithms and protocols would require significant updates to the bitcoin network, including changes to wallet software and transaction verification processes.

#### Quantum Key Distribution (QKD)
QKD offers a method of secure key exchange that is theoretically secure against any computational attack, including those from quantum computers. Integrating QKD into the bitcoin network could enhance security but would also introduce new complexities and infrastructure requirements.
