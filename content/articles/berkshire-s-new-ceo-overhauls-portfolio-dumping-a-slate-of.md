## Berkshire's New CEO Overhauls Portfolio
Berkshire Hathaway's equity portfolio has undergone one of its most significant renovations in recent history, with new CEO Greg Abel at the helm. During his first three months as CEO, Abel has overseen a major overhaul of the company's investment portfolio, dumping a slate of stocks and signaling a new direction for the conglomerate.

### Background on Berkshire Hathaway
Berkshire Hathaway is a multinational conglomerate led by Warren Buffett, who served as CEO from 1970 to 2025. The company has a diverse portfolio of businesses, including insurance, retail, manufacturing, and finance. Berkshire Hathaway is known for its value investing approach, which emphasizes buying undervalued companies with strong fundamentals.

### The Overhaul Under Greg Abel
Greg Abel, who took over as CEO in 2025, has brought a new perspective to the company's investment strategy. Abel has been with Berkshire Hathaway since 2000 and has served as the company's vice chairman of non-insurance operations. Under his leadership, Berkshire Hathaway has dumped a significant number of stocks, including some long-held positions.

Some of the notable stocks sold by Berkshire Hathaway during the overhaul include:
