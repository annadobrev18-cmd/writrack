A recent survey revealed that nearly half of Americans buy stocks based on FOMO, highlighting the emotional aspect of investing. This phenomenon is particularly relevant in the context of highly anticipated initial public offerings (IPOs), such as the upcoming SpaceX IPO.

### Historical Context of FOMO in the Stock Market

FOMO has been a persistent factor in the stock market, with investors often making impulsive decisions based on fear of missing out on potential gains. This behavior is not unique to the current market environment, as it has been observed in various forms throughout history. For instance, during the dot-com bubble of the late 1990s, investors flocked to tech stocks, driven by the fear of missing out on the perceived opportunities.

#### FOMO and Market Volatility

The FOMO effect can contribute to market volatility, as investors rush to buy or sell stocks based on emotional decisions rather than fundamental analysis. This can lead to rapid price movements, making it challenging for investors to make informed decisions. The SpaceX IPO is likely to be a highly volatile event, with investors closely watching the stock's performance and adjusting their positions accordingly.
