## The Background
The state of California has recently urged the court and the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) to rule that AT&T must continue to provide basic phone services to its customers. This move comes as a response to AT&T's plans to discontinue its traditional landline phone services in favor of more modern alternatives.

### The Importance of Basic Phone Services
Basic phone services, including landline phones, are still widely used by many individuals, particularly in rural areas where access to more advanced technologies may be limited. These services are often considered essential, as they provide a reliable means of communication in emergency situations.

#### Historical Context
The use of landline phones dates back to the late 19th century, and they have been a staple of modern life for over a century. However, with the advent of new technologies, such as cell phones and Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP), the use of landline phones has declined significantly. Despite this, many people still rely on these services, and their discontinuation could have significant consequences.

## The Players Involved
The main players involved in this dispute are the state of California, AT&T, and the FCC.

### AT&T
AT&T is one of the largest telecommunications companies in the world, providing a range of services, including phone, internet, and television. The company has been seeking to discontinue its traditional landline phone services, citing declining demand and the need to focus on more modern technologies.

### The FCC
The FCC is an independent agency of the US government, responsible for regulating interstate and international communications by radio, television, wire, satellite, and cable. The agency has the authority to rule on matters related to telecommunications, including the provision of basic phone services.

### The State of California
The state of California has taken a strong stance on the issue, urging the court and the FCC to rule that AT&T must continue to provide basic phone services. The state argues that these services are essential, particularly for vulnerable populations, such as the elderly and those living in rural areas.
