## The 'Magnificent Seven' Stocks: A Historical Context
The 'Magnificent Seven' stocks, comprising Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet, Facebook, Tesla, and Nvidia, have been the driving force behind the US stock market's bull run over the past decade. However, the current year has been rough for these megacaps, with many of them experiencing significant declines in their stock prices. Despite this, one firm has noted that these stocks are now at their cheapest levels in a decade, sparking interest among investors.

### Current Valuations
The price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio, a key metric used to evaluate a stock's valuation, has declined significantly for the 'Magnificent Seven' stocks. As of the current date, the average P/E ratio for these stocks is around 20, down from a peak of over 30 in 2021. This decline in valuations has made these stocks more attractive to investors, particularly those with a long-term perspective.
