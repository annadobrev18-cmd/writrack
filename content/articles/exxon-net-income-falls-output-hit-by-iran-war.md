Exxon's recent earnings report has sent shockwaves through the oil industry, with the company's net income taking a significant hit due to the ongoing conflict in Iran. Exxon has a long history of navigating complex geopolitical situations, including conflicts in the Middle East. In the 1990s, the company successfully mitigated the impact of the Gulf War on its operations, and in the 2000s, it adapted to the changing landscape of the Iraq War. However, the current conflict in Iran poses a unique set of challenges, with the company's output in the region being severely curtailed.
