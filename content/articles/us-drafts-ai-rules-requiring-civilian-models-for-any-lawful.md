## US AI Regulatory Landscape: An Overview
The US government has recently drafted rules for Artificial Intelligence (AI), requiring civilian models for any lawful use. This move is expected to have significant implications for the tech industry, particularly for companies involved in AI development and deployment.

### Historical Context
The development and use of AI have been rapidly increasing over the past decade, with applications in various industries such as healthcare, finance, and transportation. However, concerns have been raised regarding the potential risks and biases associated with AI systems. In response, governments and regulatory bodies have started to take notice and develop guidelines to ensure the safe and responsible use of AI.

#### Early Regulatory Efforts
One of the earliest regulatory efforts in the US was the establishment of the National Science and Technology Council's (NSTC) Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence (MLAI) Subcommittee in 2016. The subcommittee aimed to coordinate federal efforts in AI research and development, as well as to identify potential regulatory barriers to innovation.

### Market Impact
The drafted AI rules are expected to have a significant impact on the US tech industry, particularly for companies involved in AI development and deployment. The rules may lead to increased costs and complexity for companies, as they will need to ensure that their AI systems comply with the new regulations.

#### Affected Industries
Some of the industries that may be most affected by the drafted AI rules include:
* Healthcare: AI is widely used in healthcare for applications such as medical imaging and patient diagnosis. The new rules may require healthcare companies to re-evaluate their AI systems to ensure compliance.
* Finance: AI is used in finance for applications such as risk management and portfolio optimization. The new rules may require financial institutions to re-assess their AI systems to ensure compliance.
* Technology: Tech companies such as Google, Amazon, and Microsoft may be most affected by the new rules, as they are heavily involved in AI development and deployment.

### Expert Opinions
Experts in the field have mixed opinions regarding the drafted AI rules. Some believe that the rules are necessary to ensure the safe and responsible use of AI, while others believe that the rules may stifle innovation and creativity.

#### Pro-Regulation
Some experts believe that the drafted AI rules are necessary to prevent potential risks and biases associated with AI systems. For example, a recent report by the MIT Initiative on the Digital Economy found that AI systems can perpetuate existing social biases if they are not designed and trained carefully.

#### Anti-Regulation
On the other hand, some experts believe that the drafted AI rules may stifle innovation and creativity in the tech industry. For example, a recent report by the Information Technology and Innovation Foundation found that over-regulation of AI could lead to decreased investment and innovation in the field.
