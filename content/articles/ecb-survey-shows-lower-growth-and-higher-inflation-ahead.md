This survey, which is conducted regularly to gauge the sentiment of various stakeholders, including banks, financial institutions, and businesses, provides valuable insights into the future of the European economy.

The European economy has been facing numerous challenges in recent years, including the COVID-19 pandemic, the Ukraine-Russia conflict, and the resulting supply chain disruptions. These factors have contributed to a slowdown in economic growth and a rise in inflation rates.

#### Pre-Pandemic Era
In the pre-pandemic era, the European economy was experiencing a period of moderate growth, with the GDP growth rate averaging around 2%. However, the pandemic led to a significant decline in economic activity, with the GDP growth rate contracting by over 6% in 2020.

#### Post-Pandemic Era
In the post-pandemic era, the European economy has been experiencing a slow and uneven recovery. The GDP growth rate has been improving, but it remains below the pre-pandemic levels. The inflation rate, on the other hand, has been rising, driven by factors such as supply chain disruptions, higher energy prices, and an increase in demand.
