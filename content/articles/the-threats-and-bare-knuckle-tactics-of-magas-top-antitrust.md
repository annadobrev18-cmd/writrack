### Historical Context of Antitrust Laws
Antitrust laws have been a cornerstone of the US economic system, aiming to promote competition and prevent monopolies. The Sherman Antitrust Act of 1890 was the first federal law to outlaw monopolistic practices, and it has been followed by several other laws, including the Clayton Antitrust Act of 1914 and the Federal Trade Commission Act of 1914. These laws have been instrumental in shaping the US market, ensuring that companies compete fairly and that consumers have access to a wide range of products and services.
