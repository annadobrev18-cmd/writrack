The global energy market has been on high alert for the past year, with many analysts predicting a significant spike in oil prices due to various geopolitical tensions and supply chain disruptions. However, despite these concerns, the oil market has remained surprisingly stable, with prices fluctuating within a relatively narrow range.

### Understanding the Factors That Influence Oil Prices

#### Global Demand
Global demand for oil has been steadily increasing over the past decade, driven by growing economies in Asia and other emerging markets. However, this demand has been largely offset by improvements in energy efficiency and the increasing adoption of renewable energy sources.

#### Supply Chain Disruptions
Supply chain disruptions, such as those caused by natural disasters or geopolitical conflicts, can have a significant impact on oil prices. However, the oil market has become increasingly resilient to these types of disruptions, with many producers and refineries implementing robust contingency plans to minimize the impact of any disruptions.

#### Geopolitical Tensions
Geopolitical tensions, particularly in the Middle East, have been a major concern for the oil market in recent years. However, despite these tensions, the oil market has remained relatively stable, with many producers and consumers implementing hedging strategies to mitigate the risks associated with price volatility.

#### Value of the US Dollar
The value of the US dollar also plays a significant role in determining oil prices. A strong US dollar can make oil more expensive for consumers in other countries, which can lead to reduced demand and lower prices. Conversely, a weak US dollar can make oil cheaper for consumers in other countries, which can lead to increased demand and higher prices.
