## Stablecoins Under Regulatory Scrutiny
The international finance watchdog has issued a warning that stablecoins are increasingly being used in sanctions evasion and money laundering. This development has significant implications for the cryptocurrency market, as it highlights the ongoing challenges in regulating digital assets. Stablecoins, which are designed to maintain a stable value relative to a fiat currency, have gained popularity in recent years due to their potential for reducing volatility in cryptocurrency transactions.

### Historical Context of Stablecoins
Stablecoins have been around since 2014, but they gained significant traction in 2018 with the launch of USD Coin (USDC) and Paxos Standard (PAX). These early stablecoins were designed to be fully backed by reserves of fiat currency, which would be held in escrow by a trusted third party. However, as the market evolved, other types of stablecoins emerged, including those that are algorithmically stabilized or backed by other cryptocurrencies.
