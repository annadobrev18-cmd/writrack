## Fundamentals of Biogen's Business
Biogen is a biotechnology company that has been at the forefront of developing innovative medicines for various diseases. The company's product portfolio includes several successful drugs, with Ocrevus being one of its top-selling medicines. Ocrevus is a treatment for multiple sclerosis and has been a significant contributor to Biogen's revenue. However, with the patent for Ocrevus set to expire, Biogen's royalties from this drug are expected to shrink.

### Impact of Patent Expiration on Royalties
The expiration of the Ocrevus patent will allow other companies to manufacture generic versions of the drug, which will likely lead to a decrease in Biogen's royalties. According to a report by Piper Sandler, Biogen's royalties for Ocrevus could decline by as much as 50% after the patent expires. This decline in royalties will undoubtedly have an impact on Biogen's revenue, but the company has been preparing for this eventuality by developing other medicines that will support its growth.

### Diversification of Product Portfolio
Biogen has been actively developing new medicines to diversify its product portfolio and reduce its dependence on Ocrevus. The company has a strong pipeline of potential new drugs, including treatments for multiple sclerosis, Alzheimer's disease, and other neurological disorders. Some of the notable medicines in Biogen's pipeline include Vumerity, a treatment for relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis, and Aducanumab, a treatment for Alzheimer's disease.

## Valuation of Biogen's Stock

The stock's valuation is supported by its solid financials, with a price-to-earnings ratio of 25.6, which is in line with the industry average.

## Risk Factors
While Biogen's growth prospects look promising, there are several risk factors that investors should be aware of. One of the main risks is the uncertainty surrounding the approval and commercialization of the company's new medicines. The development of new drugs is a complex and time-consuming process, and there is always a risk that a medicine may not receive regulatory approval or may not be commercially successful.

### Regulatory Risks
The regulatory environment for biotechnology companies is highly uncertain, with changing regulations and guidelines affecting the approval and commercialization of new medicines. Biogen is subject to various regulatory risks, including the risk of non-approval of its new medicines or changes in labeling requirements for its existing medicines.

### Competitive Risks
The biotechnology industry is highly competitive, with several large and small companies competing for market share. Biogen faces competition from other companies that are developing similar medicines, which could impact the company's sales and revenue growth.

## Competitive Landscape
