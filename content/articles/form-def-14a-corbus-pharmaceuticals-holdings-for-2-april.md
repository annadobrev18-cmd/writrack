## Recent Filing Analysis
The recent Form DEF 14A filing by Corbus Pharmaceuticals Holdings on April 2 has garnered significant attention from investors and market analysts. This filing is a definitive proxy statement, which provides detailed information about the company's governance, executive compensation, and other matters that will be voted on by shareholders at the upcoming annual meeting.

### Overview of Corbus Pharmaceuticals
Corbus Pharmaceuticals is a clinical-stage pharmaceutical company focused on the development and commercialization of novel therapeutics for the treatment of inflammatory and fibrotic diseases. The company's lead product candidate, lenabasum, is a synthetic, oral, small-molecule cannabis-like drug designed to resolve chronic inflammation and fibrosis.

The DEF 14A filing provides insight into the company's executive compensation, board composition, and shareholder proposals. Some key takeaways from the filing include:
* The company's CEO, Yuval Cohen, received a total compensation package of $1.3 million in 2022, which includes a base salary of $525,000, stock awards of $425,000, and other compensation of $350,000.
* The board of directors has proposed the election of two new directors, both of whom have significant experience in the pharmaceutical industry.
