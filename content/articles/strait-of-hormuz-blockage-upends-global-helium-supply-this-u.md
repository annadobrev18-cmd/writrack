The recent blockage of the Strait of Hormuz has sent shockwaves throughout the global energy market, with far-reaching implications for companies like Exxon Mobil. ### Historical Context of the Strait of Hormuz
The Strait of Hormuz is a critical waterway that connects the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman, with approximately 20% of the world's oil supply passing through it. The strait has been a focal point of geopolitical tensions in the past, with various incidents and conflicts impacting global energy markets. The current blockage is no exception, with the potential to significantly impact the global helium supply.
