### Background on US Tariff Exemptions

The US tariffs on steel and aluminum were imposed in 2018, with a 25% tariff on steel imports and a 10% tariff on aluminum imports. The tariffs were imposed under Section 232 of the Trade Expansion Act, which allows the President to impose tariffs on imports that are deemed a threat to national security. The tariffs have had a significant impact on the steel and aluminum industries, with many manufacturers seeking exemptions to avoid the costly tariffs.

### The Exemption Process

The process for obtaining a tariff exemption is complex and time-consuming. Manufacturers must submit an application to the US Department of Commerce, which reviews the application and determines whether the product is eligible for an exemption. The application process involves providing detailed information about the product, including its composition, production process, and uses.
