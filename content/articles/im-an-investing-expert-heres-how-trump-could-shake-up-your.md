## Risk Factors: What Investors Should Consider
When investing in bonds, it's essential to consider the potential risks associated with Trump's policies. Some key risk factors include:

### Inflation Risk
If Trump's policies lead to higher inflation, bond yields will increase, causing bond prices to decrease. This means that investors who hold bonds with fixed interest rates may see the purchasing power of their investments decrease over time.

### Interest Rate Risk
If interest rates rise, bond yields will increase, causing bond prices to decrease. This means that investors who hold bonds with fixed interest rates may see the value of their investments decrease if they need to sell their bonds before maturity.

### Credit Risk
If Trump's policies lead to a decrease in the creditworthiness of bond issuers, bond yields will increase, causing bond prices to decrease. This means that investors who hold bonds with lower credit ratings may see the value of their investments decrease if the issuer defaults.
