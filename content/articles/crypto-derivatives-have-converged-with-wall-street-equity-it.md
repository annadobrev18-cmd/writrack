### The Rise of Crypto Derivatives

Crypto derivatives have experienced exponential growth in recent years, with the total value of outstanding contracts surpassing $1 trillion. This surge in popularity can be attributed to the increasing adoption of cryptocurrencies as a legitimate asset class, as well as the growing demand for hedging and speculative instruments. Crypto derivatives have provided investors with a means to gain exposure to the crypto market while minimizing risk, thereby increasing their appeal to institutional investors.

### Equity Perps: A New Frontier

Equity perps, which allow investors to take long or short positions on traditional stocks, are gaining traction as a viable alternative to crypto perps. These contracts are perpetual, meaning they have no expiration date, and are typically traded on decentralized exchanges (DEXs) or specialized platforms. Equity perps offer several advantages over traditional stock trading, including increased liquidity, lower fees, and the ability to trade with leverage.

#### Key Characteristics of Equity Perps

* **Perpetual contracts**: No expiration date, allowing investors to maintain positions indefinitely
* **Decentralized trading**: Traded on DEXs or specialized platforms, reducing counterparty risk
* **Leverage**: Investors can trade with leverage, amplifying potential gains and losses
* **Lower fees**: Reduced fees compared to traditional stock trading, increasing net returns

## Valuation and Risk Factors

When evaluating equity perps, investors must consider several key factors, including valuation, risk, and liquidity. The valuation of equity perps is typically based on the underlying stock price, with the contract price reflecting the market's expectation of future price movements. However, the use of leverage and the perpetual nature of these contracts introduce additional risks, such as liquidation and market volatility.

The following table highlights key valuation metrics for equity perps:

| Metric | Description | Example |

| **Underlying stock price** | The current price of the underlying stock | $90 |
| **Leverage** | The amount of leverage used to trade the equity perp | 5x |
| **Liquidation price** | The price at which the position will be liquidated | $80 |

### Risk Factors

Investors must be aware of the following risk factors when trading equity perps:

* **Market volatility**: Sudden changes in market conditions can result in significant losses
* **Liquidity risk**: Insufficient liquidity can lead to difficulty entering or exiting positions
* **Counterparty risk**: The risk of default by the counterparty, resulting in losses
