The recent escalation of the Iran war has sent shockwaves through the global economy, with the chemicals sector being one of the most affected. As tensions rise, the price of key chemical commodities has surged, driving up the stock prices of chemical manufacturers. Citi has taken notice of this trend and has upgraded a prominent chemical manufacturer to 'buy' from 'neutral', citing the potential for further price hikes.

### Impact on Chemical Prices
The Iran war has disrupted global supply chains, leading to a shortage of key chemical commodities. This shortage has driven up prices, making chemical manufacturers more profitable. The most affected chemicals include petrochemicals, fertilizers, and plastics. As the conflict continues to escalate, it is likely that prices will remain high, driving up the stock prices of chemical manufacturers.
