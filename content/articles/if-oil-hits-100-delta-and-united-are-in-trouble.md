The recent surge in oil prices has sent shockwaves through the aviation industry, with Delta and United Airlines being particularly vulnerable to the fluctuations in the energy market. ### Historical Context: Oil Prices and the Aviation Industry
The relationship between oil prices and the aviation industry is intricately linked. The cost of fuel is one of the largest expenses for airlines, accounting for approximately 30% of their total operating costs. Historically, fluctuations in oil prices have had a significant impact on the profitability of airlines. For instance, during the 2008 financial crisis, oil prices soared to $147 per barrel, resulting in significant losses for airlines. Conversely, when oil prices plummeted to $30 per barrel in 2016, airlines experienced a surge in profitability.
