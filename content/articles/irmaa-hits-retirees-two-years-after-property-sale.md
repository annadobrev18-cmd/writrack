### Historical Context of IRMAA

IRMAA was introduced as part of the Medicare Modernization Act of 2003. The primary purpose of IRMAA is to adjust the Medicare Part B and Part D premiums based on the beneficiary's income level. The idea behind IRMAA is to ensure that higher-income beneficiaries contribute more to the Medicare program, thereby reducing the financial burden on the system. However, the implementation of IRMAA has been criticized for its complexity and the potential for unexpected tax liabilities.

The IRMAA surcharge brackets are based on the beneficiary's modified adjusted gross income (MAGI). The brackets are as follows:

| MAGI | IRMAA Surcharge |
| --- | --- |
