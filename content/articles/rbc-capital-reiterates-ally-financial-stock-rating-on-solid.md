### Q1 Results Overview

Ally Financial's Q1 results showcased a strong performance, with key financial metrics exceeding expectations. The company reported a significant increase in revenue, driven by growth in its automotive and mortgage lending segments. Net income also saw a substantial boost, reflecting the company's ability to manage expenses and maintain a healthy balance sheet.

### Financial Metrics Comparison

The following table provides a comparison of Ally Financial's financial metrics for Q1:

| Metric | Q1 2026 | Q1 2025 | Change |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Revenue | $2.1B | $1.8B | 16.7% |
| Net Income | $434M | $351M | 23.6% |
| EPS | $1.13 | $0.92 | 22.8% |
| Return on Equity (ROE) | 14.1% | 12.3% | 1.8% |

As seen in the table, Ally Financial's revenue, net income, and EPS have all increased significantly compared to the same period last year. The company's ROE has also improved, indicating a more efficient use of shareholder capital.

### RBC Capital's Rating Reiteration

RBC Capital's decision to reiterate Ally Financial's stock rating is a vote of confidence in the company's ability to sustain its strong performance. The rating is based on the company's solid Q1 results, as well as its potential for long-term growth. RBC Capital's analyst noted that Ally Financial's diversified business model, which includes automotive and mortgage lending, as well as online banking and investment services, positions the company for success in a variety of market conditions.
