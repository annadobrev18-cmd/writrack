### Background of the Clarity Act

The Clarity Act, also known as the Cryptocurrency Clarity Act, is a bill introduced in the US Congress aimed at providing regulatory clarity for the cryptocurrency industry. The bill seeks to amend the Securities Act of 1933 and the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 to exclude digital assets from the definition of a security. This would provide a clear framework for the regulation of cryptocurrencies, allowing companies to operate without the fear of being classified as security issuers.

The passage of the Clarity Act would have significant implications for the cryptocurrency industry.
