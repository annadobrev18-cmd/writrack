## Impact of Rising Raw Materials Costs on Sherwin-Williams
The recent downgrade of Sherwin-Williams by Wells Fargo due to rising raw materials costs, exacerbated by the Iran conflict, has significant implications for the paint industry. As a major player in the industry, Sherwin-Williams' profitability is heavily influenced by the cost of raw materials, such as titanium dioxide, which is a key component in paint production.

### Historical Context of Raw Materials Costs
Historically, the cost of raw materials has been a significant factor in determining the profitability of paint manufacturers. The price of titanium dioxide, in particular, has been volatile, with prices fluctuating in response to changes in global demand and supply. In recent years, the price of titanium dioxide has increased significantly, driven by growing demand from the construction and automotive industries.

### Iran Conflict and Raw Materials Costs
The recent conflict in Iran has further exacerbated the increase in raw materials costs, as it has disrupted global supply chains and led to a shortage of certain raw materials. The conflict has also led to an increase in oil prices, which has a ripple effect on the cost of raw materials.
