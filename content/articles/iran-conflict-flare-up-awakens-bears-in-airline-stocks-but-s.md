The recent flare-up in the Iran conflict has sent shockwaves through the global markets, with airline stocks being particularly hard hit. The U.S. Global Jets ETF, which tracks the performance of the airline industry, has extended its two-day decline, falling by 4% on Wednesday. This downturn comes after the ETF had challenged all-time highs just last week. As investors navigate this volatile landscape, it's essential to examine the underlying factors driving this trend and assess the potential risks and opportunities that lie ahead.

### Historical Context: Airline Stocks and Geopolitical Tensions
The airline industry has long been susceptible to geopolitical tensions, with conflicts in key regions often leading to increased volatility in airline stocks. The Iran conflict is the latest in a series of global events that have impacted the industry, including the COVID-19 pandemic, the Russia-Ukraine conflict, and rising oil prices. Historically, airline stocks have been sensitive to changes in oil prices, as fuel costs account for a significant portion of an airline's operating expenses. Furthermore, geopolitical tensions can lead to increased security measures, travel restrictions, and decreased demand, all of which can negatively impact airline stocks.
