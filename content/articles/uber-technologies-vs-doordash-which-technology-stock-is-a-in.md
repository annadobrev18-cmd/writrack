## Fundamentals of Uber Technologies and DoorDash
Uber Technologies and DoorDash are two prominent players in the technology sector, specifically in the food delivery and ride-hailing industries. To determine which stock is a better buy in 2026, it's essential to analyze their fundamentals.

### Revenue Growth
Uber Technologies has consistently demonstrated strong revenue growth, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 18.4% over the past five years. In contrast, DoorDash has shown a CAGR of 24.1% during the same period. However, it's crucial to consider the base effect, as DoorDash's revenue was significantly lower than Uber's in the initial years.

### Profitability
Uber Technologies has been investing heavily in its various segments, including ride-hailing, food delivery, and freight. As a result, the company has reported net losses in recent years. In contrast, DoorDash has managed to maintain profitability, with a net income margin of 3.4% in 2025.

### Market Share
Uber Technologies dominates the ride-hailing market, with a market share of approximately 68% in the United States. DoorDash, on the other hand, leads the food delivery market, with a market share of around 59% in the United States.

## Valuation
The valuation of Uber Technologies and DoorDash can be assessed using various metrics, including price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio, price-to-sales (P/S) ratio, and enterprise value-to-EBITDA (EV/EBITDA) ratio.

### Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
The P/E ratio of Uber Technologies is currently around 25.6, while DoorDash's P/E ratio is approximately 34.1. This suggests that DoorDash's stock is more expensive than Uber's, based on earnings.

### Price-to-Sales (P/S) Ratio
The P/S ratio of Uber Technologies is around 2.3, while DoorDash's P/S ratio is approximately 4.1. This indicates that DoorDash's stock is more expensive than Uber's, based on sales.

### Enterprise Value-to-EBITDA (EV/EBITDA) Ratio
The EV/EBITDA ratio of Uber Technologies is around 14.5, while DoorDash's EV/EBITDA ratio is approximately 20.3. This suggests that DoorDash's stock is more expensive than Uber's, based on EBITDA.

## Risk Factors
Both Uber Technologies and DoorDash face various risk factors that could impact their stock performance.

### Regulatory Risks
Uber Technologies and DoorDash are subject to regulatory risks, particularly in the ride-hailing and food delivery industries. Changes in laws and regulations could negatively impact their business models.

### Competitive Risks

### Economic Risks
The COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on the global economy, and the ride-hailing and food delivery industries have not been immune. Economic downturns could negatively impact Uber Technologies and DoorDash's revenue and profitability.
