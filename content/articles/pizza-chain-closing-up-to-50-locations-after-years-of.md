The recent announcement that a pizza chain is closing up to 50 locations after years of decline is a stark reminder of the economic headwinds facing the restaurant industry. ### Rising Labor Costs
One of the main challenges facing the restaurant industry is the rising cost of labor. The minimum wage has increased in many states, and restaurants are struggling to absorb these costs without passing them on to consumers. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average hourly wage for food preparation and serving related occupations has increased by 10% over the past year, outpacing the overall wage growth in the US economy.

### Shift in Consumer Behavior
The way people consume food is changing, with more emphasis on convenience, sustainability, and health.
