## SLB and Baker Hughes: A New Era in Oil Exploration
The recent escalation of the Iran war has sent shockwaves through the global energy market, leading to a significant increase in oil prices. This surge has prompted oil giants SLB (formerly Schlumberger) and Baker Hughes to revise their forecasts, anticipating a substantial rise in oil exploration spending. As the world grapples with the consequences of supply disruptions, these two industry leaders are poised to capitalize on the growing demand for oil and gas.

### Historical Context: Oil Price Volatility
The oil market has long been characterized by its volatility, with prices influenced by a complex array of factors, including geopolitical tensions, global demand, and production levels. The Iran war has introduced a new layer of uncertainty, exacerbating existing concerns about supply chain disruptions and the potential for further conflict in the region. As seen in the past, such events can have a profound impact on oil prices, with the 1973 oil embargo and the 1990 Gulf War serving as notable examples.
