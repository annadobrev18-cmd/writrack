## EU's Regulatory Storm: Google in the Crosshairs
The European Union (EU) has been at the forefront of regulating big tech companies, and Google has been a prime target. According to recent reports from Handelsblatt, the EU is planning to fine Google a staggering high triple-digit million euro sum. This move is part of the EU's ongoing efforts to crack down on anticompetitive practices in the tech industry.

### Background: EU's Antitrust Efforts
The EU has been actively pursuing antitrust cases against major tech companies, including Google, Amazon, Facebook, and Apple. In 2018, the EU imposed a record-breaking $5 billion fine on Google for abusing its dominant position in the Android operating system market. This was followed by a $1.7 billion fine in 2019 for violating EU antitrust rules in the online advertising market.
