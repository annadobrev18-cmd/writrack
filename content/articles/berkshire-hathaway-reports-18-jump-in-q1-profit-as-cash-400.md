The increase in Berkshire Hathaway's profit can be attributed to its diversified portfolio, which includes companies from various sectors such as insurance, retail, and manufacturing. The company's ability to navigate sector rotations and market fluctuations has been a key factor in its success.

Berkshire Hathaway's insurance sector has been a significant contributor to its profit. The company's insurance subsidiaries, including Geico and Gen Re, have reported strong earnings, driven by increased premiums and lower claims.

The retail sector has also been a bright spot for Berkshire Hathaway, with companies like Nebraska Furniture Mart and See's Candies reporting strong sales. The company's retail portfolio has been able to withstand the challenges posed by e-commerce and changing consumer behavior.
