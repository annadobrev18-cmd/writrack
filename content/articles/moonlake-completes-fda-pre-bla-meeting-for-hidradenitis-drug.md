### Understanding Hidradenitis

Hidradenitis suppurativa, commonly referred to as hidradenitis, is a chronic skin condition characterized by the formation of painful, inflamed nodules and abscesses in the apocrine-gland-bearing areas of the body. The condition affects approximately 1-4% of the global population, with women being more commonly affected than men. Despite its prevalence, hidradenitis remains a challenging condition to treat, with current therapies often providing limited relief.

## FDA Pre-BLA Meeting: Implications and Next Steps

The completion of the FDA pre-BLA meeting marks a crucial step in the regulatory process for MoonLake's hidradenitis drug. ### BLA Submission: What to Expect

The BLA submission is a comprehensive document that outlines the safety, efficacy, and manufacturing process for a biological product. MoonLake will need to provide detailed information on its clinical trials, including patient demographics, treatment outcomes, and adverse event data. The company will also need to demonstrate the quality and consistency of its manufacturing process, as well as provide a plan for post-marketing surveillance.

#### FDA Review Process

Once the BLA submission is received, the FDA will initiate a review process that typically takes 10-12 months to complete. During this time, the agency will assess the safety and efficacy of the treatment, as well as its potential benefits and risks. The FDA may also request additional information or clarification on certain aspects of the submission.
