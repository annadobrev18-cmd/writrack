### Merger Rationale

The merger is driven by the desire to create a more competitive and efficient company, with a stronger balance sheet and increased scale. The combined entity will have a more diversified portfolio, with a presence in over 100 markets across the United States. This diversification is expected to reduce the company's exposure to any one particular market, making it more resilient to economic downturns.

The merger is also expected to result in significant cost savings, with the companies anticipating $100 million in annual cost synergies. These cost savings will be achieved through the elimination of redundant positions, the consolidation of operations, and the optimization of procurement and other business processes.
