## The K-Shaped Economy and Its Implications
The K-shaped economy refers to the phenomenon where different segments of the economy recover at varying rates, resulting in a divergence between the haves and have-nots. This trend has been observed in the post-pandemic era, where the luxury sector has outperformed the overall market. As Citi Research notes, Toll Brothers, a luxury homebuilding stock, offers a way to play this increasing bifurcation of the economy.

### Historical Context of the K-Shaped Economy
The K-shaped economy is not a new concept, but its effects have been exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic. The pandemic has accelerated the shift towards online shopping, remote work, and digital entertainment, benefiting companies that cater to the affluent segment of the population. On the other hand, small businesses and low-income households have struggled to cope with the new reality. The K-shaped economy has significant implications for investors, as it creates opportunities for stocks that cater to the luxury segment.
