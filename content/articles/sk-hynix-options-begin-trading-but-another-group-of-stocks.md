## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What are single-stock ETFs, and how do they differ from traditional ETFs?
Single-stock ETFs are exchange-traded funds that track the performance of a single stock, offering investors a way to gain exposure to a specific company. They differ from traditional ETFs, which typically track a basket of stocks or an index.
2. How do leveraged funds work, and what are the associated risks?
Leveraged funds use debt to amplify potential returns, offering investors a way to magnify their gains. However, these funds also increase the risk of significant losses, as the use of leverage can work against investors if the market moves in the opposite direction.
3. What are the key factors driving the growth of single-stock ETFs and leveraged funds?
The growth of single-stock ETFs and leveraged funds can be attributed to their flexibility, ease of use, and the ability to amplify potential gains. Additionally, the increasing popularity of retail investing and the desire for more sophisticated investment products have contributed to their rapid growth.
