## Geopolitical Landscape and Financial Markets
The recent escalation of tensions between the US and Iran has significant implications for global financial markets. As the conflict approaches its two-week mark, investors are becoming increasingly wary of the potential consequences. The killing of Iran's leaders has been met with retaliation, and the situation continues to deteriorate.

### Historical Context
The US-Iran conflict is not a new development, with tensions dating back to the 1979 Iranian Revolution. However, the current situation is particularly volatile, with both sides engaging in aggressive rhetoric and military action. The assassination of top Iranian military leaders has been seen as a significant escalation, and the markets are reflecting this increased uncertainty.

## Risk Factors and Uncertainties
There are several risk factors and uncertainties associated with the conflict that investors should be aware of.

### Escalation of Conflict
The potential for further escalation of the conflict is a significant risk factor. If the situation continues to deteriorate, it could lead to a broader regional conflict, with significant implications for global financial markets.

### Cyberattacks and Terrorism
There is also a risk of cyberattacks and terrorism, which could have a significant impact on critical infrastructure and global supply chains.

### Economic Sanctions
The imposition of economic sanctions by the US and its allies could have a significant impact on the Iranian economy, leading to increased poverty and instability.
