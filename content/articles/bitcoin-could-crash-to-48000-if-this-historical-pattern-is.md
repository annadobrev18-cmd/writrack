## Historical Patterns in Bitcoin's Price Movement
Bitcoin's price has been known to follow certain historical patterns, which can be used to forecast its future movement. One such pattern is the 'super cycle' theory, which suggests that Bitcoin's price follows a cyclical pattern, with each cycle consisting of a bull run followed by a bear market.

### The Super Cycle Theory
The super cycle theory is based on the idea that Bitcoin's price is influenced by its halving events, which occur every four years. During each halving event, the reward for mining Bitcoin is cut in half, leading to a reduction in the supply of new Bitcoins being added to the market. This reduction in supply, combined with increasing demand, leads to an increase in price.

#### Halving Events and Price Movement
The first halving event occurred in 2012, when the reward for mining Bitcoin was cut from 50 BTC to 25 BTC. This event was followed by a significant increase in price, with Bitcoin's price rising from around $10 to over $1,000. The second halving event occurred in 2016, when the reward was cut from 25 BTC to 12.5 BTC. This event was also followed by a significant increase in price, with Bitcoin's price rising from around $650 to over $20,000.
