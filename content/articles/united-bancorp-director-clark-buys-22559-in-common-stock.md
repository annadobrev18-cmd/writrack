### Historical Context: Insider Buying and Stock Performance
Insider buying activity has long been considered a significant indicator of a company's potential for growth. Studies have shown that companies with high levels of insider buying tend to outperform those with low levels of insider buying. This is because insiders, such as directors and executives, have access to sensitive information about the company's financial health and future plans. When they choose to invest their own money in the company's stock, it suggests that they believe the company is undervalued or has significant growth potential.

### Market Impact: Insider Buying and Investor Sentiment
The impact of insider buying on investor sentiment should not be underestimated. When insiders buy stock, it can create a positive feedback loop, where investors become more confident in the company's prospects and are more likely to invest. This, in turn, can drive up the stock price and attract even more investors. Conversely, insider selling can have a negative impact on investor sentiment, leading to a decline in the stock price.
