### Understanding Form 144

Form 144 is a document filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) by insiders of a publicly traded company who intend to sell restricted or control securities. This form is used to notify the SEC of the proposed sale and to provide certain information about the transaction, including the number of shares to be sold, the price at which they will be sold, and the identity of the seller.

#### Historical Context of Form 144 Filings

To better understand the implications of Cushman & Wakefield's Form 144 filing, it's essential to examine the historical context of such filings. Over the past year, several companies have filed Form 144, resulting in varying market reactions. For instance, a Form 144 filing by a company's CEO may be viewed as a negative signal, potentially leading to a decline in the stock's price. On the other hand, a filing by a lesser-known insider may have a minimal impact on the market.
