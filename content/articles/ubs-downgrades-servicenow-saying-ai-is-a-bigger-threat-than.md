## Fundamentals of ServiceNow
ServiceNow is a leading provider of cloud-based platforms that automate and manage digital workflows, making businesses more efficient. The company's stock has been a darling of the market, with significant growth over the past few years. However, the recent downgrade by UBS, citing AI as a bigger threat than initially believed, has raised concerns among investors.

### Historical Performance
To understand the potential impact of AI on ServiceNow, it's essential to look at the company's historical performance. ServiceNow has consistently delivered high revenue growth, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of over 30% in the past five years. The company's net income has also increased significantly, with a CAGR of over 25% during the same period.

### Business Model
ServiceNow's business model is based on a subscription-based approach, where customers pay an annual fee to use the company's cloud-based platforms. This model provides a high level of visibility and predictability, making it easier for investors to forecast future revenue. However, the company's reliance on a few large customers and the potential for AI-powered automation to disrupt its business model are significant risks.

## Valuation
The valuation of ServiceNow is a critical aspect of the analysis. The company's stock price has been trading at a premium to its peers, with a price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio of over 70. This valuation is based on the company's high growth rate and the expectation that it will continue to deliver strong revenue and earnings growth in the future.
