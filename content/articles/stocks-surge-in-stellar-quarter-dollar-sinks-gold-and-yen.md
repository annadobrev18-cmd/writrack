## Stocks Surge in Stellar Quarter
The second quarter of 2026 has been a remarkable one for the stock market, with major indices surging to record-breaking highs. The S&P 500, Dow Jones, and Nasdaq have all experienced significant gains, driven by a combination of factors including strong economic growth, low unemployment, and a favorable business environment.

### Economic Growth
The US economy has been on a tear, with GDP growth exceeding expectations and consumer spending remaining robust. This has led to increased corporate profits, which in turn have driven stock prices higher. The current economic expansion, which began in 2009, is now one of the longest on record, and there are few signs of it slowing down anytime soon.

## Dollar Dominance
The US dollar has been a major beneficiary of the strong economy, with the currency surging to multi-year highs against a basket of major currencies. This has had a significant impact on gold and yen, which have both declined in value as a result of the dollar's strength.

### Impact on Gold
Gold, which is often seen as a safe-haven asset, has struggled in the face of a strong dollar. As the dollar rises, gold becomes more expensive for foreign buyers, which can lead to decreased demand and lower prices. This has been evident in recent months, with gold prices declining by over 10%.

As the stock market has surged, there have been significant sector rotations. Technology and healthcare stocks have been major beneficiaries, driven by strong earnings growth and innovation. On the other hand, energy and materials stocks have struggled, due to concerns over global demand and trade tensions.

## Global Ripple Effects
The strong US economy and surging stock market have had significant global ripple effects. Emerging markets, which are heavily dependent on exports to the US, have experienced significant gains. On the other hand, countries with large trade deficits with the US, such as China and Japan, have struggled.
