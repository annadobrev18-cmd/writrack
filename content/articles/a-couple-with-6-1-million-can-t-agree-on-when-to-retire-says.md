### Historical Context: The Evolution of Retirement Planning

Retirement planning has undergone significant changes over the years. In the past, retirement was often seen as a fixed point in time, typically around the age of 65. However, with advancements in medicine and increases in life expectancy, retirement has become a more fluid concept. Today, many people are choosing to retire earlier or later than traditional retirement age, and some are even opting for semi-retirement or entrepreneurship in their golden years.

#### The Rise of the Financial Independence Movement

The financial independence movement, popularized by bloggers and authors such as Mr. Money Mustache and Mad Fientist, has also contributed to the evolution of retirement planning. This movement emphasizes the importance of saving aggressively, investing wisely, and achieving financial independence as quickly as possible. Proponents of this movement argue that financial independence provides the freedom to pursue one's passions and interests, rather than being tied to a traditional 9-to-5 job.

### The Psychology of Money: Understanding the Emotional Aspects of Financial Decision-Making

The psychology of money is a complex and fascinating topic. Research has shown that financial decision-making is often influenced by emotional and psychological factors, such as fear, greed, and loss aversion. The couple's $6.1 million retirement dilemma is a prime example of how emotional and psychological factors can impact financial decision-making.

#### The Role of Cognitive Biases in Financial Decision-Making

Cognitive biases, such as confirmation bias and anchoring bias, can also play a significant role in financial decision-making. Confirmation bias, for example, can lead individuals to seek out information that confirms their existing beliefs, rather than seeking out diverse perspectives. Anchoring bias, on the other hand, can cause individuals to rely too heavily on the first piece of information they receive, rather than considering alternative options.
