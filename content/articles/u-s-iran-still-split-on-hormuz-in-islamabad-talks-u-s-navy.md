## Geopolitical Tensions Escalate: US-Iran Talks in Islamabad
The recent talks between the US and Iran in Islamabad have failed to yield a breakthrough, with both sides remaining split on the issue of the Strait of Hormuz. The US Navy has moved into the Strait, escalating tensions in the region. This development has significant implications for global markets, particularly for oil prices and the overall economy.

### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations
The US and Iran have a long and complex history of tensions, dating back to the 1979 Iranian Revolution. The two countries have been at odds over a range of issues, including Iran's nuclear program, its support for militant groups, and its human rights record. The current tensions are centered on the Strait of Hormuz, a vital waterway through which a significant portion of the world's oil passes.
