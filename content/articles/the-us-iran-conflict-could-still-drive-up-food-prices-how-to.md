### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations and Food Prices

To comprehend the potential impact of the US-Iran conflict on food prices, it's essential to examine the historical context of their relations. The US and Iran have had a complex and often tense relationship, with periods of heightened tensions and relative calm. One of the most significant events in recent history was the US withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in 2018, which led to the reimposition of sanctions on Iran.

The sanctions have had a devastating impact on Iran's economy, with the country's currency, the rial, plummeting in value. This has made it challenging for Iran to import goods, including food, leading to shortages and price increases. The effects of the sanctions have been felt globally, with food prices increasing in response to the reduced supply.

## Fundamentals: Understanding the Impact on Food Prices

The US-Iran conflict has the potential to drive up food prices due to several factors. One of the primary concerns is the impact on global oil prices. Iran is a significant oil producer, and any disruption to its oil exports could lead to a surge in global oil prices. Higher oil prices would increase the cost of production and transportation for food, leading to higher prices for consumers.

Another factor to consider is the potential impact on global food supplies. Iran is a significant producer of agricultural products, including wheat, barley, and rice. Any disruption to its agricultural production or exports could lead to shortages and price increases globally.
