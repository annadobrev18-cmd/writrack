### Historical Context: The Strait of Hormuz's Significance
The Strait of Hormuz, a narrow waterway connecting the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman, is one of the most critical chokepoints in the global oil trade. Approximately 20% of the world's oil passes through this strait, making it a vital artery for the global economy. Any disruption to the flow of oil through the Strait of Hormuz can have far-reaching consequences, including spikes in oil prices and potential economic downturns. The strait's strategic importance has made it a focal point of geopolitical tensions, with various nations vying for influence and control in the region.
