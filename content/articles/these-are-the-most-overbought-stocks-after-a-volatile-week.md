## Fundamentals of Overbought Stocks
The concept of overbought stocks refers to securities that have experienced a significant price increase in a short period, often driven by high demand and bullish sentiment. This can lead to a situation where the stock's price exceeds its intrinsic value, making it vulnerable to a correction. Hewlett Packard Enterprise, among others, has been identified as one of the most overbought stocks after a volatile week in the market.

### Valuation Metrics
Valuation metrics provide insight into a stock's intrinsic value and help investors determine if it's overbought or undervalued. The Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratio, Price-to-Book (P/B) ratio, and Dividend Yield are essential metrics to consider.

#### Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
The P/E ratio is calculated by dividing the stock's current price by its earnings per share (EPS). A high P/E ratio indicates that investors are willing to pay more for each dollar of earnings, which can be a sign of an overbought stock.

#### Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio
The P/B ratio is calculated by dividing the stock's current price by its book value per share. A high P/B ratio suggests that the stock is overvalued, as investors are paying more for the company's assets than they are worth.

#### Dividend Yield
The dividend yield is the ratio of the annual dividend payment to the stock's current price. A low dividend yield can indicate that the stock is overbought, as investors are prioritizing capital appreciation over income.

## Risk Factors
Investing in overbought stocks comes with significant risks. Some of the key risk factors to consider include:

### Market Volatility
Market volatility can lead to rapid price fluctuations, making it challenging to predict the stock's future performance. A volatile market can also increase the likelihood of a market correction, which can negatively impact overbought stocks.

### Economic Downturn
An economic downturn can lead to reduced consumer spending, decreased revenue, and lower earnings for companies. This can have a disproportionate impact on overbought stocks, as investors may become risk-averse and sell their holdings.

### Competitive Landscape
The competitive landscape of the industry can also impact overbought stocks. If a company is facing increased competition, it may struggle to maintain its market share, leading to decreased revenue and earnings.
