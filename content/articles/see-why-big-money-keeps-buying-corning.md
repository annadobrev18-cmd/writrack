## Fundamentals of Corning Incorporated
Corning Incorporated, listed on the New York Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol GLW, has been a stalwart in the technology and manufacturing sector for over 170 years. Founded in 1851, the company has evolved significantly, adapting to technological advancements and market demands. Today, Corning is a leading provider of specialty glass, ceramics, and related materials and technologies. Its product portfolio includes display technologies, optical communications, environmental technologies, and life sciences vessels.

### Historical Performance
Historically, Corning has demonstrated resilience and the ability to innovate, which has contributed to its enduring presence in the market. The company's commitment to research and development has been a key driver of its success, enabling it to stay ahead of the curve in terms of technology and innovation. Over the years, Corning has made strategic investments in emerging technologies, including 5G, augmented reality (AR), and the Internet of Things (IoT), positioning itself for long-term growth.

### Business Segments
Corning operates through several business segments, each focusing on different aspects of its diverse product portfolio:
- **Display Technologies**: This segment manufactures glass substrates for flat panel displays, including liquid crystal displays (LCDs) and organic light-emitting diode (OLED) displays.
- **Optical Communications**: This segment provides optical fiber, cable, and connectivity solutions for telecommunications, data communications, and submarine networks.
- **Environmental Technologies**: This segment produces ceramic substrates and filters for emissions control in vehicles and various industrial applications.
- **Life Sciences**: This segment offers laboratory products, such as glass and plastic vessels, that support the pharmaceutical, biotechnology, and environmental science industries.
