### Historical Context: The Ukraine-Russia Conflict

The conflict between Ukraine and Russia has been ongoing since 2014, with periods of relative calm punctuated by outbreaks of violence. The current escalation is a stark reminder of the volatility and unpredictability of geopolitical events. Historically, such conflicts have had far-reaching consequences for global trade, energy prices, and financial markets.

#### Key Events in the Ukraine-Russia Conflict

- 2014: Russia annexes Crimea, leading to international sanctions and a significant decline in Russian-Ukrainian relations.
- 2015: The Minsk II agreement is signed, aiming to cease hostilities in eastern Ukraine, but the conflict continues.
- 2022: Russia launches a large-scale invasion of Ukraine, leading to a major escalation of the conflict and significant international response.

## Market Impact: Assessing the Financial Fallout

The impact of the Russian attack on Kyiv on financial markets is multifaceted. The immediate reaction includes increased volatility, a flight to safe-haven assets, and a decline in riskier assets. The energy sector is particularly affected, given Russia's significant role as a global energy supplier.
