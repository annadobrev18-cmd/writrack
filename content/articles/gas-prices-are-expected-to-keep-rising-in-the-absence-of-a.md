## Fundamentals of the Gas Price Increase
The recent impasse in the US-Iran deal has significant implications for the global energy market, particularly in regards to gas prices. The absence of a deal is expected to lead to a continued rise in gas prices, affecting not only the United States but also the global economy. To understand the fundamentals of this increase, it is essential to examine the current state of the oil market and the role of Iran in the global energy landscape.

### Historical Context of US-Iran Relations
The relationship between the US and Iran has been complex, with periods of tension and diplomacy. The 2015 Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) aimed to limit Iran's nuclear program in exchange for relief from economic sanctions. However, the US withdrawal from the deal in 2018 and the subsequent reimposition of sanctions have led to increased tensions and a significant impact on the global oil market.

### Impact on Gas Prices
The increase in gas prices can be attributed to several factors, including the reduction in Iranian oil exports due to US sanctions, increased demand from countries like China and India, and geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) has also played a crucial role in shaping the global oil market, with its production cuts aimed at stabilizing prices.

## Risk Factors
The energy market is subject to various risk factors, including geopolitical tensions, changes in government policies, and fluctuations in global demand. The absence of a US-Iran deal exacerbates these risks, leading to increased volatility in the market.

### Geopolitical Risks
The Middle East is a critical region for the global oil market, with many countries relying on imports from the region. The ongoing conflict in Yemen, the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, and the Syrian civil war all contribute to the geopolitical risks facing the energy market.

### Regulatory Risks
Changes in government policies and regulations can significantly impact the energy market. The US withdrawal from the JCPOA and the reimposition of sanctions on Iran are examples of regulatory risks that can affect the market.
