## Geopolitical Landscape: Understanding the Iran Conflict
The recent escalation of tensions between the United States and Iran has sent shockwaves throughout the global economy. One month into the conflict, the Trump administration is faced with a multitude of hard choices, each with significant implications for international relations, global markets, and the US economy. To comprehend the complexity of this situation, it is essential to delve into the historical context of US-Iran relations and the events leading up to the current conflict.

### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations
The relationship between the United States and Iran has been strained for decades, with tensions escalating significantly following the 1979 Iranian Revolution. The US has imposed numerous economic sanctions on Iran, citing concerns over the country's nuclear program and human rights record. In 2018, the Trump administration withdrew from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), a nuclear deal between Iran, the US, and other world powers, and reimposed severe sanctions on the Iranian economy.

#### Recent Events: The Path to Conflict
The current conflict began to unfold in late 2019, with a series of incidents involving Iranian-backed militias and US military personnel in the Middle East. On January 3, 2020, a US drone strike killed top Iranian military commander Qasem Soleimani, leading to a significant escalation of tensions between the two nations. Iran responded with a ballistic missile attack on US military bases in Iraq, and the US has since launched airstrikes against Iranian-backed militias in the region.

## Market Impact: Assessing the Economic Consequences
The ongoing conflict between the US and Iran has already had a profound impact on global markets. The price of oil has surged, with Brent crude reaching a peak of over $70 per barrel in early January. This increase in oil prices has led to higher energy costs for consumers and businesses, potentially dampening economic growth.

2. How will the conflict impact the US economy?
