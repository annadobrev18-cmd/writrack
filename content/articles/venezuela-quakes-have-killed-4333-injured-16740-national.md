## Venezuela Earthquakes: A Nation in Crisis
The recent earthquakes in Venezuela have resulted in a devastating loss of life, with 4,333 fatalities and 16,740 injuries reported by the National Assembly president. This tragedy comes at a time when the nation is already grappling with severe economic challenges, including hyperinflation, food shortages, and a significant decline in oil production.

### Historical Context
Venezuela's economic woes began in the early 2000s, when the country's oil industry, which accounts for the majority of its exports, started to decline. The government's failure to diversify the economy and invest in other sectors has led to a severe dependence on oil revenues. The situation has been exacerbated by the imposition of economic sanctions by the United States and other countries, which has limited Venezuela's access to international markets and financing.
