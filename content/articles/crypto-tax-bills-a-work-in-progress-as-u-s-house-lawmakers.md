### Historical Context: The Evolution of Crypto Taxation
The taxation of cryptocurrencies has been a topic of discussion for several years, with the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) issuing guidelines on the treatment of virtual currencies in 2014. However, the rapid growth of the crypto market has led to increased scrutiny and calls for more comprehensive regulation. In 2020, the IRS issued additional guidance on the taxation of cryptocurrencies, including the treatment of hard forks and airdrops.

### Peer Comparison: Crypto Taxation Around the World
A comparison of crypto taxation policies around the world reveals a mixed approach, with some countries embracing a more lenient stance while others impose stricter regulations.

| Country | Crypto Tax Policy |
| --- | --- |
| United States | Taxed as property, with capital gains tax applicable |
| Canada | Taxed as commodity, with capital gains tax applicable |
| Singapore | Taxed as goods, with goods and services tax (GST) applicable |
| Japan | Taxed as miscellaneous income, with income tax applicable |
