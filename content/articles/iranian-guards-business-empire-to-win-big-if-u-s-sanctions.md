## Iranian Economy and the Impact of Sanctions
The Iranian economy has been heavily influenced by the sanctions imposed by the United States. These sanctions have limited Iran's ability to engage in international trade, affecting various sectors such as oil, banking, and manufacturing. However, with the possibility of these sanctions being lifted, the Iranian Guards' business empire is poised to benefit significantly.

### Historical Context of Sanctions
The US sanctions on Iran date back to 1979, following the Iranian Revolution. Over the years, these sanctions have been tightened, particularly during the Trump administration. The sanctions have had a profound impact on the Iranian economy, with the country's GDP contracting by over 6% in 2019. However, the Iranian government has been working to diversify its economy and reduce its dependence on oil exports.

#### Key Sectors Affected by Sanctions
The sanctions have affected various sectors of the Iranian economy, including:
- Oil and gas: Iran's oil exports have been severely impacted, with the country's oil production decreasing significantly.
- Banking: Iranian banks have been cut off from the international financial system, making it difficult for the country to engage in international trade.
- Manufacturing: The sanctions have limited Iran's ability to import raw materials and machinery, affecting the country's manufacturing sector.
