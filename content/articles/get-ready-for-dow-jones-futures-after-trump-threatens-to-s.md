### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations

The relationship between the United States and Iran has been strained for decades, with the two countries engaging in a series of diplomatic and military confrontations. The current tensions can be traced back to the US withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), also known as the Iran nuclear deal, in 2018. Since then, the US has imposed strict economic sanctions on Iran, leading to a significant decline in the country's economy.

The situation took a turn for the worse in January 2020, when the US launched a drone strike that killed top Iranian military commander Qasem Soleimani. Iran responded by launching a ballistic missile attack on US military bases in Iraq, leading to a significant escalation of tensions in the region.
