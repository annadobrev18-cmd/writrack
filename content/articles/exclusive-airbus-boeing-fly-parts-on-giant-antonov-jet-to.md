The COVID-19 pandemic has exposed the vulnerabilities of global supply chains, and the aerospace industry has been no exception. Airbus and Boeing, two of the world's largest aircraft manufacturers, have faced significant challenges in maintaining a steady supply of parts and components. To mitigate these disruptions, both companies have resorted to innovative solutions, including the use of the giant Antonov An-225 Mriya cargo aircraft.

### The Antonov An-225 Mriya: A Game-Changer in Logistics
The Antonov An-225 Mriya is the world's largest cargo aircraft, with a maximum payload capacity of 250 tons. Its massive size and lifting capability make it an ideal solution for transporting oversized cargo, including aircraft parts and components. By utilizing the Antonov An-225, Airbus and Boeing can transport large volumes of cargo quickly and efficiently, reducing the risk of supply chain disruptions.
