### Historical Context: Russia-Ukraine Conflict

The Russia-Ukraine conflict has its roots in the early 2010s, when Ukraine's pro-Russian government was overthrown, leading to Russia's annexation of Crimea in 2014. Since then, the conflict has escalated, with both sides suffering significant losses. The conflict has had far-reaching consequences, including economic sanctions imposed on Russia by the international community.

### Economic Sanctions: Impact on Russia

The economic sanctions imposed on Russia have had a significant impact on the country's economy. The sanctions, which include restrictions on trade, finance, and energy, have limited Russia's access to international markets and have led to a decline in foreign investment. According to data from the World Bank, Russia's GDP growth rate has slowed significantly since the imposition of sanctions, from 1.7% in 2013 to -3.7% in 2015.
