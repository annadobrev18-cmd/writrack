## Alumis' Breakthrough in Psoriasis Treatment
Alumis has reported the Phase 3 trial results for envudeucitinib, a novel treatment for psoriasis. The results indicate a significant improvement in symptoms, with a considerable percentage of patients achieving complete skin clearance. This breakthrough has the potential to revolutionize the treatment of psoriasis, a chronic skin condition affecting millions worldwide.

### Psoriasis: A Growing Concern
Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune skin condition characterized by thick, scaly, and inflamed skin patches. According to the National Psoriasis Foundation, over 8 million people in the United States suffer from psoriasis, with approximately 125 million people affected worldwide. The condition can significantly impact a patient's quality of life, causing discomfort, pain, and emotional distress.

### Current Treatment Options
Current treatments for psoriasis include topical corticosteroids, vitamin D analogs, and systemic therapies such as biologics and oral medications. While these treatments can provide relief, they often have limitations, including side effects, limited efficacy, and high costs. The need for more effective and safer treatments has driven the development of new therapies, including envudeucitinib.

### Envudeucitinib: A Novel Approach
Envudeucitinib is a selective tyrosine kinase 2 (TYK2) inhibitor, which targets the underlying inflammatory pathways involved in psoriasis. By inhibiting TYK2, envudeucitinib reduces the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, leading to a decrease in inflammation and skin symptoms. The drug has shown promising results in earlier clinical trials, demonstrating significant improvements in skin clearance and symptom reduction.

### Phase 3 Trial Results
The Phase 3 trial results reported by Alumis demonstrate the efficacy and safety of envudeucitinib in patients with moderate to severe psoriasis. The trial included over 1,000 patients, who were randomized to receive either envudeucitinib or a placebo. The primary endpoint was the proportion of patients achieving a 90% reduction in the Psoriasis Area and Severity Index (PASI 90) at week 16.

| **Treatment** | **PASI 90 at Week 16** | **PASI 100 at Week 16** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Envudeucitinib | 55.1% | 23.4% |
| Placebo | 5.5% | 1.1% |

The results show that envudeucitinib significantly outperformed the placebo, with over 55% of patients achieving PASI 90 and nearly 25% achieving complete skin clearance (PASI 100). The treatment was also well-tolerated, with a safety profile consistent with previous trials.
