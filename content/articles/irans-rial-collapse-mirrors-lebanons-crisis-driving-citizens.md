## Lebanon's Economic Crisis: A Mirror to Iran's Situation
Lebanon's economic crisis provides a stark reminder of the dangers of unchecked government spending, corruption, and external shocks. The country's economy has been in a state of crisis since 2019, with the Lebanese pound losing over 90% of its value against the US dollar. The crisis has been exacerbated by a decline in remittances, a decrease in foreign investment, and a massive debt burden.

### Causes of Lebanon's Economic Crisis
Lebanon's economic crisis is attributed to a combination of internal and external factors. The country's government has been criticized for its mismanagement of the economy, including excessive spending and corruption. The decline in remittances from Lebanese citizens living abroad has also had a significant impact on the country's economy. Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has disrupted global trade, further exacerbating Lebanon's economic woes.

#### Impact on Citizens
The economic crisis in Lebanon has had a devastating impact on its citizens. The decline in the value of the Lebanese pound has resulted in a significant increase in the cost of living, making it challenging for citizens to afford basic necessities. The crisis has also led to a decline in employment opportunities, with many businesses forced to close due to the economic uncertainty.
