## US Trade Policy Shift: Background and Context
The US government has proposed imposing tariffs of 10% or 12.5% on goods from 60 economies due to concerns over forced labor practices. This move is seen as a significant shift in US trade policy, as it aims to hold countries accountable for human rights violations in their supply chains. The proposed tariffs will have far-reaching implications for global trade, economic growth, and human rights.

### Historical Context: US Trade Policy and Human Rights
The US has a long history of using trade policy as a tool to promote human rights and democratic values. The Tariff Act of 1930, for example, prohibited the importation of goods made with forced labor. However, the enforcement of this provision has been inconsistent over the years. The proposed tariffs mark a renewed commitment by the US to address forced labor concerns and promote fair labor practices globally.
