Historically, political tensions and conflicts have significantly impacted economic landscapes. The Cold War, for instance, led to the division of Europe, affecting trade and economic growth. Similarly, the ongoing tensions between the US and China have resulted in trade wars, influencing global supply chains and market volatility.

## Sector Rotations and Investment Strategies
In times of geopolitical uncertainty, investors often seek safe-haven assets such as gold, bonds, and the US dollar. This shift can lead to sector rotations, where investments move from risky assets like stocks to more stable ones. Understanding these rotations is crucial for developing effective investment strategies.
