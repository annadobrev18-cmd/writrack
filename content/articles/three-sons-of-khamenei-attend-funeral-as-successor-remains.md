### Historical Context

Iran's political system is based on the concept of the Supreme Leader, who holds absolute authority over the government and the military. The current Supreme Leader, Ali Khamenei, has been in power since 1989 and is now 83 years old. The question of who will succeed him has been a topic of speculation for several years.

### Potential Successors

There are several potential successors to Khamenei, including his son, Mojtaba Khamenei, who is rumored to be the favored candidate. However, Mojtaba's lack of public profile and experience in government has raised concerns about his ability to lead the country.

Other potential successors include:

* Ebrahim Raisi, the current President of Iran, who has a strong background in the judiciary and is known for his conservative views.
* Sadeq Larijani, the former head of the judiciary, who is seen as a moderate and has experience in government.
