## Nvidia's Strategic Expansion
Nvidia is set to unveil its first Windows PCs powered by its own chips next week, marking a significant milestone in the company's history. This move is expected to have far-reaching implications for the tech industry, with potential repercussions for competitors and investors alike.

### Historical Context
Nvidia has long been a dominant player in the graphics processing unit (GPU) market, with its chips powering many of the world's top gaming PCs and laptops. However, the company has been expanding its reach in recent years, exploring new markets such as artificial intelligence, autonomous vehicles, and datacenter technology.

In 2020, Nvidia acquired ARM Ltd, a leading chip design company, in a deal worth $40 billion. This acquisition has given Nvidia a significant boost in its efforts to develop its own central processing units (CPUs) and compete with established players like Intel and AMD.
