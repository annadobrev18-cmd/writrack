### Historical Context of Options Expiry

To grasp the potential impact of this $10 billion options expiry, it's essential to look at historical data. Previous large options expiries have shown that the price of Bitcoin can be volatile in the days leading up to and following the event. This volatility is often due to option holders adjusting their positions to minimize losses or maximize gains, depending on their outlook on the market. The max pain theory has been a topic of discussion among traders, as it provides a potential target for the price movement based on open interest.

#### Analyzing the Max Pain Theory

The max pain theory is based on the idea that option sellers (those who sell call and put options) aim to minimize their risk by pushing the price of the underlying asset towards a level where the most option buyers experience the maximum pain. This level is usually the strike price with the highest open interest. In the case of Bitcoin, if the max pain theory holds, the price could move towards this level as the options expiry approaches. However, with Bitcoin currently trading below the $72,000 magnet, it's crucial to assess whether this theory will hold true this time around.
