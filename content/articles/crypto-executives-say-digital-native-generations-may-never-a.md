## The Rise of Digital Native Generations
The world is witnessing a significant shift in the way people interact with financial systems. Crypto executives believe that digital native generations, who have grown up with the internet and smartphones, may never need a bank account. This statement has far-reaching implications for the traditional banking sector and the future of finance.

### The Changing Landscape of Finance
The rise of cryptocurrency and blockchain technology has led to the development of decentralized financial systems (DeFi). DeFi platforms provide an alternative to traditional banking, allowing users to lend, borrow, and trade assets without the need for intermediaries. This shift towards decentralized finance has the potential to disrupt the traditional banking sector, which has long been dominated by centralized institutions.

## The Impact on Traditional Banking
The rise of DeFi and the potential for digital native generations to never need a bank account has significant implications for the traditional banking sector. Banks will need to adapt to the changing landscape of finance, or risk becoming obsolete.

### The Need for Innovation
Traditional banks will need to innovate and develop new products and services that cater to the needs of digital native generations. This could include the development of digital banking platforms, mobile payment systems, and cryptocurrency-based services.

#### Case Study: Goldman Sachs
Goldman Sachs, one of the largest investment banks in the world, has already begun to adapt to the changing landscape of finance. The bank has developed a digital banking platform, Marcus, which offers high-yield savings accounts and personal loans. Goldman Sachs has also invested in several cryptocurrency and blockchain startups, including Circle and Axoni.
