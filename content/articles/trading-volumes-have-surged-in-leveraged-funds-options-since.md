## Leveraged Funds and Options Trading: A New Era
The COVID-19 pandemic has brought about a significant shift in the way investors approach the markets. With the rise of remote work and increased accessibility to trading platforms, individual investors have become more active participants in the financial markets. A recent report highlights the surge in trading volumes of leveraged funds and options since the pandemic, with daily volumes growing at compound annual rates of 29% and 16%, respectively, between 2020 and 2025.

### Historical Context
To understand the magnitude of this growth, it's essential to look at the historical context. Prior to the pandemic, leveraged funds and options trading were largely dominated by institutional investors. However, with the advent of zero-commission trading and the rise of fintech platforms, individual investors have become increasingly active in these markets. The pandemic has accelerated this trend, with many investors seeking to capitalize on the market volatility.
