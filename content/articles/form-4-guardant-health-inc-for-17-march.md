### Background on Form 4 Filings

Form 4 is a document that insiders, including executives, directors, and significant shareholders, are required to file with the SEC to disclose their transactions involving the company's securities. The filing provides information on the date of the transaction, the number of shares bought or sold, and the price at which the transaction was made. This information can be useful for investors to gauge the sentiment of insiders towards the company's stock.
