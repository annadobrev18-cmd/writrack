Despite this temporary reprieve, the ECB remains cautious due to the ongoing geopolitical tensions and supply chain disruptions. These factors could lead to a resurgence in energy prices, further exacerbating inflationary pressures.

### Inflation Risks: A Persistent Concern

The ECB's primary objective is to maintain price stability, with an inflation target of close to 2%. However, the current inflation rate stands at 3.5%, exceeding the target. The following table illustrates the evolution of inflation rates in the Eurozone:

| Year | Inflation Rate |
| --- | --- |
| 2020 | 0.3% |
| 2021 | 3.4% |
| 2022 | 3.9% |
| 2023 (YTD) | 3.5% |

Schnabel's statement highlights the ECB's concerns over the persistence of inflation risks, driven by factors such as:

* **Wage growth**: Rising labor costs, particularly in the service sector, are contributing to inflationary pressures.
* **Supply chain disruptions**: Ongoing global supply chain issues, exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic and geopolitical tensions, continue to drive up prices.
* **Fiscal policy**: Expansionary fiscal policies in some Eurozone countries may further fuel inflation.
