The US Senate has recently voted to ban Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) in a housing bill, a move that may face opposition in the House. This decision has significant implications for the crypto market and the future of digital currencies in the United States.

### Historical Context of CBDCs
CBDCs have been a topic of discussion among central banks and governments worldwide. The concept of a digital currency issued by a central bank has been explored by several countries, including China, Sweden, and Singapore. The idea behind CBDCs is to provide a digital alternative to traditional fiat currencies, potentially increasing efficiency and reducing transaction costs.
