### The Fear Gauge: Understanding the VIX
The Chicago Board Options Exchange (CBOE) Volatility Index, commonly referred to as the VIX, is a widely recognized measure of market volatility. It is often called the 'fear gauge' because it reflects the level of uncertainty and anxiety among investors. A high VIX reading indicates increased fear, while a low reading suggests a more stable market environment. The recent tumble in the VIX below its long-term average is a clear indication of the market's growing confidence.
