## CME's Decision to Sue CFTC: Understanding the Context
The recent announcement by the CME chief executive to sue the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) after the approval of perpetual futures has sent shockwaves throughout the financial industry. This move by CME, one of the world's leading derivatives exchanges, raises significant questions about the regulatory landscape and the future of financial markets.

### Background on Perpetual Futures
Perpetual futures, also known as perpetual swaps, are a type of financial derivative that allows investors to bet on the future value of an asset without an expiration date. Unlike traditional futures contracts, which have a specific expiration date, perpetual futures can be held indefinitely, making them a popular choice among traders and investors.
