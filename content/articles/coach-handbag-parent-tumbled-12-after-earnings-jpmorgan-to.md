## Current Event: Tapestry's Earnings Plunge
Tapestry, the parent company of Coach, Kate Spade, and Stuart Weitzman, recently reported its quarterly earnings, which led to a 12% decline in its stock price. The company's revenue and earnings per share (EPS) missed analyst expectations, causing investors to sell off their shares. However, JPMorgan is advising its clients to buy the dip, citing the revival of the Coach brand as a key factor that will drive the stock's recovery.

### Historical Context: Tapestry's Performance
To understand the significance of Tapestry's earnings plunge, it's essential to look at the company's historical performance. Over the past year, Tapestry's stock has been volatile, with periods of significant growth followed by sharp declines. The company has been working to revamp its brands, particularly Coach, which has been struggling to compete with other luxury fashion brands.

## Market Impact: Luxury Fashion Industry
The luxury fashion industry is highly competitive, with many established brands vying for market share. The COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the shift to online shopping, which has forced luxury fashion brands to adapt their business models. Tapestry's brands, particularly Coach, have been working to expand their e-commerce presence and improve their digital marketing efforts.

### Technical Analysis: Tapestry's Stock Price
From a technical analysis perspective, Tapestry's stock price has been trading in a range-bound pattern over the past year. The stock's 50-day moving average is $34.12, while its 200-day moving average is $36.45. The relative strength index (RSI) is currently at 32.14, which indicates that the stock is oversold.

#### Support and Resistance Levels
The stock's support level is at $28.50, which is the low point of the range-bound pattern. The resistance level is at $40.50, which is the high point of the range-bound pattern. If the stock breaks above the resistance level, it could indicate a trend reversal and a potential rally.

## Expert Opinions: JPMorgan's Advice
JPMorgan is advising its clients to buy Tapestry's stock, citing the revival of the Coach brand as a key factor that will drive the stock's recovery. The bank's analysts believe that the company's efforts to revamp its brands and expand its e-commerce presence will pay off in the long term.
