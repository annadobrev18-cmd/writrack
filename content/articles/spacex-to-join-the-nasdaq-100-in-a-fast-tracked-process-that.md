The recent announcement that SpaceX is set to join the Nasdaq-100 in a fast-tracked process has sent shockwaves through the financial world. This move is expected to drive huge ETF buying demand, making SpaceX one of the first beneficiaries of Nasdaq's recently adopted fast-track inclusion framework. ### Historical Context: Nasdaq's Fast-Track Inclusion Framework
Nasdaq's decision to adopt a fast-track inclusion framework is a significant departure from its traditional methodology. Historically, companies were required to meet specific criteria, including a minimum market capitalization and trading volume, before being considered for inclusion in the Nasdaq-100. However, with the rapid growth of technology companies, Nasdaq recognized the need to adapt its framework to accommodate innovative and disruptive players like SpaceX.

#### Nasdaq-100 Index Composition
The Nasdaq-100 is a modified market capitalization-weighted index, comprising the largest and most actively traded non-financial stocks listed on the Nasdaq stock exchange. The index is rebalanced quarterly to ensure that it remains representative of the underlying market. With SpaceX's inclusion, the index will undergo a significant transformation, reflecting the growing importance of technology and innovation in the modern economy.

## Market Impact: ETF Buying Demand and Trading Volumes
The inclusion of SpaceX in the Nasdaq-100 is expected to drive huge ETF buying demand, as investors seek to capitalize on the company's growth potential. ETFs tracking the Nasdaq-100, such as the Invesco QQQ Trust (QQQ), will be forced to purchase SpaceX shares to maintain their tracking error. This increased demand will likely lead to higher trading volumes and potentially drive up the stock price.
