## Unmanned Aerial Systems: A Growing Market
The global drone market is expected to experience significant growth over the next decade, with Oppenheimer analysts predicting it will be worth $400 billion by 2036. This growth is driven by the increasing adoption of unmanned aerial systems (UAS) in various industries, including defense, commercial, and consumer markets.

### Historical Context
The use of drones in the defense sector is not new, with the first unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) being used in the 1990s for reconnaissance and surveillance missions. However, in recent years, the development and deployment of UAS have accelerated, with many countries investing heavily in drone technology. The global defense market has been growing steadily, but the 'lower-skies' category, which includes UAS, is growing twice as fast as the entire market.

### Market Impact
The growth of the drone market is expected to have a significant impact on various industries, including defense, construction, agriculture, and infrastructure inspection. The use of UAS in these industries can increase efficiency, reduce costs, and improve safety. For example, in the construction industry, drones can be used to monitor site progress, inspect buildings, and detect potential safety hazards.

#### Defense Sector
The defense sector is expected to be a major driver of the drone market, with many countries increasing their investment in UAS technology. The use of drones in the defense sector can provide significant advantages, including increased surveillance capabilities, improved situational awareness, and enhanced combat effectiveness.

#### Commercial Sector
The commercial sector is also expected to be a significant contributor to the growth of the drone market. The use of UAS in industries such as construction, agriculture, and infrastructure inspection can increase efficiency, reduce costs, and improve safety.
