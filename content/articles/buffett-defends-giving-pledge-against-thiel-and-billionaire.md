## Buffett Defends 'Giving Pledge'
Warren Buffett, the renowned investor and philanthropist, has come to the defense of the 'Giving Pledge', a philanthropic initiative he co-founded with Bill Gates almost 15 years ago. The initiative, which aims to encourage billionaires to donate the majority of their wealth to charitable causes during their lifetime or in their will, has faced criticism from some quarters, including billionaire Peter Thiel.

### Background of the Giving Pledge
The Giving Pledge was launched in 2010 by Warren Buffett and Bill Gates, with the goal of encouraging wealthy individuals to donate at least half of their net worth to philanthropic causes. The initiative has gained significant traction over the years, with over 200 billionaires from around the world signing up to the pledge. The Giving Pledge has been credited with helping to raise billions of dollars for various charitable causes, including education, healthcare, and poverty alleviation.

### Buffett's Defense of the Giving Pledge
In response to the criticism, Warren Buffett has defended the Giving Pledge, arguing that it has helped to raise awareness about the importance of philanthropy and has encouraged many wealthy individuals to donate to charitable causes. Buffett has also pointed out that the Giving Pledge is not a substitute for government action, but rather a complement to it. He has argued that philanthropy can help to fill gaps in social services and support innovative solutions to social problems.
