### Historical Context

To put the current risk landscape into perspective, it is essential to examine historical data. The global economy has faced numerous challenges in the past, from the 2008 financial crisis to the COVID-19 pandemic. Each of these events has contributed to the evolving risk mosaic, with new factors emerging and old ones dissipating.

| Risk Factor | 2008 | 2020 | 2026 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Geopolitical Tensions | High | Medium | High |
| Economic Uncertainty | High | High | Medium |
| Pandemic Risks | Low | High | Low |
| Climate Change | Low | Medium | High |

As the table illustrates, the risk landscape has undergone significant changes over the years. Geopolitical tensions and economic uncertainty have remained persistent risks, while pandemic risks have decreased. Climate change has emerged as a significant risk factor, with far-reaching implications for the global economy.

### Sector Rotations

The changing risk landscape has led to sector rotations, with investors shifting their focus to areas that are less vulnerable to risk. The technology sector, for example, has been a beneficiary of this trend, as investors seek safe-haven assets.

### Global Ripple Effects

The changing risk landscape has also had significant global ripple effects, with emerging markets being particularly vulnerable to risk. The COVID-19 pandemic, for example, has had a devastating impact on emerging markets, with many countries struggling to recover.

#### Emerging Markets Analysis
