## Exxon's Strategic Move
Exxon's decision to supply liquefied natural gas (LNG) to South Africa's first import terminal marks a significant strategic move in the energy market. This development is expected to have far-reaching implications for the global energy landscape, particularly in the context of the ongoing transition to cleaner energy sources.

### Background
South Africa, a country heavily reliant on coal for its energy needs, has been seeking to diversify its energy mix in recent years. The construction of the country's first LNG import terminal is a key component of this strategy, aiming to reduce dependence on coal and mitigate the environmental impact of energy production. Exxon, as one of the world's largest energy companies, is well-positioned to capitalize on this opportunity.
