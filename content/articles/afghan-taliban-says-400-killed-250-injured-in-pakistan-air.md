## Geopolitical Tensions Escalate
The recent air strike by Pakistan on a hospital in Kabul, resulting in significant loss of life, has escalated geopolitical tensions in the region. According to reports from the Afghan Taliban, the strike killed 400 people and injured 250. This incident has far-reaching implications, not only for the countries involved but also for the global economy.

### Historical Context
The complex and often volatile relationship between Pakistan and Afghanistan has been a source of concern for decades. The region has experienced numerous conflicts, including the Soviet-Afghan War and the US-led invasion of Afghanistan in 2001. The Taliban's return to power in Afghanistan in 2021 further complicated the situation, with Pakistan's government walking a tight rope in its relations with the Taliban and other international actors.
