## Kraken's Strategic Move
Kraken, one of the world's largest and most reputable cryptocurrency exchanges, has set its sights on becoming a bank in Europe. This move is a significant departure from its current business model and marks a new chapter in the company's history. By becoming a bank, Kraken aims to expand its offerings and provide a more comprehensive suite of financial services to its customers.

The cryptocurrency space has undergone tremendous growth and transformation over the past decade. However, despite this growth, cryptocurrency exchanges have often found themselves at odds with traditional financial institutions and regulatory bodies. Kraken's bid to become a bank is a strategic attempt to bridge this gap and gain greater legitimacy in the eyes of regulators and investors.

### Market Impact
Kraken's move into banking is likely to have far-reaching implications for the cryptocurrency market. By obtaining a banking license, Kraken will be able to offer a range of traditional banking services, including deposits, loans, and credit products. This could attract a new wave of customers who are looking for a more secure and regulated way to interact with cryptocurrencies. Additionally, Kraken's banking ambitions could also lead to increased institutional investment in the cryptocurrency space, as traditional financial institutions become more comfortable with the idea of investing in cryptocurrencies.

#### Competitive Landscape
Kraken is not the first cryptocurrency exchange to explore banking. Companies like Coinbase and Gemini have already made significant strides in this area, with Coinbase launching its own debit card and Gemini offering a range of banking services to its customers. However, Kraken's move into banking is notable due to its size and scale. As one of the largest cryptocurrency exchanges in the world, Kraken's banking ambitions could have a significant impact on the market.

## Technical Analysis
