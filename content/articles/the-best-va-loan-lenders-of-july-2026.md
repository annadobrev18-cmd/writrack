## VA Loans: An Overview
VA loans are a type of mortgage loan guaranteed by the United States Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). These loans are designed to help active-duty military personnel, veterans, and surviving spouses purchase, refinance, or improve a home. VA loans offer several benefits, including lower interest rates, lower or no down payment requirements, and more lenient credit score requirements.

### History of VA Loans
The VA loan program was established in 1944 as part of the Servicemen's Readjustment Act, also known as the GI Bill. The program was designed to help veterans returning from World War II purchase homes and readjust to civilian life. Since then, the program has undergone several changes and expansions, including the addition of new benefits and eligibility requirements.

## Valuation and Risk Factors
When evaluating VA loan lenders, it's essential to consider valuation and risk factors. These include:

### Interest Rates
Interest rates can significantly impact the overall cost of a VA loan. Lenders with lower interest rates can save borrowers thousands of dollars over the life of the loan.

### Fees
Fees can also add up quickly, and lenders with lower or no fees can offer significant savings.

### Credit Score Requirements
Credit score requirements can impact eligibility for a VA loan. Lenders with lower credit score requirements can offer more flexibility for borrowers with less-than-perfect credit.
