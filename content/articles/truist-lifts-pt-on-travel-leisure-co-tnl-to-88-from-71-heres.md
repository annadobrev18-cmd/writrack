## Sector Outlook
The travel and leisure industry is experiencing a significant rebound, driven by pent-up demand and improved consumer confidence. As the global economy continues to recover, travel restrictions are being lifted, and people are eager to explore new destinations. This trend is expected to continue, with the global travel market projected to grow at a CAGR of 5.5% from 2023 to 2028.

### Competitive Landscape
TNL operates in a highly competitive industry, with several major players vying for market share. However, the company's strong brand portfolio, which includes iconic brands such as Wyndham and RCI, provides a competitive advantage. TNL's focus on digital transformation and customer experience has also enabled it to stay ahead of the curve and attract a loyal customer base.

## Fed Implications
The Federal Reserve's monetary policy decisions have a significant impact on the travel and leisure industry. Interest rate changes can influence consumer spending and borrowing costs, which in turn affect travel demand. The current low-interest-rate environment is supportive of the industry, and TNL is well-positioned to benefit from this trend.

### Global Ripple Effects
The travel and leisure industry is highly interconnected, with global events and trends having a significant impact on demand.
