### Historical Context: Nvidia's Rise to Prominence

Nvidia's journey to becoming a leader in the AI chip market has been nothing short of remarkable. The company's graphics processing units (GPUs) were initially designed for gaming, but their high-performance capabilities made them an ideal choice for AI and deep learning applications. As the demand for AI computing grew, Nvidia's GPUs became the go-to choice for data centers and cloud computing providers.

#### Key Milestones in Nvidia's Growth

* 2016: Nvidia launches its Pascal-based GPUs, which become a huge success in the AI and deep learning space
* 2017: The company introduces its Volta-based GPUs, further solidifying its position in the market
* 2018: Nvidia acquires Arm, a leading provider of processor intellectual property, in a bid to expand its reach in the AI and IoT markets
* 2020: The company launches its Ampere-based GPUs, which offer significant performance improvements for AI and deep learning workloads

## Market Impact: The Shift to Chip-Centric Infrastructure

The shift to chip-centric infrastructure is likely to have a significant impact on the tech industry, with companies like Nvidia poised to benefit from this trend. As physical infrastructure takes a backseat, the focus will be on developing high-performance chips that can handle the complex workloads associated with AI and deep learning.
