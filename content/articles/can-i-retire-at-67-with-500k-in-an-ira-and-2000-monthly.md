### Retirement Expenses
Next, let's consider the individual's retirement expenses. A common rule of thumb is that retirees will need around 70-80% of their pre-retirement income to maintain a similar standard of living. However, this can vary significantly depending on individual circumstances, such as housing costs, healthcare expenses, and lifestyle choices.

#### Expense Categories
Here are some typical expense categories for retirees:
* Housing: 30-40% of total expenses
* Healthcare: 10-20% of total expenses
* Food: 10-15% of total expenses
* Transportation: 5-10% of total expenses
* Entertainment: 5-10% of total expenses

### Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the optimal asset allocation for a retirement portfolio?**
The optimal asset allocation will depend on individual circumstances, such as risk tolerance, time horizon, and income needs. A diversified portfolio with a mix of stocks, bonds, and alternatives can provide a balanced return and reduce risk.
2. **How can I minimize taxes in retirement?**
Minimizing taxes in retirement requires careful planning, including tax-loss harvesting, charitable donations, and optimizing income sources.
3. **What is the impact of sequence of returns risk on retirement income?**
Sequence of returns risk refers to the potential for negative returns early in retirement to deplete the IRA more quickly. This can be mitigated by diversifying the portfolio, using guaranteed income sources, and adjusting the withdrawal rate as needed.
