In 2011, the S&P 500 index had suffered a significant decline, plummeting by over 16% in the summer of that year. However, despite the prevailing gloom, a contrarian view emerged, suggesting that the market was poised for a substantial rebound. This call was based on a combination of factors, including the Fed's accommodative monetary policy, the resilience of corporate earnings, and the attractiveness of valuations.
