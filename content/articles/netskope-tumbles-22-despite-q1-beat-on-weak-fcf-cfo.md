## Netskope's Q1 Earnings: A Mixed Bag
Netskope, a leading security company, recently announced its Q1 earnings, which beat analyst expectations. However, despite the positive top-line performance, the company's stock price plummeted 22% due to concerns over weak free cash flow (FCF) and the transition of its Chief Financial Officer (CFO).

### Q1 Earnings Highlights
Netskope reported Q1 revenue of $123.4 million, exceeding analyst estimates of $118.5 million. The company's revenue growth was driven by a 35% increase in product revenue, which accounted for 80% of total revenue. Additionally, Netskope's non-GAAP gross margin expanded 200 basis points year-over-year to 74.5%.

### Weak Free Cash Flow: A Major Concern
Despite the strong top-line performance, Netskope's free cash flow (FCF) was a major disappointment. The company reported a negative FCF of $15.6 million, which was significantly lower than analyst estimates of $5 million. The weak FCF was primarily due to higher operating expenses, particularly in sales and marketing, which increased 45% year-over-year.

#### FCF Metrics
| Metric | Q1 2025 | Q1 2026 | YoY Change |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Revenue | $93.2M | $123.4M | 32% |
| Non-GAAP Gross Margin | 72.5% | 74.5% | 200 bps |
| Operating Expenses | $83.1M | $120.5M | 45% |
| Free Cash Flow | ($5.5M) | ($15.6M) | -184% |

### CFO Transition: A Leadership Change
Netskope also announced the transition of its CFO, who will be leaving the company after a brief transition period. The CFO's departure has raised concerns among investors, as it may impact the company's ability to manage its finances and achieve its growth objectives.
