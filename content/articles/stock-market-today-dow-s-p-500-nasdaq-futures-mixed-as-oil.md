## Geopolitical Tensions and the Stock Market
The current stock market landscape is characterized by heightened volatility, driven in part by the recent exchange of strikes between Iran and Israel. This escalation of tensions in the Middle East has significant implications for global markets, particularly in the energy sector. As oil prices rise, investors are becoming increasingly cautious, leading to mixed futures for the Dow, S&P 500, and Nasdaq.

### Historical Context: Geopolitics and Oil Prices
The relationship between geopolitical tensions and oil prices is well-established. The 1973 oil embargo, triggered by the Yom Kippur War, is a notable example of how conflict in the Middle East can lead to significant increases in oil prices. More recently, the 2019 drone attacks on Saudi Aramco's oil facilities resulted in a spike in oil prices, highlighting the continued vulnerability of global energy markets to geopolitical instability.
