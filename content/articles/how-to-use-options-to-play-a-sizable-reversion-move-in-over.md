## Micron's Recent Price Action: A Story of Extremes
Micron Technology, a leading memory chip manufacturer, has experienced a rollercoaster ride in its stock price over the past few weeks. After surging nearly 70% in just five weeks to peak above $1,250 in late June, the stock has since retreated, surrendering 35% of its value in the 22 days that followed. This dramatic price movement presents an intriguing opportunity for traders and investors to capitalize on a potential reversion move.

### Understanding Reversion Moves
A reversion move, or mean reversion, refers to the phenomenon where asset prices that have deviated significantly from their historical means tend to revert back to their average values over time. This concept is rooted in the idea that markets often overreact to news and events, leading to price movements that are not sustained in the long term. By identifying assets that have experienced extreme price movements, traders can position themselves for a potential reversion, aiming to profit from the anticipated return to more normal valuation levels.

### Options Trading Strategy
For traders looking to capitalize on a potential reversion move in Micron, options can provide a flexible and leveraged way to express this view. One strategy could involve buying calls or selling puts, aiming to profit from an increase in the stock price. However, given the recent volatility, it's crucial to manage risk through careful position sizing and the selection of appropriate strike prices and expiration dates.

#### Call Option Strategy
Buying call options can provide a direct way to benefit from a potential increase in Micron's stock price. Traders should focus on strike prices that are slightly out of the money, balancing the cost of the option with the potential for significant upside. The table below illustrates a hypothetical call option trade:

| Strike Price | Premium | Expiration Date |
| --- | --- | --- |
| $1,100 | $20 | 1 week |
| $1,150 | $15 | 1 week |
| $1,200 | $10 | 1 week |

In this example, buying the $1,100 strike call option offers a relatively low premium, with the potential for significant upside if Micron's stock price reverts back to its recent highs.

#### Put Option Strategy
Selling put options can provide another way to profit from a reversion move, as traders aim to benefit from a decrease in volatility and an increase in the stock price. By selling put options with strike prices below the current market price, traders can collect the premium, hoping that the stock price will not fall below the strike price at expiration.
