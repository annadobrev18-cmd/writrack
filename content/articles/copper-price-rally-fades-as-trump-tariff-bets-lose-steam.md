## Risk Factors
The copper market and mining stocks are subject to various risk factors, including:

* **Trade policies**: The ongoing trade tensions between the US and China, coupled with the uncertainty surrounding the Trump tariffs, are likely to impact the copper price and mining stocks.
* **Global demand**: The copper market is subject to fluctuations in global demand, particularly from the renewable energy sector and the automotive industry.
* **Supply chain disruptions**: The copper market is vulnerable to supply chain disruptions, particularly in countries such as Chile and Peru, which are significant copper producers.

### Mitigating Risk Factors
To mitigate these risk factors, investors can consider the following strategies:

* **Diversification**: Diversifying a portfolio across different commodities and mining companies can help reduce risk.
* **Hedging**: Hedging against potential losses through the use of derivatives, such as options and futures, can help mitigate risk.
* **Active management**: Active management of a portfolio, including regular monitoring and rebalancing, can help mitigate risk and optimize returns.
