### Valuation
To determine whether Meta's stock is indeed oversold, we need to assess its valuation. The price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio is a commonly used metric to evaluate a company's valuation. Meta's current P/E ratio is approximately 20.5, which is lower than its 5-year average of 25.3. This suggests that the stock may be undervalued, supporting the oversold thesis.

#### Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio
Another essential valuation metric is the price-to-book (P/B) ratio, which compares a company's market capitalization to its book value. Meta's P/B ratio is currently 4.2, which is lower than its 5-year average of 5.5. This further supports the notion that Meta's stock is undervalued.

## Risk Factors
While Meta's financials and valuation metrics suggest that the stock may be oversold, there are several risk factors that investors should be aware of.

### Regulatory Risks
Meta operates in a highly regulated industry, and changes in regulations can significantly impact its business. For instance, the company has faced intense scrutiny over data privacy concerns, which may lead to increased regulatory oversight.

### Competitive Landscape
The technology sector is highly competitive, with numerous players vying for market share. Meta faces intense competition from companies like Alphabet (Google), Amazon, and Microsoft, which may impact its revenue growth and market share.

## Future Outlook
Despite the current oversold status, Meta's future outlook remains promising. The company is investing heavily in emerging technologies like artificial intelligence (AI) and virtual reality (VR), which may drive future growth.

### Emerging Technologies
Meta's investments in AI and VR are expected to pay off in the long term, as these technologies become increasingly mainstream. The company's Oculus VR division has already shown significant growth, with revenue increasing by 25% year-over-year.

#### AI-Driven Advertising
Meta is also leveraging AI to improve its advertising capabilities, which may lead to increased revenue growth. The company's AI-driven advertising platform has already shown promising results, with a 15% increase in ad revenue year-over-year.
