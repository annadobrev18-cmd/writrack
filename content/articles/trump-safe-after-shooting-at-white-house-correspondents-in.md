#### Market Reaction to Geopolitical Events
In the immediate aftermath of a geopolitical event, markets often react with a mix of fear and uncertainty, leading to increased volatility. This can result in a flight to safe-haven assets such as gold, US Treasury bonds, and the Japanese yen. However, as the situation unfolds and more information becomes available, markets tend to adjust and stabilize. The key factor determining the market's reaction is the perceived level of risk and uncertainty associated with the event.
