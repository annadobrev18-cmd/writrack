## Australia's Big Four Accounting Firms Under Fire
The Australian government is considering a drastic measure to address the ongoing scandals plaguing the country's Big Four accounting firms: breaking them up. This move comes after a series of high-profile scandals have eroded trust in the industry, prompting regulators to take a closer look at the oligopoly that has dominated the accounting landscape for decades.

### Background on the Big Four
The Big Four accounting firms - Deloitte, Ernst & Young, KPMG, and PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) - have long been the backbone of Australia's financial system, providing auditing, tax, and consulting services to the country's largest corporations. However, in recent years, the firms have been embroiled in a series of scandals, including allegations of tax evasion, money laundering, and audit failures.

#### Notable Scandals
Some notable scandals include:
* The Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) fined Deloitte $10 million for failing to maintain independence while auditing a client.
* Ernst & Young was sued by a group of investors who claimed the firm had failed to detect a massive fraud at a mining company.
* KPMG was accused of perpetuating a tax avoidance scheme that cost the Australian government millions of dollars in revenue.
* PwC was criticized for its role in the collapse of a major Australian bank, with regulators alleging that the firm had failed to identify significant risks.

## Implications of a Break-Up
If the Australian government were to break up the Big Four accounting firms, it would have significant implications for the industry and the broader economy. A break-up would likely lead to increased competition, as smaller firms would be able to compete more effectively for clients. However, it could also lead to a loss of expertise and resources, as the smaller firms may not have the same level of capabilities as the Big Four.
