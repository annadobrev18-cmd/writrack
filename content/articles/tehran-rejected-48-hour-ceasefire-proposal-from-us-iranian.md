### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations

To understand the current situation, it's essential to delve into the historical context of US-Iran relations. The two countries have had a tumultuous relationship since the 1979 Iranian Revolution, which overthrew the US-backed Shah. The subsequent hostage crisis, where Iranian students held American diplomats captive for 444 days, marked a significant low point in relations. Since then, the US and Iran have been at odds over various issues, including Iran's nuclear program, support for terrorism, and human rights abuses.

#### Recent Escalations

In recent years, tensions have escalated significantly. The US withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), also known as the Iran nuclear deal, in 2018 marked a turning point. The reimposition of sanctions on Iran has had a devastating impact on its economy, leading to widespread protests and instability. The situation has been further complicated by the US's "maximum pressure" campaign, which aims to strangle Iran's economy and force it to renegotiate the nuclear deal.

### Impact on Oil Prices

The current tensions have significant implications for oil prices. As a major oil producer, any disruption to Iran's oil exports can have a substantial impact on global oil prices. The Strait of Hormuz, which connects the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman, is a critical waterway for oil exports, with approximately 20% of the world's oil passing through it. Any disruption to shipping lanes in the Strait could lead to a significant increase in oil prices, which would have far-reaching consequences for the global economy.

#### Oil Price Volatility

The recent rejection of the ceasefire proposal has already led to increased volatility in oil prices. As of April 3, 2026, Brent crude oil prices have risen by over 5% in the past week, reaching $73.50 per barrel. This increase in oil prices could have significant implications for inflation, economic growth, and consumer spending.
