### Historical Context and Cost Performance

Norfolk Southern, one of the major railroad companies in the United States, has been under scrutiny for its ability to manage costs and improve efficiency. Over the years, the company has implemented various strategies to streamline its operations, reduce expenses, and enhance its service offerings. The latest cost performance metrics indicate a significant improvement in the company's operational efficiency, which has been a key factor in RBC Capital's decision to raise the stock price target.

#### Key Cost Performance Indicators

- **Operating Ratio**: Norfolk Southern has shown a steady decline in its operating ratio, a key metric that indicates the company's ability to manage its costs. A lower operating ratio signifies better cost management and higher profitability.
- **Fuel Efficiency**: The company has made significant strides in improving its fuel efficiency, which not only reduces costs but also contributes to a more sustainable operations model.
- **Labor Productivity**: Improvements in labor productivity have been another area of focus for Norfolk Southern, aiming to optimize its workforce while maintaining high service standards.
