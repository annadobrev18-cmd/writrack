### Historical Context: Understanding the Jet Fuel Supply Chain

To grasp the complexity of the situation, it's essential to delve into the historical context of Europe's jet fuel supply chain. The region's reliance on imported fuel, particularly from Russia, has been a significant concern. The ongoing conflict in Ukraine and subsequent sanctions imposed on Russia have disrupted traditional supply routes, leading to fears of a severe shortage.

| **Country** | **Jet Fuel Imports (2022)** | **Jet Fuel Production (2022)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Germany | 1.2 million metric tons | 0.5 million metric tons |
| France | 1.5 million metric tons | 0.7 million metric tons |
| UK | 1.8 million metric tons | 0.3 million metric tons |
| Russia | - | 2.1 million metric tons |

As the table above illustrates, European countries have been heavily reliant on imported jet fuel, with Russia being a significant supplier. The loss of this supply chain has forced the region to seek alternative sources, which has proven to be a challenging task.

### Diversification Efforts: A Key Factor in Mitigating Shortages

One of the primary reasons Europe has managed to avoid a jet fuel shortage is the swift diversification of its supply chain. Governments and private companies have invested heavily in establishing new trade relationships with countries like the United States, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates.
