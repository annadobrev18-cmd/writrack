### Background on Form 13D
To understand the significance of this filing, it's essential to grasp what Form 13D represents. The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) requires investors to file a Form 13D when they acquire more than 5% of a company's outstanding shares. This form discloses the investor's identity, the number of shares owned, and their intentions regarding the company's future operations or governance. Amendments to the initial filing, like the Form 13D/A submitted by Dominari Holdings INC, are filed when there are material changes to the information previously disclosed.
