## Geopolitical Tensions and Market Volatility
The rejection of Iran's response to the peace proposal by Trump has led to increased tensions in the Middle East, causing oil prices to rise. This surge in oil prices has a direct impact on the stock market, as higher oil prices can lead to increased production costs and decreased consumer spending. As a result, stocks are set for a muted open, with investors awaiting the release of US inflation data.

### Historical Context: Geopolitics and Oil Prices
The relationship between geopolitical events and oil prices is well-documented. The 1973 oil embargo, triggered by the Yom Kippur War, led to a significant increase in oil prices and had a lasting impact on the global economy. Similarly, the 1990-1991 Gulf War led to a surge in oil prices, which in turn affected the stock market. In recent years, the conflict in Syria and the sanctions on Iran have also contributed to fluctuations in oil prices.

#### Impact of Oil Prices on the Stock Market
The impact of oil prices on the stock market is multifaceted. Higher oil prices can lead to increased production costs, decreased consumer spending, and reduced economic growth. This can result in decreased stock prices, particularly in industries that are heavily reliant on oil. On the other hand, oil-producing companies may see an increase in stock prices due to higher revenue.

## Market Impact: US Inflation Data
The release of US inflation data is highly anticipated, as it will provide insight into the current state of the economy. The Consumer Price Index (CPI) and the Producer Price Index (PPI) are two key indicators of inflation. If the data shows higher-than-expected inflation, it may lead to increased interest rates, which can have a negative impact on the stock market.
