## Volaris Stock Downgrade: Understanding the Impact
The recent downgrade of Volaris stock by Goldman Sachs has sent shockwaves through the aviation industry, with investors and analysts alike scrambling to understand the implications of this move. At the heart of the downgrade are engine issues that have plagued the Mexican ultra-low-cost carrier, raising concerns about the company's ability to maintain its competitive edge in the market.

### Background on Volaris and Its Operations
Volaris is one of the leading low-cost carriers in Mexico, known for its aggressive pricing strategy and extensive network of routes. The airline has been a darling of investors in recent years, thanks to its impressive growth trajectory and solid financial performance. However, the emergence of engine issues has cast a shadow over the company's prospects, prompting Goldman Sachs to reassess its stock rating.

### The Engine Issues: A Technical Perspective
The engine issues affecting Volaris are related to the Pratt & Whitney PW1100G engines used in its Airbus A320neo fleet. These engines have been plagued by technical problems, including issues with the gearbox and compressor, which have resulted in increased maintenance costs and reduced aircraft availability. The impact of these issues on Volaris's operations has been significant, with the airline being forced to ground several aircraft and incur additional expenses to rectify the problems.
