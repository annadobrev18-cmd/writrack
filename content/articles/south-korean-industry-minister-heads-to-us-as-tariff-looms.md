### Historical Context: US-South Korea Trade Relations

The US and South Korea have a long-standing trade relationship, with the two nations signing the US-Korea Free Trade Agreement (KORUS FTA) in 2012. The agreement aimed to reduce trade barriers and increase economic cooperation between the two countries. However, the relationship has been strained in recent years, with the US imposing tariffs on various South Korean products, including steel and aluminum.

#### Section 301 Tariffs: A Brief Overview

Section 301 of the US Trade Act of 1974 grants the US Trade Representative (USTR) the authority to investigate and respond to unfair trade practices by foreign countries. The USTR has been conducting an investigation into South Korea's trade practices, with a focus on the country's automotive and electronics industries. The investigation has led to the imposition of tariffs on certain South Korean products, which has had a significant impact on the country's economy.
