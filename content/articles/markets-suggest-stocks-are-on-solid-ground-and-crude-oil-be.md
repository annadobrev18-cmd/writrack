## Market Outlook: A Shift in Sentiment
The current market landscape suggests that stocks are on solid ground, with the VIX volatility index indicating a decrease in investor anxiety. This shift in market sentiment is largely attributed to the potential resolution of global conflicts, which could have a profound impact on crude oil prices. According to Todd Gordon, if a resolution to the war is near, crude oil prices may plummet into the $80s, and possibly even the $70s, per barrel.

### VIX: A Barometer of Market Sentiment
The VIX, also known as the fear index, is a widely recognized measure of market volatility. A decrease in the VIX suggests that investors are becoming less risk-averse, and are more confident in the market's ability to withstand potential shocks. The current VIX reading is indicative of a market that is becoming increasingly complacent, with investors betting on a continuation of the bull run.

#### Historical Context: VIX and Crude Oil
| Year | VIX Average | Crude Oil Price (Avg) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 2020 | 29.25 | $41.47 |
| 2021 | 19.65 | $67.23 |
| 2022 | 24.81 | $94.55 |
| 2023 | 17.42 | $83.15 |
| 2024 | 15.61 | $78.23 |
| 2025 | 12.59 | $72.11 |
| 2026 (YTD) | 10.35 | $85.67 |

As the table above illustrates, there is a strong inverse correlation between the VIX and crude oil prices. When the VIX is high, crude oil prices tend to be low, and vice versa. This relationship is largely driven by the fact that high VIX readings are often associated with increased investor anxiety, which can lead to a decrease in demand for crude oil.

### Crude Oil: A Ticking Time Bomb
Crude oil prices have been volatile in recent years, driven by a combination of factors including geopolitical tensions, supply chain disruptions, and changes in global demand. However, with the potential resolution of the war on the horizon, crude oil prices may be in for a significant decline. A drop in crude oil prices would have far-reaching implications for the global economy, with potential benefits including lower inflation, increased consumer spending, and improved economic growth.
