### What is Margin Trading?
Margin trading is a type of trading that involves borrowing funds from a broker to purchase securities. This allows traders to leverage their investments, potentially amplifying their gains. However, it also increases the risk of significant losses if the market moves against them. Margin trading is a common practice in traditional financial markets, but its availability in the cryptocurrency space has been limited due to regulatory concerns.

### Regulatory Landscape
The regulatory landscape for cryptocurrency trading in the U.S. is complex and evolving. The Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) and the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) are the primary regulatory bodies overseeing the industry. While there is no clear framework for margin trading in cryptocurrencies, the CFTC has provided guidance on the issue, stating that platforms offering margin trading must register as a designated contract market (DCM) or a swap execution facility (SEF).

### Polymarket's Approach
Polymarket's approach to seeking approval for margin trading is two-pronged. First, the company is working to register as a DCM with the CFTC, which would allow it to offer margin trading to U.S. customers. Second, Polymarket is engaging with the SEC to ensure that its platform complies with all relevant securities laws and regulations.
