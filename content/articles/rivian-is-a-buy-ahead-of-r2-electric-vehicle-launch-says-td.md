## Fundamentals of Rivian Automotive
Rivian Automotive, Inc. is an American electric vehicle manufacturer that has been making waves in the automotive industry since its inception. Founded in 2009, the company has been focused on producing electric adventure vehicles, including the R1T pickup truck and the R1S SUV. With a strong emphasis on sustainability and performance, Rivian has managed to carve out a niche for itself in the rapidly evolving electric vehicle market.

### Historical Performance
Since its initial public offering (IPO) in November 2021, Rivian's stock has experienced significant volatility. The company's shares debuted at $78 and soared to an all-time high of $179.47 in November 2021, only to decline to a low of $24.48 in June 2022. However, with the announcement of the upcoming R2 model, Rivian's stock has started to show signs of recovery, with the shares trading at around $40 as of March 2026.

## Risk Factors
While Rivian's upcoming R2 model launch presents a significant opportunity for growth, there are also several risk factors that investors should be aware of. Some of the key risks facing Rivian include:

### Competition
The electric vehicle market is becoming increasingly competitive, with established players such as Tesla, General Motors, and Ford Motor Company competing with new entrants such as Rivian, Lucid Motors, and Fisker Inc. The competition is expected to intensify in the coming years, which could put pressure on Rivian's pricing and market share.

### Regulatory Risks
The electric vehicle industry is heavily regulated, with governments around the world implementing policies to promote the adoption of electric vehicles. However, changes in regulations or government policies could negatively impact Rivian's business, particularly if they affect the company's ability to access incentives or tax credits.

### Supply Chain Risks
Rivian relies on a complex global supply chain to produce its vehicles, which exposes the company to risks such as component shortages, logistics disruptions, and trade tensions. Any disruptions to Rivian's supply chain could negatively impact the company's production and delivery capabilities.
