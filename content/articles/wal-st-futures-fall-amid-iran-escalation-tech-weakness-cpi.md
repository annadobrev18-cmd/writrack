The Wall Street futures have fallen significantly amid the escalating tensions between the US and Iran, coupled with the weakness in the tech sector. The Dow Jones Industrial Average futures dropped by over 100 points, while the S&P 500 futures and Nasdaq-100 futures also declined. This downturn in the market is largely attributed to the rising geopolitical tensions and the disappointing earnings reports from major tech companies.

### Historical Context of US-Iran Relations

The US-Iran relations have been strained for decades, with the two countries having significant differences in their political, economic, and social ideologies. The current escalation of tensions between the two nations is largely attributed to the US withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), also known as the Iran nuclear deal, in 2018. Since then, the US has imposed severe economic sanctions on Iran, which has led to a significant decline in the country's economy.

The recent attacks on oil tankers in the Gulf of Oman and the downing of a US drone by Iran have further exacerbated the tensions between the two countries. The US has responded to these attacks by imposing additional sanctions on Iran and deploying more troops to the Middle East. The escalating tensions between the US and Iran have led to a significant increase in oil prices, which has had a negative impact on the global economy.

### Impact of Tech Sector Weakness on the Market

The tech sector has been one of the worst-performing sectors in the market, with major tech companies such as Apple, Amazon, and Facebook experiencing significant declines in their stock prices. The disappointing earnings reports from these companies have led to a loss of investor confidence in the tech sector, which has had a ripple effect on the entire market.

The tech sector is a significant component of the S&P 500 index, and any weakness in this sector can have a significant impact on the overall market. The decline in the tech sector has also led to a decline in the Nasdaq-100 index, which is heavily weighted towards tech stocks.

### Market Impact of CPI Data

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) data is scheduled to be released, and it is expected to have a significant impact on the market. The CPI data is a key indicator of inflation, and any significant changes in the data can have a significant impact on the market.

If the CPI data shows a higher-than-expected increase in inflation, it could lead to a decline in the market, as it would indicate that the economy is growing too quickly and that the Federal Reserve may need to raise interest rates to control inflation. On the other hand, if the CPI data shows a lower-than-expected increase in inflation, it could lead to an increase in the market, as it would indicate that the economy is growing at a slower pace and that the Federal Reserve may not need to raise interest rates.
