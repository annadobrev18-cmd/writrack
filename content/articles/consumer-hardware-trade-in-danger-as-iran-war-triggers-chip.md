## Sector Rotation: Consumer Hardware vs. Enterprise Tech
The impact of the memory chip price surge will be felt across various sectors of the tech industry, with consumer hardware being the most vulnerable. Consumer hardware companies, such as smartphone and laptop manufacturers, rely heavily on memory chips to produce their products. As a result, they will be forced to absorb the increased costs or pass them on to consumers, which could lead to a decline in sales and revenue.

On the other hand, enterprise tech companies, which cater to businesses and governments, may be less affected by the price surge. These companies often have more flexible pricing models and can adjust their costs accordingly. Moreover, enterprise tech products tend to have higher profit margins, which can help absorb the increased costs of memory chips.
