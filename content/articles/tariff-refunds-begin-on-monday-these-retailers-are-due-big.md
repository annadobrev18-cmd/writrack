## Tariff Refund Program: An Overview
The U.S. government is set to launch its claims-filing portal for tariff refunds on Monday, a move that could result in billions of dollars in refunds for U.S. importers, including major retailers like Walmart and Target. This development is a significant one, with far-reaching implications for the retail industry, investors, and the broader economy.

### Background: The Tariff Refund Program
The tariff refund program is a result of the U.S. Trade Representative's (USTR) decision to review and potentially refund certain tariffs imposed on imported goods. The program aims to provide relief to U.S. importers who have been affected by the tariffs, which were imposed as part of the ongoing trade tensions between the U.S. and other countries.

#### Eligibility and Refund Process
To be eligible for a tariff refund, importers must have paid tariffs on imported goods that are now subject to refund. The refund process is expected to be complex, with importers required to submit claims and provide documentation to support their claims. The USTR will review each claim and determine the amount of the refund.
