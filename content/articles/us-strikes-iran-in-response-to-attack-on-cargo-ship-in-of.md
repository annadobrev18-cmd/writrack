### Historical Context

The Strait of Hormuz is a critical waterway that connects the Persian Gulf to the Arabian Sea, with approximately 20% of the world's oil passing through it. The region has been a flashpoint for tensions between the US and Iran, with both countries having a significant interest in the area. The US has a long history of involvement in the region, dating back to the 1970s, while Iran has sought to assert its influence in the region.

#### Key Events

* 1979: The Iranian Revolution leads to the establishment of an Islamic republic, with the US imposing economic sanctions on the country.
* 1980: The Iran-Iraq War breaks out, with the US providing support to Iraq.
* 2002: The US invasion of Iraq leads to a significant increase in US military presence in the region.
* 2015: The Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) is signed, lifting economic sanctions on Iran in exchange for limits on its nuclear program.
* 2018: The US withdraws from the JCPOA, reimposing economic sanctions on Iran.
