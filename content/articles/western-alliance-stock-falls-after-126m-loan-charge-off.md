### Historical Context: Loan Charge-Offs in the Banking Sector

Loan charge-offs are not uncommon in the banking industry. They occur when a bank deems a loan to be uncollectible and writes it off as a loss. However, the size of the charge-off can significantly impact a bank's financials. In recent years, several major banks have reported significant loan charge-offs, citing various reasons such as economic downturns, industry disruptions, or poor lending practices.

Western Alliance's $126M loan charge-off is substantial, but it's essential to consider the bank's overall financial health and the context in which this charge-off occurred. The bank has been expanding its lending portfolio in recent years, which may have increased its exposure to risky loans.
