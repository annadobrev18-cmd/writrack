## Risk Factors
While high-quality bonds are considered a relatively stable investment, there are still risk factors that investors should consider.

### Interest Rate Risk
Changes in interest rates can affect the value of bonds, with rising interest rates leading to a decrease in bond values. However, this risk can be mitigated by investing in bonds with shorter maturities, which are less sensitive to changes in interest rates.

### Credit Risk
Credit risk is the risk that the issuer of the bond will default on their obligations. However, high-quality bonds are characterized by their strong credit ratings, which minimize the risk of default.

### Liquidity Risk
Liquidity risk is the risk that investors will be unable to sell their bonds quickly enough or at a fair price. However, high-quality bonds are typically highly liquid, with a large and active market.
