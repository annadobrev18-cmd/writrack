## Global Oil Markets Face Uncertainty Amidst Iran Conflict
The recent escalation of tensions between Iran and other global powers has sent shockwaves through the oil markets, leaving investors and consumers on edge. With the global oil reserves at historically low levels, the potential for an oil price shock has never been more real. In this analysis, we will delve into the current state of the oil markets, the implications of the Iran conflict, and the potential sector rotations that may arise from this turmoil.

### Current State of Oil Markets
The oil markets have been facing a perfect storm of supply and demand issues, with the global economy still reeling from the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic. The demand for oil has been slowly increasing, but the supply has been constrained due to a combination of factors, including the OPEC+ production cuts and the decline in US shale production. This has resulted in a significant drawdown in global oil reserves, leaving the markets vulnerable to any disruptions in supply.
