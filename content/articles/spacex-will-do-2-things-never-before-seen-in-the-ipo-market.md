### Democratization of IPOs
The first unprecedented move by SpaceX is its decision to allow retail investors to participate in the IPO process. Historically, IPOs have been the exclusive domain of institutional investors, with retail investors often being left out of the process. However, with the advent of new technologies and platforms, it has become possible for retail investors to participate in IPOs. SpaceX's move is a significant step in this direction, as it will allow retail investors to purchase shares in the company at the IPO stage.

### Direct Listing
The second unprecedented move by SpaceX is its decision to opt for a direct listing instead of a traditional IPO. A direct listing is a process where a company lists its shares on a stock exchange without raising any capital. This approach is different from a traditional IPO, where a company raises capital by issuing new shares to the public. SpaceX's decision to opt for a direct listing is significant, as it will allow the company to list its shares on a stock exchange without diluting the ownership of its existing shareholders.

#### Advantages of Direct Listing
The advantages of a direct listing are numerous. For one, it will allow SpaceX to avoid the costs and complexities associated with a traditional IPO. A direct listing is a simpler and more cost-effective way for a company to list its shares on a stock exchange. Additionally, it will also allow SpaceX to maintain control over the pricing of its shares, which is not possible in a traditional IPO.
