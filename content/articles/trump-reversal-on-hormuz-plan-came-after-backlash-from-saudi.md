### Historical Context: The Importance of the Strait of Hormuz

The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for global oil trade, with approximately 20% of the world's oil passing through the waterway. The strait is bounded by Iran to the north and the United Arab Emirates and Oman to the south. The region has been a source of tension for decades, with the Iranian Revolution in 1979 and the subsequent Iran-Iraq War (1980-1988) disrupting oil supplies and sparking concerns over regional stability.

#### The Impact of Geopolitical Tensions on Oil Prices

Geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz have historically had a significant impact on oil prices. During the Iran-Iraq War, oil prices surged to over $30 per barrel, while the 1990-1991 Gulf War saw prices rise to over $40 per barrel. More recently, the 2019 attack on Saudi Aramco's oil facilities by Yemen's Houthi rebels sparked a surge in oil prices, with Brent crude rising to over $70 per barrel.
