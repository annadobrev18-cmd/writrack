## Hedging Against Nasdaq-100 Volatility
The Nasdaq-100 index, comprised of the 100 largest and most actively traded non-financial stocks listed on the Nasdaq, is known for its volatility. Recently, the index has been experiencing overbought readings, which can be a precursor to a sharp pullback. While overbought readings do not predict when the pullback will happen, they do indicate that when it comes, it is likely to be severe. One way to hedge against this volatility is by using QQQ put spreads.

### What are QQQ Put Spreads?
QQQ put spreads involve buying and selling put options on the Invesco QQQ ETF, which tracks the Nasdaq-100 index. A put option gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to sell the underlying asset at a specified price (strike price) before a certain date (expiration date). By buying a put option with a higher strike price and selling a put option with a lower strike price, an investor can create a put spread that provides protection against a decline in the Nasdaq-100 index.

#### Example of a QQQ Put Spread
For example, an investor could buy a QQQ put option with a strike price of $150 and sell a QQQ put option with a strike price of $140. If the Nasdaq-100 index falls below $140, the investor will be obligated to buy the QQQ ETF at $140, but they will also be able to sell it at $150, limiting their losses. If the index falls below $150 but remains above $140, the investor will be able to sell the QQQ ETF at $150, reducing their losses.

## Fundamentals of the Nasdaq-100 Index
The Nasdaq-100 index is a modified capitalization-weighted index, meaning that the stocks with the largest market capitalization have a greater weight in the index. The index is comprised of stocks from a variety of sectors, including technology, healthcare, consumer goods, and industrials. The top holdings in the index include Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet, and Facebook.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the best way to hedge against a pullback in the Nasdaq-100 index?
The best way to hedge against a pullback in the Nasdaq-100 index is by using QQQ put spreads. This involves buying and selling put options on the Invesco QQQ ETF, which tracks the Nasdaq-100 index.
2. How do I determine the optimal strike prices for a QQQ put spread?
The optimal strike prices for a QQQ put spread will depend on the investor's risk tolerance and investment goals. Generally, the strike prices should be chosen such that the investor is protected against a decline in the Nasdaq-100 index, but also has the potential to profit from a rise in the index.
3. What are the potential risks and benefits of using QQQ put spreads?
The potential risks of using QQQ put spreads include the possibility of losing the premium paid for the put options, as well as the potential for the Nasdaq-100 index to rise instead of fall. The potential benefits include the ability to hedge against a decline in the index and the potential to profit from a rise in the index.
