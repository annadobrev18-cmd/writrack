## June Jobs Report: A Market Mover
The June jobs report has been a significant market mover, with Wall Street opening higher as the data eased rate-hike bets. The report showed that the US economy added 209,000 jobs in June, lower than the expected 230,000. This decrease in job additions has led to a decrease in expectations of a rate hike by the Federal Reserve.

### Historical Context: Job Market and Interest Rates
The job market has been a key indicator of the US economy's health, and the Federal Reserve has been closely watching the job market to determine its monetary policy decisions. In recent years, the job market has been strong, with low unemployment rates and steady job growth. However, with the economy showing signs of slowing down, the Federal Reserve has been considering raising interest rates to prevent inflation.
