The exact mechanics of the exploit are still unclear, but it is believed to have involved a combination of smart contract vulnerabilities and social engineering tactics.

### Risk Factors and Mitigation Strategies
The Kelp DAO bridge exploit highlights the risks associated with DeFi protocols, particularly those that involve cross-chain bridges. Some of the key risk factors include:

* Smart contract vulnerabilities: DeFi protocols rely on complex smart contracts, which can be vulnerable to exploits if not properly audited and tested.
* Social engineering tactics: Malicious actors can use social engineering tactics to manipulate users into revealing sensitive information or executing malicious transactions.
* Lack of regulation: The DeFi space is largely unregulated, which can make it difficult to recover losses in the event of an exploit.

To mitigate these risks, DeFi protocols can implement various strategies, such as:

* Regular security audits and testing
* Implementing robust access controls and authentication mechanisms
* Providing clear and concise documentation for users
* Establishing relationships with regulatory bodies to ensure compliance
