As the conflict squeezes margins, Indian companies are being forced to hike prices and shrink pack sizes to maintain profitability. ### Impact on Indian Companies

The Iran war has resulted in a significant increase in crude oil prices, which has had a cascading effect on the cost of production for Indian companies. With input costs rising, companies are being forced to pass on the increase to consumers in the form of higher prices. According to a recent report, several Indian companies, including Hindustan Unilever, Nestle, and Procter & Gamble, have hiked prices of their products in the range of 5-10% to offset the increase in input costs.

#### Sector-Wise Analysis

The impact of the Iran war on Indian companies is not uniform across all sectors. While companies in the consumer goods sector are being forced to hike prices, those in the IT sector are relatively insulated from the impact of the conflict. The following table provides a sector-wise analysis of the impact of the Iran war on Indian companies:

| Sector | Impact of Iran War |
| --- | --- |
| Consumer Goods | Significant increase in input costs, leading to price hikes |
| IT | Relatively insulated from the impact of the conflict |
| Automotive | Increase in crude oil prices leading to higher input costs |
| Pharmaceuticals | Minimal impact, with some companies benefiting from the depreciation of the Indian Rupee |

### Global Ripple Effects

The impact of the Iran war on India Inc is not limited to the Indian economy alone. The conflict has resulted in a significant increase in crude oil prices, which has had a ripple effect on the global economy. The following are some of the potential global ripple effects of the conflict:

#### Crude Oil Prices

The increase in crude oil prices has resulted in a significant increase in the cost of production for companies across the globe. This has had a cascading effect on the prices of various commodities, including food, transportation, and energy.

#### Currency Fluctuations

The Iran war has resulted in significant fluctuations in currency markets, with the Indian Rupee depreciating against the US Dollar. This has made imports more expensive for Indian companies, further exacerbating the margin squeeze.

#### Global Trade

The conflict has resulted in a significant disruption to global trade, with several countries imposing sanctions on Iran. This has resulted in a shortage of certain commodities, including crude oil, which has further exacerbated the price increase.
