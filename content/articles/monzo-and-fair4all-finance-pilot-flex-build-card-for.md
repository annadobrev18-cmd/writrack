### Historical Context of Credit Accessibility

The issue of credit accessibility has been a longstanding concern in the financial sector. Traditional credit scoring models have often been criticized for their bias towards individuals with established credit histories, leaving those with limited or no credit history at a significant disadvantage. This has resulted in a large segment of the population being excluded from accessing credit, further exacerbating social and economic inequalities.

#### Evolution of Alternative Credit Scoring Models

In recent years, there has been a growing recognition of the need for alternative credit scoring models that can more accurately assess the creditworthiness of individuals with non-traditional credit profiles. This has led to the development of innovative credit scoring systems that take into account a broader range of factors, including social media data, rental payments, and other non-traditional credit indicators.
