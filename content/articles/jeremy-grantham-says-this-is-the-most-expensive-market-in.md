## US Stock Market Valuations: A Historical Perspective
The US stock market has experienced significant growth in recent years, with valuations soaring to unprecedented levels. According to Jeremy Grantham, a renowned investor and co-founder of GMO, the current market is 'the most expensive in American history.' This assertion is supported by the staggering valuations of AI-related stocks, which have been driving the market's upward trend.

### Historical Context
To put the current valuations into perspective, it is essential to examine the historical context of the US stock market. The market has experienced several periods of significant growth, followed by corrections and bear markets. The dot-com bubble of the late 1990s and early 2000s is a notable example, where valuations reached extreme levels before crashing. Similarly, the 2008 financial crisis led to a significant decline in stock prices.

### Sector Rotations
The current market environment has led to significant sector rotations, with investors shifting their focus towards AI-related stocks. The technology sector, in particular, has experienced a substantial increase in investor interest, with many tech stocks reaching all-time highs.

However, this rotation has also led to a decline in interest in other sectors, such as energy and financials. The table below illustrates the sector rotations in the S&P 500 index.

| --- | --- | --- |
