## Geopolitical Tensions Escalate: An Overview
The recent threat by Trump to bomb Iran if no deal is reached by Wednesday has sent shockwaves across global markets, sparking concerns over the potential consequences of such an action. The US-Iran conflict has been a major source of geopolitical risk in recent years, with the two countries engaging in a series of tit-for-tat actions that have impacted oil prices, global trade, and economic stability.

### Historical Context
The US-Iran conflict has its roots in the 1979 Iranian Revolution, which led to the overthrow of the US-backed Shah and the establishment of an Islamic republic. Since then, the two countries have had a complex and often tense relationship, with the US imposing economic sanctions on Iran in response to its nuclear program and support for terrorism. The conflict has escalated in recent years, with the US withdrawing from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in 2018 and reimposing sanctions on Iran.
