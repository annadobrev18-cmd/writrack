### Nasdaq Listing Requirements
Nasdaq has strict listing requirements that companies must meet to maintain their listing status. One of these requirements is a minimum bid price of $1.00 per share. If a company's stock price falls below this threshold for an extended period, it may receive a delisting notice.

#### Minimum Bid Price Requirement
The minimum bid price requirement is in place to ensure that listed companies maintain a certain level of market capitalization and liquidity. A stock price below $1.00 per share can indicate a lack of investor confidence and a higher risk of volatility.
