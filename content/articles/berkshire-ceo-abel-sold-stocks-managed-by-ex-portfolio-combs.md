## Berkshire's Strategic Move: Understanding the Context
Berkshire Hathaway, under the leadership of CEO Greg Abel, has made a significant move by selling stocks managed by former portfolio manager Todd Combs. This development, as reported by the Wall Street Journal, marks a notable shift in Berkshire's investment strategy. To understand the implications of this move, it's essential to delve into the context and analyze the potential reasons behind Abel's decision.

### Historical Background: Combs' Tenure and Performance
Todd Combs, a former hedge fund manager, joined Berkshire Hathaway in 2010. During his tenure, Combs managed a significant portion of Berkshire's portfolio, focusing on investments in the technology and healthcare sectors. His performance was marked by periods of both outperformance and underperformance relative to the broader market. However, Combs' departure from Berkshire in 2022 marked the end of an era, and Abel's recent sale of stocks managed by Combs signals a new direction for the company.

### Investment Strategy: A Shift Towards Value Investing
The sale of stocks managed by Combs may indicate a shift in Berkshire's investment strategy, with a greater emphasis on value investing. This approach, championed by Warren Buffett, involves identifying undervalued companies with strong fundamentals and holding them for the long term. By selling stocks that no longer align with this strategy, Abel may be positioning Berkshire for more opportunistic investments in the future.
