### Historical Context: Iran-US Relations

To understand the current situation, it is essential to examine the historical context of Iran-US relations. The two countries have had a tumultuous relationship since the 1979 Iranian Revolution, which saw the overthrow of the US-backed Shah and the establishment of an Islamic republic. The US has imposed numerous sanctions on Iran over the years, citing concerns over its nuclear program, human rights record, and support for terrorism.

In recent years, tensions between the two countries have increased significantly, with the US withdrawing from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in 2018 and reimposing sanctions on Iran. The US has also been actively seeking to isolate Iran economically and diplomatically, which has led to a significant escalation of tensions in the region.

### Impact on Oil Prices

One of the most significant implications of the Iran-US conflict is the potential disruption to the global oil supply chain. The Strait of Hormuz, which connects the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman, is a critical waterway through which a significant portion of the world's oil is transported. Any disruption to the strait could have a devastating impact on global oil prices, leading to increased volatility and potential shortages.
