### Historical Context: Oil Prices and Financial Markets

Oil prices have historically been a significant factor influencing global financial markets. The 1970s saw a substantial increase in oil prices due to the OPEC embargo, leading to high inflation and economic stagnation in many countries. However, the US economy has become less dependent on oil over the years, thanks to increased domestic production and a shift towards renewable energy sources.

#### US Energy Independence

The US has made significant strides in achieving energy independence, with domestic oil production increasing substantially since the early 2000s. This reduction in reliance on foreign oil has made the US economy less vulnerable to oil price shocks. According to the US Energy Information Administration (EIA), the country's net imports of petroleum have decreased by more than 50% since 2005.
