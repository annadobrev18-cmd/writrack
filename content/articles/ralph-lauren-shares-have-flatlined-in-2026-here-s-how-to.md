### Understanding Strangles
A strangle is a type of options trading strategy that involves selling two out-of-the-money options: a put and a call. The put option gives the buyer the right to sell the underlying asset at a specified price (strike price), while the call option gives the buyer the right to buy the underlying asset at the strike price. By selling these options, investors are essentially betting that the underlying asset will remain within a certain price range, allowing them to collect the premiums from the options without having to buy or sell the asset.

#### Key Components of a Strangle
To successfully implement a strangle strategy, investors need to understand the following key components:
* **Strike prices**: The prices at which the put and call options can be exercised. These prices should be out-of-the-money, meaning they are above or below the current market price.
* **Expiration dates**: The dates on which the options expire. Investors should choose expiration dates that align with their investment goals and risk tolerance.
* **Premiums**: The prices at which the options are sold. Investors collect the premiums from the options, which can provide a steady stream of income.
