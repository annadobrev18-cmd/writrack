## Fundamentals of Ensign Group
The Ensign Group, Inc. is a provider of healthcare services, operating in the long-term care industry. The company's services include skilled nursing, physical, occupational, and speech therapies, as well as other rehabilitative and healthcare services. With a strong presence in the Western United States, Ensign Group has demonstrated remarkable resilience and growth amidst the challenges faced by the healthcare sector.

### Financial Performance
Ensign Group's financials have been impressive, with consistent revenue and earnings growth over the years. The company's revenue has grown at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.3% from 2017 to 2022, while its net income has increased at a CAGR of 14.1% during the same period. This growth is a testament to the company's ability to expand its operations and improve its profitability.

## Risk Factors
While Ensign Group has demonstrated strong growth and defensive attributes, there are certain risk factors that investors should be aware of. These include:

### Regulatory Risks
The long-term care industry is heavily regulated, and changes in regulations or reimbursement rates can impact Ensign Group's operations and profitability. For example, changes in Medicare and Medicaid reimbursement rates can affect the company's revenue and profitability.

### Competitive Risks
The long-term care industry is competitive, and Ensign Group faces competition from other providers of healthcare services. The company must maintain its competitive position by providing high-quality services and investing in its operations.

### Economic Risks
Economic downturns can impact Ensign Group's operations, as they can lead to reduced demand for healthcare services and lower reimbursement rates. The company must be prepared to adapt to changing economic conditions and maintain its financial stability.

## Competitive Landscape
The long-term care industry is highly competitive, with many providers of healthcare services operating in the market. Ensign Group competes with other companies, such as Genesis Healthcare and Kindred Healthcare, to attract patients and provide high-quality services. The company's competitive position is based on its strong brand, high-quality services, and strategic locations.

### Market Trends
The long-term care industry is expected to grow in the coming years, driven by an aging population and increasing demand for healthcare services. Ensign Group is well-positioned to benefit from these trends, with its strong presence in the Western United States and its ability to expand its operations.
