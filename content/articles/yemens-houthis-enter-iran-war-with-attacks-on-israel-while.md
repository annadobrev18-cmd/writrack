## Geopolitical Tensions in the Middle East
The recent escalation of tensions in the Middle East, with Yemen's Houthis entering the Iran war by launching attacks on Israel, has significant implications for the global economy. The arrival of US Marines in the region further complicates the situation, posing a threat to global stability and potentially disrupting oil supplies.

### Historical Context
The Middle East has long been a volatile region, with various conflicts and power struggles shaping the geopolitical landscape. The Iran-Iraq war (1980-1988), the Gulf War (1990-1991), and the Iraq War (2003-2011) are just a few examples of the region's tumultuous history. The current situation, with the involvement of Yemen's Houthis and the US military, adds a new layer of complexity to the already fragile regional dynamics.
