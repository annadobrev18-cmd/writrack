## Geopolitical Tensions in the Gulf: An Overview
The recent escalation of tensions between Iran and the US has significant implications for the global economy, particularly in the oil market. The Strait of Hormuz, a vital waterway connecting the Persian Gulf to the Arabian Sea, has become a focal point of the conflict. Iran's attacks on Gulf infrastructure and the US's widened strikes have raised concerns about the stability of the region and the potential impact on global oil supplies.

### Historical Context: Iran-US Relations
The Iran-US conflict has a long and complex history, dating back to the 1979 Iranian Revolution. The US has imposed economic sanctions on Iran, which has led to a significant decline in the country's economy. The recent escalation of tensions began in 2018, when the US withdrew from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), a nuclear deal signed between Iran, the US, and other world powers. Since then, the US has imposed additional sanctions on Iran, including on its oil exports.
