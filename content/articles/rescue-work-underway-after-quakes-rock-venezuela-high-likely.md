## Economic Overview of Venezuela
Venezuela, once one of the wealthiest countries in South America, has been facing severe economic challenges for several years. The recent earthquakes that struck the country, causing widespread destruction and likely high casualties, will undoubtedly exacerbate these economic woes. The economic instability in Venezuela has been characterized by hyperinflation, a significant decline in oil production, and a severe shortage of basic goods.

### Historical Context
To understand the current economic situation in Venezuela, it is essential to examine the historical context. The country's economy has been heavily reliant on oil exports, which have been declining since 2008. The decline in oil prices in 2014 further exacerbated the economic crisis. The government's policies, including price controls and currency exchange restrictions, have led to a severe shortage of basic goods, including food and medicine.
