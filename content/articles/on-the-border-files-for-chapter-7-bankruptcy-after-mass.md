## On the Border's Financial Woes
On the Border, a casual dining chain known for its Mexican-inspired cuisine, has filed for Chapter 7 bankruptcy following a series of mass closures. This move comes as a result of the company's inability to recover from the financial strain caused by the COVID-19 pandemic and increased competition in the casual dining sector.

### Historical Context
On the Border was founded in 1982 and quickly gained popularity for its signature items such as the 'Guacamole Live' and 'Endless Tortilla Chips'. However, over the years, the chain has faced significant challenges, including increased competition from fast-casual chains like Chipotle Mexican Grill and Qdoba Mexican Eats. The company has also struggled to adapt to changing consumer preferences, particularly the shift towards online ordering and delivery.
