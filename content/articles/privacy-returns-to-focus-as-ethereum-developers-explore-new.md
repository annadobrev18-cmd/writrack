#### Early Privacy Concerns
In the early days of Ethereum, privacy was not a primary concern. The focus was on developing a functional and efficient blockchain network that could support a wide range of applications. However, as the network grew and more users began to utilize Ethereum's services, concerns about privacy started to arise. The transparent nature of blockchain technology, which allows for the tracking of transactions and balances, raised questions about the potential for surveillance and data misuse.

### The Evolution of Token Standards
Ethereum's developers have been working to address these concerns by exploring new token standards that prioritize privacy. One of the most significant developments in this area is the introduction of the ERC-20 token standard. ERC-20, which was launched in 2015, provides a set of rules and guidelines for creating and managing tokens on the Ethereum blockchain. While ERC-20 has been widely adopted, it has some limitations when it comes to privacy.

#### ERC-20 Limitations
The ERC-20 token standard is designed to be transparent and open, which can make it difficult to maintain privacy. All transactions and balances are publicly visible, which can be a concern for users who value their anonymity. Additionally, the use of ERC-20 tokens can be traced back to individual users, which can be a problem for those who want to keep their transactions private.

### New Token Standards: A Focus on Privacy
To address these limitations, Ethereum developers have been working on new token standards that prioritize privacy. One of the most promising developments in this area is the introduction of the ERC-721 token standard. ERC-721, which was launched in 2018, provides a set of rules and guidelines for creating and managing non-fungible tokens (NFTs) on the Ethereum blockchain. While ERC-721 is not specifically designed for privacy, it does provide some advantages over ERC-20 when it comes to anonymity.

#### ERC-721 Advantages
The use of ERC-721 tokens can provide a higher level of anonymity than ERC-20 tokens. Because ERC-721 tokens are non-fungible, they cannot be exchanged for other tokens of the same type. This makes it more difficult to track transactions and balances, which can help to maintain user privacy. Additionally, the use of ERC-721 tokens can provide a higher level of security, as they are more resistant to hacking and other forms of cyber attacks.
