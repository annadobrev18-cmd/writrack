#### Early Adoption (2009-2013)
In its early days, Bitcoin was primarily adopted by individuals and small-scale investors. The lack of regulation and the volatility of the market made it a high-risk, high-reward investment.

#### Growing Mainstream Acceptance (2014-2017)
As Bitcoin's price began to rise, more investors started to take notice. The cryptocurrency started to gain mainstream acceptance, with companies like Microsoft and Dell beginning to accept Bitcoin as a form of payment.

#### Institutional Investment (2018-Present)
The current period has seen a significant increase in institutional investment in Bitcoin. Companies like MicroStrategy, Tesla, and Square have invested heavily in the cryptocurrency, citing its potential as a store of value and a hedge against inflation.

## Valuation and Risk Factors

### Valuation Models
There are several valuation models that can be used to estimate the value of Bitcoin. The most common models include:
1. **Cost of Production Model**: This model estimates the value of Bitcoin based on the cost of producing one Bitcoin.
2. **Supply and Demand Model**: This model estimates the value of Bitcoin based on the supply and demand dynamics of the market.
3. **Discounted Cash Flow Model**: This model estimates the value of Bitcoin based on its expected future cash flows.
