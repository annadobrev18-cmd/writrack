## Iran Conflict and Oil Prices: A Historical Context
The recent escalation of tensions in the Iran conflict has sent shockwaves through the global oil market, causing US Gulf oil prices to soar to their highest levels since 2020. This surge in oil prices is not an isolated incident but rather a continuation of a long-standing trend where geopolitical conflicts in oil-rich regions impact the global energy landscape.

### Historical Precedents
Looking back at historical data, it's evident that conflicts in the Middle East have consistently led to increases in oil prices. The Iran-Iraq war in the 1980s, the Gulf War in the 1990s, and the Arab Spring in the 2010s all had significant impacts on global oil prices. The current situation with Iran is no exception, as the potential for supply disruptions and the involvement of major oil-producing countries in the region heighten market volatility.

## Market Impact: Assessing the Current Situation
The immediate market impact of the Iran conflict on US Gulf oil prices has been profound. Prices have risen significantly, reflecting the market's anticipation of potential supply chain disruptions.
