## US Stock Futures Fall Amid Rising Geopolitical Tensions
The US stock market has begun the week on a sour note, with futures for the Dow Jones Industrial Average, S&P 500, and Nasdaq 100 all plummeting amid escalating tensions between the US and Iran. The latest development in the conflict has seen Iran launch a series of strikes against US interests in the region, prompting a significant escalation in military action.

### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations
The current tensions between the US and Iran are not a new phenomenon. The two nations have been at odds for decades, with the US imposing economic sanctions on Iran in response to its nuclear program. However, the situation has taken a turn for the worse in recent months, with the US withdrawing from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) and reimposing sanctions on Iran.

#### Impact on Oil Prices
One of the primary concerns for investors is the impact of the conflict on oil prices. ## Q2 Earnings Season: A Crucial Test for the Market
As the US stock market navigates the challenges posed by the US-Iran conflict, it must also contend with the start of Q2 earnings season. ### Earnings Expectations
According to data from Refinitiv, Q2 earnings for the S&P 500 are expected to decline by 2.6% compared to the same period last year. This decline is largely attributed to the impact of the US-China trade war, which has led to reduced demand and increased costs for many companies. However, some sectors, such as technology and healthcare, are expected to perform well, driven by strong demand and innovation.

#### Key Earnings Releases
Some of the key earnings releases to watch this week include:
- **Bank of America**: The bank is expected to report a decline in earnings, largely due to reduced interest income and increased costs.
- **Microsoft**: The technology giant is expected to report strong earnings, driven by demand for its cloud computing services.
- **Johnson & Johnson**: The healthcare company is expected to report solid earnings, driven by demand for its pharmaceutical products.
