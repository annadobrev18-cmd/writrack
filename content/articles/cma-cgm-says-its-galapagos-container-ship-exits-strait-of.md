### Historical Context: The Strait of Hormuz
The Strait of Hormuz, located between the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman, is one of the most critical maritime trade routes globally. Approximately 20% of the world's oil passes through this strait, making it a chokepoint of immense strategic importance. The strait's narrowness and the presence of several nations with complex geopolitical relationships have made it a focal point of international tensions. Iran, in particular, has been known to exert its influence in the region, occasionally threatening to disrupt traffic through the strait in response to international sanctions and other geopolitical pressures.
