## Wall Street's Changing Perception of Crypto
The upcoming Consensus Miami conference is set to witness a significant presence of Wall Street investors and financial institutions. This development marks a substantial shift in the perception of cryptocurrency and blockchain technology within the traditional financial sector. Historically, Wall Street has been skeptical about the potential of crypto, viewing it as a speculative asset with limited real-world applications. However, the growing adoption of crypto and the increasing number of use cases have led to a change of heart among many institutional investors.

### Historical Context: The Evolution of Wall Street's Crypto Stance
In the early days of cryptocurrency, Wall Street was hesitant to embrace this new asset class. The lack of regulatory clarity, volatility, and limited understanding of the underlying technology were major concerns. However, as the crypto market continued to grow and mature, institutional investors began to take notice. The launch of bitcoin futures by the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME) in 2017 and the introduction of the first bitcoin exchange-traded fund (ETF) in 2021 were significant milestones in this journey.

#### Key Milestones in Wall Street's Crypto Adoption
* 2017: CME launches bitcoin futures, providing a regulated platform for institutional investors to trade bitcoin.
* 2020: PayPal announces the launch of a crypto trading service, allowing its users to buy, sell, and hold cryptocurrencies.
* 2021: The first bitcoin ETF is launched, providing a new investment vehicle for institutional investors.
* 2022: BlackRock, the world's largest asset manager, launches a bitcoin private trust, marking a significant entry into the crypto space.

## Technical Analysis: The Growing Correlation between Crypto and Traditional Markets
The increasing presence of institutional investors in the crypto market has led to a growing correlation between crypto and traditional markets. According to a report by Coin Metrics, the correlation between bitcoin and the S&P 500 has increased significantly over the past year, indicating that crypto is becoming more integrated into the broader financial system.
