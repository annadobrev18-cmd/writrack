### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations

The US and Iran have had a tumultuous relationship since the 1979 Iranian Revolution, with tensions escalating in recent years due to disagreements over nuclear programs, oil exports, and regional influence. However, the latest breakthrough, facilitated by diplomatic efforts, has raised hopes for a new era of cooperation between the two nations.

To understand the significance of this development, it is crucial to examine the historical context of US-Iran relations. The 2015 Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), also known as the Iran nuclear deal, was a landmark agreement that aimed to restrict Iran's nuclear program in exchange for relief from economic sanctions. However, the US withdrawal from the deal in 2018 and the subsequent reimposition of sanctions led to a significant deterioration in relations.
