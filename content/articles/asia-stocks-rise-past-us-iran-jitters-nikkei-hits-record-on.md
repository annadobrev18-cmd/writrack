## Asia Stocks Rise Past US-Iran Jitters
The recent escalation of tensions between the US and Iran has sent shockwaves across global markets, with many investors bracing for a potential downturn. However, Asia stocks have shown remarkable resilience, with the Nikkei index hitting a record high on stimulus cheer. This defies the conventional wisdom that geopolitical tensions would lead to a decline in stock prices.

### Historical Context
To understand the current market dynamics, it's essential to look at the historical context. The US-Iran conflict has been simmering for years, with periods of heightened tensions followed by relative calm. In the past, such tensions have led to increased volatility in the markets, with investors seeking safe-haven assets such as gold and bonds. However, the current situation is different, with the Asian markets showing a remarkable ability to withstand the pressures.

#### Previous Instances of Geopolitical Tensions
In 2019, the US-Iran conflict escalated, with the US imposing sanctions on Iran and Iran retaliating by attacking Saudi oil facilities. The markets responded with a significant increase in volatility, with the VIX index (a measure of market volatility) spiking to 20. However, the Asian markets, particularly the Nikkei index, showed remarkable resilience, with the index recovering quickly from the initial shock.

### Market Impact
The current market impact of the US-Iran tensions is more nuanced. While the tensions have led to an increase in oil prices, the Asian markets have been able to absorb the shock. The Nikkei index, in particular, has been driven by the stimulus package announced by the Japanese government, which has boosted investor sentiment.

#### Stimulus Package
The Japanese government's stimulus package is aimed at boosting economic growth, which has been sluggish in recent years. The package includes measures such as increased government spending, tax cuts, and monetary policy easing. The stimulus package has been well-received by investors, with the Nikkei index hitting a record high.

### Technical Analysis
From a technical analysis perspective, the Nikkei index is showing a strong uptrend, with the index breaking out above the 200-day moving average. The relative strength index (RSI) is also indicating that the index is not overbought, with a reading of 60. The moving average convergence divergence (MACD) is also showing a bullish signal, with the MACD line crossing above the signal line.

#### Key Technical Levels
The key technical levels for the Nikkei index are:
- Support: 23,000
- Resistance: 24,000
- 200-day moving average: 22,500

### Expert Opinions
Experts are divided on the outlook for the Asian markets, with some predicting a continuation of the uptrend and others warning of a potential downturn.

#### Bullish View
According to a report by Goldman Sachs, the Asian markets are likely to continue their uptrend, driven by the stimulus package and the resilient economy. The report notes that the Japanese economy is showing signs of recovery, with the GDP growth rate increasing to 1.5% in the first quarter.

#### Bearish View
On the other hand, a report by Morgan Stanley warns of a potential downturn in the Asian markets, driven by the US-Iran tensions and the slowdown in global trade. The report notes that the Asian markets are highly correlated with the global economy, and any slowdown in global trade could have a significant impact on the region.
