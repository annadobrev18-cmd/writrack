### Market Impact: The Economic Burden of Impaired Driving
The economic burden of impaired driving accidents extends beyond the immediate costs of medical care and property damage. It also includes the long-term consequences of lost productivity, legal fees, and the emotional toll on victims and their families. A study by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) found that the total economic cost of impaired driving accidents in the US is approximately $129 billion per year.

#### Breakdown of Economic Costs
The economic costs of impaired driving accidents can be broken down into several categories:
* **Medical Costs**: The cost of medical care for victims of impaired driving accidents, including emergency services, hospitalization, and rehabilitation.
* **Lost Productivity**: The loss of income and productivity due to injuries or fatalities resulting from impaired driving accidents.
* **Property Damage**: The cost of repairing or replacing damaged vehicles and property.
* **Legal Fees**: The cost of legal representation and court fees associated with impaired driving cases.
