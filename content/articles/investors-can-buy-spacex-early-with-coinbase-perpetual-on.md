### Understanding Perpetual Futures
Perpetual futures, also known as perpetual swaps, are a type of financial derivative that enables investors to bet on the future value of an underlying asset. Unlike traditional futures contracts, which have an expiration date, perpetual futures do not have a settlement date, allowing investors to hold positions indefinitely. This feature makes them particularly appealing for speculating on the value of pre-IPO companies, which can remain private for extended periods.
