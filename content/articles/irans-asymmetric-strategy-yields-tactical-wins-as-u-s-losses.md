### Historical Context: Iran's Asymmetric Strategy

Iran's asymmetric strategy is not a new phenomenon. The country has been employing unconventional tactics since the Iran-Iraq war in the 1980s. However, the recent escalation of tensions with the United States has brought this strategy to the forefront of global attention. Iran's use of proxy forces, cyberattacks, and missile strikes has allowed it to counter the US military's conventional superiority.

#### Key Components of Iran's Asymmetric Strategy

* **Proxy Forces**: Iran's use of proxy forces, such as Hezbollah and the Houthis, has allowed it to project power beyond its borders without directly engaging the US military.
* **Cyberattacks**: Iran's cyberattacks on US targets, including oil facilities and government agencies, have demonstrated its ability to disrupt critical infrastructure.
* **Missile Strikes**: Iran's missile strikes on US bases in Iraq have shown its ability to retaliate against US military actions.
