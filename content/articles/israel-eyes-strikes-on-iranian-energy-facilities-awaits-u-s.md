### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations

The US-Iran relationship has been strained for decades, with tensions escalating since the US withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in 2018. The reimposition of sanctions has severely impacted Iran's economy, leading to a significant decline in oil exports. Iran has responded by increasing its nuclear activities, which has raised concerns among the international community.

### Current Situation: Israeli Considerations

Israel has long been concerned about Iran's nuclear program and its support for militant groups in the region. The recent intelligence reports suggesting a potential Hormuz blockade have prompted Israel to consider strikes on Iranian energy facilities. This move is seen as a way to cripple Iran's economy and limit its ability to fund its nuclear program.

#### Potential Targets

The potential targets for Israeli strikes include Iran's oil refineries, petrochemical plants, and other energy infrastructure. These facilities are critical to Iran's economy, and damaging them could have significant consequences for the country's ability to export oil and generate revenue.

### Energy Market Implications

A strike on Iranian energy facilities would have significant implications for the global energy market. Iran is a major oil producer, and any disruption to its production would lead to a shortage in the global market. This would drive up oil prices, potentially leading to higher inflation and slower economic growth.
