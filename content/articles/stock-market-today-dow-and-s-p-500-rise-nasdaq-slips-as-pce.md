## US Market Overview
The US stock market has witnessed a mixed performance, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average and the S&P 500 index rising, while the Nasdaq Composite index has slipped. This trend can be attributed to the release of the Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) data, which came in hotter than expected. The PCE index, a key measure of inflation, has risen by 0.6% in May, exceeding the forecasted 0.4% increase.

### Historical Context
The PCE index has been closely watched by investors and policymakers, as it is considered a reliable indicator of inflationary pressures in the economy. The recent increase in the PCE index has sparked concerns about the potential for higher interest rates, which could impact the overall market performance. Historically, the PCE index has been a key driver of monetary policy decisions, with the Federal Reserve using it as a benchmark to gauge the effectiveness of its policies.
