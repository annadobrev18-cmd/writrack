## The Rise of AI Memory Supercycle
The advent of Artificial Intelligence (AI) has ushered in a new era of technological advancements, with memory chips playing a critical role in the development and deployment of AI systems. The AI memory supercycle refers to the surge in demand for memory chips, particularly DRAM (Dynamic Random-Access Memory) and NAND (Not AND) flash memory, driven by the growing need for data storage and processing in AI applications. Two major players in the memory chip industry, SK Hynix and Micron, are vying for dominance in this emerging market.

### Historical Context
To understand the current landscape, it's essential to delve into the history of the memory chip industry. The market has experienced several cycles of boom and bust, with the most recent downturn occurring in 2019. However, the emergence of AI and the subsequent increase in demand for memory chips have led to a significant upturn in the industry's fortunes. SK Hynix and Micron, as two of the largest memory chip manufacturers, have been at the forefront of this shift.
