The Strait of Hormuz, a vital waterway connecting the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman, has long been a flashpoint for geopolitical tensions. With approximately 20% of the world's oil passing through this narrow strait, any disruption to traffic flow can have significant implications for global energy markets. Recent events have once again brought the Strait of Hormuz into focus, with Bank of America identifying a key oil and gas name as its top pick for playing the potential closure of this critical waterway.
