The year 2026 has been a rollercoaster ride for Bitcoin, the flagship cryptocurrency. Despite its brutal sell-off, traders and investors haven't backed away from the space. In fact, the recent downturn has sparked a flurry of trading in related stocks, including one big bullish bet.

### Historical Context: Bitcoin's Price Volatility
To put the current situation into perspective, it's essential to look at Bitcoin's historical price volatility. The cryptocurrency has experienced several boom-and-bust cycles since its inception in 2009. The most notable ones include the 2017 bull run, which saw Bitcoin's price surge to nearly $20,000, and the 2020 COVID-19-induced crash, which sent the price plummeting to around $3,800.
