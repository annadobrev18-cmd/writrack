## Gold Prices Rebound on Dip Buying

The recent rebound in gold prices can be attributed to dip buying, a strategy where investors purchase assets that have experienced a significant decline in value. This phenomenon is particularly evident in the gold market, where prices have been volatile due to various geopolitical and economic factors.

One of the primary drivers of gold prices is geopolitical tension. The ongoing conflict between the US and Iran has led to increased uncertainty in the market, causing investors to seek safe-haven assets like gold. The recent progress in US-Iran talks has eased some of these tensions, resulting in a slight decline in gold prices. However, the situation remains volatile, and any escalation in tensions could lead to a surge in gold prices.

## Fed Minutes and Interest Rates

Another crucial factor influencing gold prices is the Federal Reserve's monetary policy. The Fed's decision to raise or lower interest rates can significantly impact the value of gold. When interest rates are low, gold becomes a more attractive investment option, as the opportunity cost of holding gold is lower. Conversely, when interest rates are high, investors may prefer to hold assets with higher yields, such as bonds or stocks.
