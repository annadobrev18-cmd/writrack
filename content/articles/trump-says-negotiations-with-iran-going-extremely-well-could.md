## Geopolitical Landscape: US-Iran Relations
The recent statement by President Trump that negotiations with Iran are going 'extremely well' and a ceasefire could be imminent has sparked a wave of optimism in the market. This development has the potential to significantly impact the global economy, particularly in the energy sector. To understand the implications of this statement, it is essential to delve into the history of US-Iran relations and the current geopolitical landscape.

### Historical Context
US-Iran relations have been strained since the 1979 Iranian Revolution, which led to the overthrow of the US-backed Shah. The relationship further deteriorated in 1980 when American diplomats were taken hostage, leading to a prolonged period of tension. In recent years, the US withdrawal from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in 2018 and the subsequent imposition of sanctions have escalated tensions between the two nations.

### Current Developments
The current negotiations between the US and Iran are centered around the possibility of reviving the JCPOA, which aimed to limit Iran's nuclear program in exchange for relief from economic sanctions. A successful negotiation could lead to the easing of sanctions, potentially resulting in increased oil exports from Iran and a subsequent decrease in global oil prices.
