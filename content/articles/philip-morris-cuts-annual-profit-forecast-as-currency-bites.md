## Philip Morris Cuts Annual Profit Forecast: Understanding the Impact
Philip Morris, a leading international tobacco company, has recently cut its annual profit forecast due to the significant impact of currency volatility and cost pressures. This move has sent ripples through the stock market, with investors and analysts closely watching the developments.

### Currency Volatility: A Major Challenge
Currency volatility has been a major challenge for multinational companies like Philip Morris, which operates in multiple countries and currencies. The strengthening of the US dollar against other currencies has resulted in a significant decline in the value of foreign earnings when translated back into dollars. This has negatively impacted Philip Morris's revenue and profit margins.

### Cost Pressures: Another Key Challenge
In addition to currency volatility, Philip Morris is also facing significant cost pressures. The company has been investing heavily in new products and technologies, including reduced-risk products (RRPs) such as heated tobacco and e-vapor products. While these investments are expected to drive long-term growth, they have resulted in increased costs in the short term.

## Valuation: Assessing the Impact on Stock Price
The cut in annual profit forecast has resulted in a decline in Philip Morris's stock price. The company's stock price has fallen by over 10% since the announcement, reflecting the market's concerns about the impact of currency volatility and cost pressures on the company's profitability.

### Technical Analysis: Support and Resistance Levels
The decline in Philip Morris's stock price has resulted in a test of the support level at $80. The stock price has bounced back from this level in the past, and it is likely to do so again. However, if the stock price breaks below this level, it could result in a further decline to the next support level at $70.
