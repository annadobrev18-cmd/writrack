### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations

The US-Iran relationship has been strained for decades, with the 1979 Iranian Revolution marking a significant turning point. The revolution led to the establishment of an Islamic republic, which has been at odds with Western powers, particularly the United States. The US has imposed numerous sanctions on Iran, citing concerns over its nuclear program, human rights record, and support for terrorism.

In recent years, tensions have escalated, with the US withdrawing from the Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA) in 2018. The JCPOA, also known as the Iran nuclear deal, was signed in 2015 by the US, Iran, and other world powers. The deal aimed to limit Iran's nuclear program in exchange for relief from economic sanctions.

#### Key Events in US-Iran Relations

* 1979: Iranian Revolution
* 1980: US imposes economic sanctions on Iran
* 2015: JCPOA signed
* 2018: US withdraws from JCPOA
* 2020: US drone strike kills top Iranian military commander
