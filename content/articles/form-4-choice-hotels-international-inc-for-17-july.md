### Historical Context of Form 4 Filings
Form 4 filings are mandatory reports that insiders of publicly traded companies must submit to the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) within two business days of executing a transaction in the company's securities. These filings are crucial as they offer transparency into the actions of company insiders, including executives, directors, and significant shareholders. By analyzing these filings, investors can gain a better understanding of the confidence levels of insiders in the company's future prospects.
