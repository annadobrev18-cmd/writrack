Coinbase, one of the leading cryptocurrency exchanges, has announced the introduction of stock perpetual futures contracts for its non-U.S. customers. This move marks a significant expansion of the company's derivatives offerings, allowing users to trade contracts based on the value of traditional stocks. The introduction of these contracts is a strategic move by Coinbase to increase its market share and provide more diverse investment options to its global customer base.

### Background on Perpetual Futures Contracts
Perpetual futures contracts, also known as perpetual swaps, are a type of derivative contract that allows traders to bet on the future value of an underlying asset, such as a stock or commodity. Unlike traditional futures contracts, which have an expiration date, perpetual futures contracts do not expire and can be held indefinitely. This feature makes them attractive to traders who want to speculate on the price movements of an asset without having to worry about expiration dates.
