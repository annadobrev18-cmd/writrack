## Amazon Stock's Recent Performance
Amazon stock has been on a tear recently, leading a strong day for the so-called Magnificent Seven, a group of top tech stocks that have been driving the market's gains. The stock's climb can be attributed to several factors, including the success of its Prime Day sales event and a recent price hike in its cloud computing services.

### Prime Day Sales
Prime Day, which was held on June 21-22, was a huge success for Amazon, with sales surpassing $12 billion, a 20% increase from last year's event. The two-day shopping extravaganza offered exclusive deals to Amazon's Prime members, with millions of items sold worldwide. The success of Prime Day not only boosted Amazon's revenue but also helped to increase the company's profitability, as the event encouraged customers to sign up for Prime memberships, which provide a steady stream of revenue for the company.

### Cloud Price Hike
In addition to the success of Prime Day, Amazon's cloud computing arm, Amazon Web Services (AWS), recently announced a price hike for its services. The price increase, which ranges from 5-20% depending on the service, is expected to boost AWS's revenue and profitability. AWS is the largest cloud computing provider in the world, with a market share of over 30%, and its services are used by millions of customers, including large enterprises, governments, and startups.

## Fundamentals
Amazon's strong performance can be attributed to its solid fundamentals. The company has a diverse revenue stream, with e-commerce, cloud computing, and advertising being its main sources of income. Amazon's e-commerce business is the largest in the world, with over $300 billion in annual sales. The company's cloud computing arm, AWS, is the largest cloud computing provider in the world, with a market share of over 30%. Amazon's advertising business is also growing rapidly, with revenue increasing by over 50% in the last year.
