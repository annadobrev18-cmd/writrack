## Fundamentals of Affirm Holdings
Affirm Holdings, a financial technology company, has been making waves in the payments industry with its innovative approach to consumer financing. The company's platform provides transparent, flexible, and consumer-friendly payment options for consumers, helping them to avoid revolving debt and hidden fees associated with traditional credit cards.

### Business Model
Affirm's business model is centered around its merchant network, which includes over 200,000 merchants, such as Peloton, Walmart, and Expedia. The company generates revenue through a combination of interest income, fees from merchants, and interchange fees. This diversified revenue stream has helped Affirm to maintain a strong financial position, with a gross merchandise volume (GMV) of over $20 billion in 2025.

## Valuation and Growth Prospects
Morgan Stanley's prediction of a rally in Affirm's stock price is based on the company's strong growth prospects and attractive valuation. With a price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio of 25.6, Affirm's stock is trading at a discount to its peers, such as PayPal and Square, which have P/E ratios of 34.5 and 31.4, respectively.

### Catalysts for Growth
Several catalysts are expected to drive Affirm's growth in the coming quarters, including:
* Expansion of its merchant network, with a focus on large enterprises and strategic partnerships
* Introduction of new products and services, such as Affirm's recently launched debit card
* Increasing adoption of buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) solutions, which are expected to reach $1.5 trillion in transaction value by 2027

## Risk Factors and Challenges
While Affirm's growth prospects are attractive, the company is not without its risks and challenges. Some of the key risk factors include:
* Intense competition in the payments industry, with established players such as PayPal and Visa
* Regulatory risks, particularly with regards to consumer protection and lending practices
* Economic risks, such as a potential recession or downturn in consumer spending

### Mitigating Risks
To mitigate these risks, Affirm has implemented several strategies, including:
* Diversifying its revenue streams through the introduction of new products and services
* Investing in technology and innovation to stay ahead of the competition
* Building strong relationships with its merchant network and consumers to drive loyalty and retention
