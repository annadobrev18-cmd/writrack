## Fundamentals of the Case
Sam Bankman-Fried, the founder of FTX, has officially requested a presidential pardon from former President Donald Trump. This move has sparked intense debate and discussion within the cryptocurrency community and beyond. To understand the implications of this request, it is essential to delve into the fundamentals of the case.

### Background on Sam Bankman-Fried and FTX
Sam Bankman-Fried, also known as SBF, was the CEO of FTX, a cryptocurrency derivatives exchange that was once valued at $32 billion. However, in November 2022, FTX filed for bankruptcy, and SBF resigned as CEO. The collapse of FTX led to a significant loss of customer funds, with estimates suggesting that around $8 billion in customer assets were missing.

### Charges Against Sam Bankman-Fried
SBF has been charged with multiple counts of fraud, money laundering, and conspiracy. The charges allege that SBF used customer funds for personal expenses, invested in other companies, and made risky trades without proper disclosure. If convicted, SBF faces up to 115 years in prison.
