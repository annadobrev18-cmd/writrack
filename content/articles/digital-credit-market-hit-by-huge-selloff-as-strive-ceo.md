### Historical Context of Digital Credit Markets
Digital credit markets have experienced rapid growth in recent years, driven by the increasing adoption of blockchain technology and the expansion of decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms. These platforms have enabled individuals and institutions to access credit and lending services in a more transparent, efficient, and often less regulated environment compared to traditional financial systems. However, this growth has also been accompanied by increased volatility and risk, particularly due to the high levels of leverage used by many market participants.

#### Leverage in Digital Credit Markets
Leverage, or the use of borrowed capital to increase the potential return of an investment, is a common practice in financial markets. In the context of digital credit markets, leverage can amplify gains but also significantly increases the risk of substantial losses. When asset prices move against a leveraged position, the resulting liquidations can lead to a cascade of selling, exacerbating market downturns. The Strive CEO's comments suggest that such a scenario may have contributed to the recent selloff, highlighting the delicate balance between risk and reward in these markets.
