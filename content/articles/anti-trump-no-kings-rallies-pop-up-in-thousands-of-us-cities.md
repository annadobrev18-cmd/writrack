Social unrest and protests are not new to the US, and their impact on the economy and stock market can be significant. The 1960s and 1970s saw widespread protests against the Vietnam War, which led to increased social and economic instability. The 1992 Los Angeles riots and the 2011 Occupy Wall Street movement also had significant economic implications. Understanding the historical context of social unrest and its impact on the economy can help investors make informed decisions.
