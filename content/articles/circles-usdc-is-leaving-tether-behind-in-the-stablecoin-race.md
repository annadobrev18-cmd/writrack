## Stablecoin Market Overview
The stablecoin market has experienced significant growth in recent years, with various players competing for dominance. Stablecoins are cryptocurrencies designed to maintain a stable value relative to a fiat currency, such as the US dollar. They have gained popularity due to their potential to reduce volatility in the crypto market and provide a reliable means of exchange.

### USDC and Tether: Market Leaders
USDC, issued by Circle, and Tether (USDT), issued by Tether Limited, are two of the most widely used stablecoins. Both have gained significant traction in the market, with USDC recently surpassing Tether in terms of volume, according to new data from Visa.

#### Historical Data
To understand the significance of this development, it's essential to examine the historical data. In 2020, Tether was the clear market leader, with a dominance of over 80% in the stablecoin market. However, in recent years, USDC has been gaining ground, with its market share increasing steadily.

## Fundamentals of USDC and Tether
To analyze the reasons behind USDC's surge, it's crucial to understand the fundamentals of both stablecoins.

### USDC
USDC is a fully collateralized stablecoin, meaning that every USDC in circulation is backed by an equivalent amount of US dollars held in reserve. This approach provides a high level of transparency and security, as the reserves are regularly audited and published.

### Tether
Tether, on the other hand, has faced controversy in the past regarding its reserve holdings. While Tether claims to be fully backed by US dollars, there have been concerns about the accuracy of these claims. In 2021, Tether published a breakdown of its reserves, which included a significant portion of commercial paper and other assets.
