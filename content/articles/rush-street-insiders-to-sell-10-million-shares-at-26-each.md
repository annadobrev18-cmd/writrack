### Market Impact: Insider Selling and Investor Sentiment

Insider selling can have a profound impact on investor sentiment and the stock price. When insiders, who have access to internal information, decide to sell their shares, it can signal to the market that the company's prospects may not be as positive as perceived. This can lead to a decrease in investor confidence, potentially causing the stock price to drop.

#### Insider Selling: A Red Flag?

Insider selling is not always a negative indicator. It can be a part of a planned diversification strategy or a response to personal financial needs. However, the scale of the selling, as in the case of Rush Street, can raise eyebrows. The decision to sell 10 million shares at $26 each may indicate that insiders believe the stock is overvalued or that they anticipate a downturn in the company's performance.
