Private credit has emerged as a major point of contention, with investors growing increasingly wary of the potential risks associated with this sector. The private credit market has expanded substantially in recent years, with numerous firms and investors seeking to capitalize on the attractive yields offered by private debt instruments. However, this rapid growth has also raised concerns about the potential for a bubble, with some analysts warning that the market may be due for a correction.

### US-Iran Tensions
The simmering tensions between the US and Iran have also contributed to the market's volatility. The ongoing conflict has led to a surge in oil prices, with Brent crude rising above $80 per barrel. This increase in oil prices has far-reaching implications for the global economy, as higher energy costs can erode corporate profit margins and dampen consumer spending.

#### Geopolitical Risks
The US-Iran conflict is not an isolated event; rather, it is part of a broader landscape of geopolitical risks that investors must navigate. The ongoing trade tensions between the US and China, the Brexit saga, and the rising tensions in the Middle East all contribute to an environment of uncertainty and volatility.
