### Fundamentals of Retirement Planning
Retirement planning involves creating a sustainable income stream to support one's lifestyle after ceasing to work. The key components of a retirement plan include:
* **Savings**: Accumulating a sufficient amount of wealth to support living expenses during retirement.
* **Investments**: Growing savings through investments to keep pace with inflation and generate returns.
* **Expenses**: Estimating and managing living expenses during retirement to ensure a sustainable income stream.

### Valuation of Investment Strategies
To retire by 40, the Florida woman will need to employ an aggressive investment strategy to grow her wealth rapidly. Some possible investment options include:
* **Stocks**: Historically, stocks have provided higher returns over the long-term, but they come with higher volatility.
* **Real Estate**: Investing in real estate can provide a steady income stream and potential for capital appreciation.
* **Bonds**: Bonds offer a lower-risk investment option with regular income, but returns may not keep pace with inflation.

### Risk Factors Associated with Early Retirement
Early retirement poses several risks, including:
* **Inflation**: Inflation can erode the purchasing power of retirement savings, reducing the standard of living.
* **Market Volatility**: Market downturns can significantly impact investment returns, potentially depleting retirement savings.
* **Longevity Risk**: Outliving retirement savings is a significant risk, particularly if retirement begins too early.
