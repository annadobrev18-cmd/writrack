The implications of Iran's response are far-reaching, with potential consequences for the global oil market, regional stability, and the future of US-Iran relations.

#### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations
To understand the current situation, it's essential to examine the historical context of US-Iran relations. The two countries have a long and complicated history, with periods of cooperation and conflict. The 1979 Iranian Revolution marked a significant turning point, as the US-backed Shah was overthrown, and the Islamic Republic of Iran was established. Since then, the US and Iran have been at odds, with the US imposing sanctions and supporting regional allies to counter Iranian influence.

## Trump-Xi Summit: A High-Stakes Meeting
The upcoming Trump-Xi summit is a high-stakes meeting, with the fate of global trade and economic stability hanging in the balance. The US and China have been engaged in a bitter trade war, with tariffs and counter-tariffs imposed on each other's goods. The summit offers a glimmer of hope for a resolution, but the outcome is far from certain. The US is seeking significant concessions from China, including greater market access, intellectual property protection, and an end to forced technology transfers. China, on the other hand, is seeking relief from US tariffs and a more balanced trade relationship.

### Sector Rotations: A Shift in Market Sentiment
