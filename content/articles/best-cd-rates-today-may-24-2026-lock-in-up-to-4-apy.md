## Valuation and Risk Factors
When evaluating CD investments, it's crucial to consider the valuation and risk factors associated with these products. CDs are generally considered low-risk investments, as they are insured by the FDIC or NCUA, protecting deposits up to $250,000.

### Liquidity and Penalty Structures
One of the primary risks associated with CDs is the potential penalty for early withdrawal. Investors should carefully review the terms and conditions of their CD, as some institutions may impose significant penalties for withdrawing funds before the maturity date.

#### Penalty-Free CDs
Some financial institutions offer penalty-free CDs, allowing investors to withdraw their funds without incurring a penalty. These products often come with lower interest rates or more restrictive terms, making it essential to weigh the benefits and drawbacks before investing.
