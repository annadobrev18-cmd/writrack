## Aave's Governance Structure
Aave, a leading decentralized finance (DeFi) protocol, is on the cusp of a significant upgrade, prompting a heated governance battle among its stakeholders. The protocol's governance structure is designed to be decentralized, allowing token holders to participate in decision-making processes. However, this structure has also led to disagreements and power struggles within the community.

### Governance Token (AKE)
The AKE token is at the heart of Aave's governance system. Token holders can vote on proposals, which are used to determine the direction of the protocol. The AKE token has been trading at a relatively stable price, with a market capitalization of over $1 billion.

## Risk Factors
The ongoing governance battle poses significant risks to Aave's future. If the battle is not resolved, it could lead to a decline in the protocol's TVL and a decrease in the value of the AKE token.

### Regulatory Risks
Aave, like other DeFi protocols, is subject to regulatory risks. The protocol's decentralized nature makes it difficult for regulators to oversee, and changes in regulations could negatively impact the protocol's operations.

### Security Risks
Aave's smart contracts are also subject to security risks. If the contracts are hacked, it could lead to a loss of funds and a decline in the protocol's reputation.
