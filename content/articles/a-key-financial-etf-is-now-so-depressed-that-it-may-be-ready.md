## IAI ETF Underperformance: A Rare Occurrence
The iShares U.S. Broker-Dealers & Securities Exchanges ETF (IAI) has been experiencing a significant sell-off in 2026, leading to underperformance compared to the S&P 500. This has resulted in the IAI/SPX relative ratio becoming oversold, a rare occurrence over the past few years. The relative ratio is a technical indicator used to compare the performance of two securities or indices. In this case, it measures the performance of the IAI ETF relative to the S&P 500.

### Historical Context
To understand the significance of the current oversold conditions, it's essential to examine the historical context of the IAI/SPX relative ratio. Over the past five years, the ratio has fluctuated between 0.8 and 1.2, with an average value of 1.0. However, in 2026, the ratio has dropped to a low of 0.6, indicating a significant underperformance of the IAI ETF compared to the S&P 500.
