## Fundamentals of Apalutamide
Apalutamide, developed by Johnson & Johnson (J&J), is a nonsteroidal antiandrogen primarily used in the treatment of nonmetastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (nmCRPC) and metastatic castration-sensitive prostate cancer (mCSPC). The recent announcement that apalutamide cuts prostate cancer death risk by 20% is a significant breakthrough, positioning J&J as a leader in oncology.

### Historical Development
Apalutamide was first approved by the FDA in 2018 for the treatment of nmCRPC. Since then, it has shown remarkable efficacy in clinical trials, leading to its expanded approval for additional indications. The drug works by inhibiting the action of androgens, which are male hormones that can promote the growth of prostate cancer cells.
