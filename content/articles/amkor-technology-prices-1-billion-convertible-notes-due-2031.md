## Amkor Technology's Convertible Notes: An Overview
Amkor Technology, a leading provider of semiconductor packaging and test services, has priced $1 billion in convertible notes due 2031. This move is expected to have significant implications for the company's financials and investors. In this analysis, we will delve into the details of the convertible notes, their potential impact on Amkor Technology's stock, and the broader market implications.

### Convertible Notes: A Primer
Convertible notes are a type of debt instrument that can be converted into equity at a later date. They offer investors the opportunity to lend money to a company with the option to convert the debt into shares at a predetermined price. This type of financing is often used by companies to raise capital without diluting existing shareholders' equity.

### Terms of the Convertible Notes
The $1 billion convertible notes due 2031 have a conversion price of $25.50 per share, representing a 30% premium to the closing price of Amkor Technology's stock on the pricing date. The notes will bear interest at a rate of 2.5% per annum, payable semi-annually. The conversion price and interest rate are key factors in determining the value of the convertible notes and their potential impact on Amkor Technology's stock.
