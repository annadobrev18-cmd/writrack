## Fundamentals
To understand the current state of Bitcoin, it's essential to look at its fundamentals. The cryptocurrency's price is influenced by various factors, including its supply and demand, adoption rate, and regulatory environment.

### Supply and Demand
The supply of Bitcoin is limited, with a total of 21 million coins that can be mined. The current supply is around 18.5 million, with the remaining 2.5 million expected to be mined over the next few years. The demand for Bitcoin, on the other hand, is influenced by various factors, including its adoption rate, regulatory environment, and market trends.

### Adoption Rate
The adoption rate of Bitcoin has been increasing over the years, with more businesses and individuals using it as a form of payment. However, the adoption rate has been slower than expected, which has contributed to the current decline in price.

### Regulatory Environment
The regulatory environment for Bitcoin is still uncertain, with different countries having different approaches to regulating the cryptocurrency. This uncertainty has contributed to the current decline in price, as investors are hesitant to invest in a market with unclear regulations.
