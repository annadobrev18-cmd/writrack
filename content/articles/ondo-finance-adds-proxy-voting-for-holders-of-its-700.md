### The Significance of Proxy Voting
Proxy voting allows shareholders to delegate their voting rights to a representative, typically a member of the company's management or a proxy advisory firm. This mechanism is crucial for ensuring that all shareholders have a say in the company's decision-making processes, even if they cannot attend meetings in person. By introducing proxy voting for its tokenized equities, Ondo Finance is aligning its governance structure with that of traditional equities, thereby increasing transparency and accountability.

### How Proxy Voting Works on Ondo Finance
The process of proxy voting on Ondo Finance's platform involves several steps:
1. **Registration**: Holders of tokenized equities register on the Ondo Finance platform to participate in voting.
2. **Proposal Submission**: Proposals are submitted by the company or other authorized entities.
3. **Voting Period**: A voting period is announced during which registered holders can cast their votes.
4. **Vote Counting**: Votes are counted, and the outcome is determined based on the majority vote.

### Benefits of Proxy Voting in Tokenized Equities
The introduction of proxy voting in tokenized equities offers several benefits:
- **Increased Participation**: It allows for broader participation in corporate governance, ensuring that all holders have a voice.
- **Enhanced Transparency**: The process is transparent, with clear guidelines and outcomes.
- **Aligns with Traditional Equities**: It brings the governance of tokenized equities more in line with traditional equities, enhancing credibility and trust.
