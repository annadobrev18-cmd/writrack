## Defense Stocks in Focus
The recent escalation of tensions between the US and Iran has significant implications for the defense sector. As the US prepares for a potential prolonged conflict, the need to restock ammunition and replenish supplies becomes a pressing concern. In this context, two defense stocks have been identified as potential beneficiaries of the US campaign against Iran.

### Bernstein's Pick: Two Defense Stocks to Watch
According to Bernstein, the two defense stocks that stand to gain from the current geopolitical situation are Lockheed Martin (LMT) and Northrop Grumman (NOC). Both companies have a strong track record of supplying the US military with advanced weaponry and ammunition.

#### Lockheed Martin: A Leader in Defense Technology
Lockheed Martin is a leading player in the defense sector, with a diverse portfolio of products and services. The company's F-35 fighter jet program is a significant contributor to its revenue, and its missile defense systems are also in high demand. With the US military expected to increase its spending on defense technology, Lockheed Martin is well-positioned to benefit.

#### Northrop Grumman: A Specialist in Ammunition and Missiles
Northrop Grumman is another major player in the defense sector, with a focus on ammunition, missiles, and other advanced weaponry. The company's products are used by the US military and its allies, and its expertise in missile defense systems makes it an attractive partner for governments looking to enhance their defense capabilities.

### Risk Factors: Geopolitical Uncertainty and Budget Constraints
While the current geopolitical situation presents opportunities for defense stocks, there are also risks to consider. The uncertainty surrounding the conflict with Iran and the potential for budget constraints in the US military's spending plans are two key factors that could impact the performance of Lockheed Martin and Northrop Grumman.

#### Geopolitical Uncertainty
The conflict with Iran is a highly unpredictable and fluid situation, with the potential for sudden changes in the geopolitical landscape. This uncertainty could impact the demand for defense products and services, making it challenging for companies like Lockheed Martin and Northrop Grumman to predict their future revenue streams.

#### Budget Constraints
The US military's budget is subject to fluctuations, and any significant reductions in spending could have a negative impact on the defense sector. Lockheed Martin and Northrop Grumman are both heavily reliant on government contracts, and any decline in military spending could affect their revenue and profitability.
