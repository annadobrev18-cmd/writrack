### Historical Context: Geopolitics and Market Volatility
Geopolitical tensions have long been a catalyst for market volatility. Throughout history, events such as wars, sanctions, and diplomatic crises have had a profound impact on global markets. The 1990-1991 Gulf War, for example, led to a significant increase in oil prices, which in turn had a major impact on the global economy. Similarly, the 2014-2015 Russian annexation of Crimea led to a decline in investor confidence, resulting in a sharp decline in stock markets.
