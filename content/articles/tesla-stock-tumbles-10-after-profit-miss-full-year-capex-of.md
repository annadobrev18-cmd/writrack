## Tesla's Q2 Earnings: A Miss on Profit Expectations

Tesla's stock price took a significant hit, tumbling 10% after the company reported a profit miss in its Q2 earnings. Despite confirming a full-year capital expenditure (capex) spend of $25 billion, investors seemed unimpressed, with the stock price plummeting to its lowest level in months. This sudden decline has raised concerns among investors, with many questioning the company's ability to meet its ambitious growth targets.

### Historical Context: Tesla's Previous Earnings

To put this recent development into perspective, it's essential to examine Tesla's previous earnings reports. In Q1 2026, Tesla reported a significant beat on both revenue and earnings per share (EPS), with the company's stock price surging as a result. However, this recent miss on profit expectations has raised concerns that the company may be experiencing a slowdown in growth.

#### Q2 Earnings Report: Key Highlights

The Q2 earnings report revealed the following key highlights:
* Revenue: $24.9 billion, up 15% year-over-year (YoY)
* EPS: $1.25, missing analyst estimates of $1.35
* Capex spend: $25 billion, confirmed for the full year
* Vehicle deliveries: 470,000, up 10% YoY

### Market Impact: Tesla's Stock Price Plummets

The immediate market reaction to Tesla's earnings report was severe, with the stock price plummeting 10% in after-hours trading. This decline has raised concerns among investors, with many questioning the company's ability to meet its ambitious growth targets. The stock's decline has also had a ripple effect on the broader market, with other electric vehicle (EV) stocks experiencing a similar decline.
