The Trump administration has undertaken a sweeping makeover of the White House and Washington, with far-reaching implications for the US economy and global markets. This analysis will delve into the key aspects of Trump's economic agenda, including tax reform, deregulation, and trade policy.

### Tax Reform: A Boost to Economic Growth?
The Trump administration's tax reform package, signed into law in 2017, has been a major driver of economic growth. The package included significant cuts to corporate and individual tax rates, as well as reforms to the tax code aimed at encouraging business investment and job creation. The impact of tax reform on the US economy has been significant, with GDP growth accelerating to 2.9% in 2018, up from 1.6% in 2016.

### Deregulation: A Shift in the Regulatory Landscape
The Trump administration has also undertaken a significant deregulation effort, aimed at reducing the burden of regulatory compliance on businesses. This has included rollbacks of regulations in the financial, energy, and healthcare sectors, among others. The impact of deregulation on the US economy has been mixed, with some industries experiencing significant benefits, while others have faced increased uncertainty and risk.

#### Key Deregulation Efforts
* Repeal of the Dodd-Frank Act's Volcker Rule, which restricted banks' ability to engage in proprietary trading
* Rollback of the Obama administration's Clean Power Plan, which aimed to reduce greenhouse gas emissions from power plants
* Repeal of the Affordable Care Act's (ACA) individual mandate, which required individuals to purchase health insurance

### Trade Policy: A New Era of Protectionism?
The Trump administration's trade policy has been marked by a significant shift towards protectionism, with a focus on reducing the US trade deficit and promoting American exports. This has included the imposition of tariffs on imported goods, as well as renegotiation of trade agreements such as NAFTA and the US-Korea Free Trade Agreement. The impact of trade policy on the US economy has been significant, with some industries experiencing benefits from tariffs, while others have faced increased costs and uncertainty.

## Sector Rotations: Winners and Losers in the Trump Era
The Trump administration's economic agenda has had a significant impact on various sectors of the US economy. Some sectors, such as finance and energy, have experienced significant benefits from deregulation and tax reform, while others, such as healthcare and technology, have faced increased uncertainty and risk.

### Finance Sector: A Beneficiary of Deregulation
The finance sector has been a major beneficiary of the Trump administration's deregulation efforts, with significant rollbacks of regulations aimed at reducing the burden of compliance on banks and other financial institutions. This has included the repeal of the Dodd-Frank Act's Volcker Rule, which restricted banks' ability to engage in proprietary trading.

### Energy Sector: A Boost from Tax Reform and Deregulation
The energy sector has also experienced significant benefits from the Trump administration's economic agenda, including tax reform and deregulation. The tax reform package included significant incentives for oil and gas production, while deregulation efforts have aimed at reducing the burden of regulatory compliance on energy companies.

### Healthcare Sector: A Loser in the Trump Era
The healthcare sector has been a major loser in the Trump era, with significant uncertainty and risk surrounding the future of the Affordable Care Act (ACA). The Trump administration's efforts to repeal and replace the ACA have been unsuccessful, but the repeal of the individual mandate has increased uncertainty for healthcare providers and insurers.
