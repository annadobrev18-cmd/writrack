## Geopolitical Tensions in the Strait of Hormuz

Recently, Japan's Defense Minister, Takaichi, signaled that there are 'high hurdles' for a potential naval dispatch to the region. This development has significant implications for the global oil supply and market stability.

### Impact on Global Oil Supply

The Strait of Hormuz is a critical chokepoint for the global oil trade, with approximately 20% of the world's oil passing through it. Any disruption to the waterway could have far-reaching consequences for the global economy. The potential for a Japanese naval dispatch to the region is seen as a way to mitigate these risks, but the 'high hurdles' mentioned by Takaichi suggest that this may not be a straightforward process.

#### Historical Context

The Strait of Hormuz has been a source of tension for decades, with various incidents and conflicts affecting the region. In 2019, there were several attacks on oil tankers in the Gulf of Oman, which were blamed on Iran. These incidents highlighted the vulnerability of the global oil supply and the need for a stable and secure waterway.
