## XRP's Price Plunge: A Liquidation-Driven Selloff
The cryptocurrency market has witnessed a significant downturn in recent days, with XRP being one of the hardest-hit tokens. As of June 5, 2026, XRP's price has fallen towards $1.10, marking a multi-month low. This downward spiral can be attributed to a liquidation-driven selloff, which has sent shockwaves throughout the market.

### Understanding the Liquidation-Driven Selloff
A liquidation-driven selloff occurs when a large number of investors are forced to sell their assets to meet margin calls or other financial obligations. This can create a cascade effect, where the selling pressure pushes the price of the asset down, triggering even more margin calls and subsequent selling. In the case of XRP, the selloff has been particularly severe, with the token's price plummeting by over 20% in a matter of days.

### Fed Implications: A Hawkish Stance
The recent price action in XRP can also be attributed to the Federal Reserve's hawkish stance on monetary policy. The Fed's decision to maintain high interest rates has led to a strengthening of the US dollar, making cryptocurrencies like XRP less attractive to investors. Furthermore, the Fed's emphasis on regulating the cryptocurrency market has created uncertainty, contributing to the selloff.
