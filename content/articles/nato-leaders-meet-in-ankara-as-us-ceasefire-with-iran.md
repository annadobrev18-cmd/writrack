## Geopolitical Landscape: Understanding the Current Scenario
The recent meeting of NATO leaders in Ankara, Turkey, has brought to the forefront the escalating geopolitical tensions between the United States and Iran. The fragile ceasefire between the two nations is on the brink of collapse, and the implications of such an event are far-reaching, affecting not only the regional stability but also the global economy.

### Historical Context: US-Iran Relations
To grasp the complexity of the situation, it's essential to delve into the historical context of US-Iran relations. The two countries have been at odds since the 1979 Iranian Revolution, which led to the overthrow of the US-backed Shah. The subsequent hostage crisis and the US imposition of economic sanctions have contributed to the deteriorating relationship. The Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action (JCPOA), also known as the Iran nuclear deal, was a significant attempt to normalize relations, but the US withdrawal from the agreement in 2018 has led to a resurgence of tensions.
