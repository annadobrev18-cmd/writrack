## Risk Factors Associated with Infrastructure Disruptions
Infrastructure disruptions, such as the fire at the Rio Olympic Park velodrome, pose significant risks to global events. These risks can be categorized into three main areas: financial, reputational, and operational.

### Financial Risks
The financial risks associated with infrastructure disruptions include the potential loss of revenue, increased costs, and decreased asset value. In the case of the Rio Olympic Park velodrome, the fire may have resulted in damage to the venue, leading to increased repair costs and potential losses in revenue.

### Reputational Risks
Reputational risks are also a significant concern for global events. The perception of a venue or event as unsafe or unreliable can have long-term consequences, including decreased attendance and sponsorship revenue.

### Operational Risks
Operational risks, such as the disruption of event schedules and logistics, can also have a significant impact on global events. The fire at the Rio Olympic Park velodrome may have required the relocation of events or the implementation of alternative arrangements, resulting in increased costs and logistical challenges.
