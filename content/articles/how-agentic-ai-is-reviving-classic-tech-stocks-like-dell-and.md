### Understanding Agentic AI

Agentic AI refers to a type of artificial intelligence that enables systems to make decisions and take actions autonomously. This technology has been gaining traction in recent years, with applications in various industries such as healthcare, finance, and manufacturing. The agentic phase of systems design is characterized by the ability of systems to adapt and learn from their environment, making them more efficient and effective.

### Impact on CPU Demand

The growth of Agentic AI has led to an increase in demand for CPUs, as these systems require powerful processing capabilities to function effectively. This has resulted in a surge in sales for companies like Intel and Dell, which specialize in the production of CPUs and other computer hardware. The sustained demand for CPUs is expected to continue, driven by the increasing adoption of Agentic AI in various industries.

### Sector Rotations

The growth of Agentic AI has also led to sector rotations, with investors shifting their focus from traditional tech stocks to those that are more closely related to AI and machine learning. This has resulted in a surge in demand for stocks like NVIDIA and AMD, which specialize in the production of graphics processing units (GPUs) and other AI-related hardware.

### Global Ripple Effects

The growth of Agentic AI is not limited to the tech industry, with ripple effects being felt across various sectors. For example, the healthcare industry is using Agentic AI to develop more effective treatments and diagnosis methods, while the finance industry is using it to improve risk management and portfolio optimization. The adoption of Agentic AI is expected to continue, driven by its ability to improve efficiency and effectiveness in various industries.
