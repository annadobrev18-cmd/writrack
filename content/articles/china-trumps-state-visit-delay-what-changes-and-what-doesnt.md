### Historical Context
To understand the current state of US-China trade relations, it's essential to look at the historical context. The US and China have a long history of trade, dating back to the 18th century. ## The Impact of Trump's State Visit Delay
The delay of Trump's state visit to China has significant implications for US-China trade relations. A state visit is a symbol of diplomatic relations between two countries and is often used to strengthen economic ties. The delay of such a visit can be seen as a sign of strained relations, which can have a negative impact on trade.

### Economic Implications
The delay of Trump's state visit can have several economic implications. Firstly, it can lead to a decrease in investor confidence, which can result in a decline in trade between the two countries. Secondly, it can lead to an increase in tariffs, which can make US goods more expensive for Chinese consumers, leading to a decline in exports.

## Risk Factors
There are several risk factors that can affect US-China trade relations. These include:

### Trade Wars
Trade wars are a significant risk factor for US-China trade relations. The Trump administration has been engaged in a trade war with China, imposing tariffs on Chinese goods and restricting Chinese investment in the US. China has retaliated with its own tariffs, leading to a decline in trade between the two countries.

### Currency Manipulation
Currency manipulation is another risk factor for US-China trade relations. The US has accused China of manipulating its currency to gain a trade advantage. China has denied these allegations, but the issue remains a point of contention between the two countries.
