## Aave's Exploit and Subsequent Restoration of Ether Borrowing Limits

The recent exploit on Aave, resulting in a loss of $230 million, has sent shockwaves through the cryptocurrency and DeFi (Decentralized Finance) communities. This significant event not only affects Aave but also has broader implications for the entire DeFi ecosystem. Following the exploit, Aave has taken swift action to restore ether borrowing limits, a crucial step towards stabilizing the platform and reassuring its users.

### Understanding the Exploit

The exploit in question was related to the use of rETH (recursive Ethereum), a token that represents a claim on the yield generated by ETH staked on the Ethereum network. The crisis began when a user manipulated the price of rETH by creating a series of complex transactions that artificially inflated its value. This manipulation allowed the user to borrow a large amount of ether (WETH) against the overvalued rETH collateral. Once the borrowed ether was withdrawn, the manipulator's actions caused a significant drop in the price of rETH, leaving Aave with a substantial amount of undercollateralized loans.

### Restoration of Borrowing Limits

In response to the exploit, Aave initially froze borrowing for ether (WETH) to prevent further potential manipulations and losses. This move was a precautionary measure to safeguard the platform's liquidity and protect its users. Following a thorough analysis and implementation of additional security measures, Aave has restored the borrowing limits for WETH. This restoration is a critical step towards returning to normal operations and regaining user trust.
