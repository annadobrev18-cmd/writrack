Stablecoins have been gaining significant attention in recent years, and according to a report by BCA Research, they are becoming a macro-relevant financial layer. This development has far-reaching implications for the cryptocurrency market and the broader financial system. ### Historical Context: The Evolution of Stablecoins
Stablecoins are a type of cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value relative to a fiat currency, such as the US dollar. The first stablecoin, Tether (USDT), was launched in 2014, but it wasn't until 2018 that the concept gained significant traction. The introduction of new stablecoins, such as USDC and PAX, marked a turning point in the market, as they offered a more transparent and auditable alternative to Tether.
