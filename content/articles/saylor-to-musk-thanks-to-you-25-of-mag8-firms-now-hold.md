#### Early Adoption
In the early days of bitcoin, adoption was primarily driven by individual investors and enthusiasts. However, as the cryptocurrency gained popularity, institutional investors began to take notice. In 2017, the launch of bitcoin futures by the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME) marked a significant turning point, as it provided a regulated platform for institutional investors to gain exposure to bitcoin.

### Risk Factors and Challenges
While institutional bitcoin adoption is on the rise, there are still several risk factors and challenges that investors need to be aware of. These include:

* Regulatory uncertainty: Despite improved regulatory clarity, there is still a risk of adverse regulatory changes that could negatively impact bitcoin's price and adoption.
* Market volatility: Bitcoin's price can be highly volatile, which may make it challenging for institutional investors to manage their exposure.
* Security risks: Bitcoin's decentralized nature and lack of central authority make it vulnerable to security risks, such as hacking and theft.
