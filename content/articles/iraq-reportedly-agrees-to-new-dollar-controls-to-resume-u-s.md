## Iraq's New Dollar Controls: An Overview
Iraq has reportedly agreed to implement new dollar controls, a move that is expected to resume US cash shipments to the country. This development has significant implications for the global economy, particularly in the context of currency markets and international trade.

### Historical Context
To understand the significance of this agreement, it's essential to delve into the historical context of Iraq's currency situation. The Iraqi dinar (IQD) has been subject to various fluctuations over the years, largely due to the country's economic and political instability. The US dollar, on the other hand, has been a widely accepted currency in Iraq, particularly for international transactions.

#### US Cash Shipments to Iraq
The US has been shipping cash to Iraq for several years, with the aim of supporting the country's financial system and stabilizing its currency. However, these shipments have been suspended on several occasions due to concerns over money laundering and the financing of terrorist activities.

## Fundamentals of the New Dollar Controls
The new dollar controls agreed upon by Iraq are designed to prevent the misuse of US dollars and ensure that they are used for legitimate purposes. The controls include measures such as:

*   Limiting the amount of cash that can be withdrawn from banks
*   Implementing stricter know-your-customer (KYC) and anti-money laundering (AML) regulations
*   Enhancing surveillance and monitoring of financial transactions
