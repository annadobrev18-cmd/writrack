## The Houthi Blockade: A Geopolitical Power Play
The recent Houthi blockade in the Red Sea has significant implications for global energy supplies, particularly in the context of Iran's growing influence in the region. The blockade, which has been in place since early 2023, has restricted the flow of oil tankers through the strategic Strait of Bab-el-Mandeb, a critical chokepoint for international trade.

### Historical Context: The Strait of Bab-el-Mandeb
The Strait of Bab-el-Mandeb, located between Yemen and Djibouti, is a vital waterway that connects the Red Sea to the Gulf of Aden. The strait is approximately 20 miles wide and is a critical passage for oil tankers and other ships traveling between Europe and Asia. The strait's strategic importance cannot be overstated, as it is estimated that over 10% of global oil supplies pass through this waterway.

#### The Role of Iran in the Region
Iran, a major oil producer, has been expanding its influence in the region, particularly in Yemen, where the Houthi rebels have been fighting a civil war against the government. The Houthi rebels, who are backed by Iran, have been using the blockade as a means to exert pressure on the international community to recognize their claims in the region. The blockade has also allowed Iran to increase its control over the flow of oil in the region, thereby tightening its grip on global energy supplies.
