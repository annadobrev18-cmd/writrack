## Iran War Impact on Global Natural Gas Market
The recent conflict in Iran has significantly altered the global natural gas market, with liquid natural gas (LNG) plays emerging as the primary beneficiaries. According to Goldman Sachs, the constrained supply of natural gas, coupled with the potential easing of demand due to higher prices, is expected to drive the growth of LNG stocks.

### Historical Context: Global Natural Gas Market
The global natural gas market has experienced significant fluctuations in recent years, driven by factors such as changes in demand, supply chain disruptions, and geopolitical events. The COVID-19 pandemic, for instance, led to a decline in natural gas demand, resulting in a surplus of supply. However, the recent conflict in Iran has disrupted the global energy landscape, leading to a shortage of natural gas supply.

#### Pre-War Global Natural Gas Market
Prior to the Iran war, the global natural gas market was characterized by a surplus of supply, driven by the shale gas revolution in the United States and the growth of LNG exports. The market was also influenced by the increasing demand for natural gas from countries such as China, India, and Japan, which were seeking to reduce their reliance on coal and other fossil fuels.

#### Post-War Global Natural Gas Market
The Iran war has significantly altered the global natural gas market, with the conflict leading to a disruption of natural gas supply from the region. The war has also led to an increase in geopolitical tensions, resulting in a surge in natural gas prices. The constrained supply of natural gas, coupled with the potential easing of demand due to higher prices, is expected to drive the growth of LNG stocks.
