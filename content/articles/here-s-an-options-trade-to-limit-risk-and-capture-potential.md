## Options Trading Strategy
For investors seeking defined-risk exposure to a potential auto recovery, an options trading strategy could be an attractive way to capture potential returns while limiting downside risk. One such strategy is a bull spread, which involves buying a call option with a lower strike price and selling a call option with a higher strike price.

### Bull Spread Example
For example, an investor could buy a call option with a strike price of $150 and sell a call option with a strike price of $160. This would give the investor the right to buy Toyota's stock at $150 and sell it at $160, capturing the potential upside if the stock price rises above $160.

### Risk Management
The key benefit of this strategy is that it limits the investor's downside risk to the premium paid for the options. If the stock price falls below $150, the investor's loss is limited to the premium paid, rather than the full value of the stock.
