### Background: The Importance of the Strait of Hormuz

The Strait of Hormuz is approximately 90 nautical miles long and 21-25 miles wide at its narrowest point. It is the only sea passage to the open ocean for oil exports from several major oil-producing countries in the region, including Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates. According to the US Energy Information Administration (EIA), in 2020, about 20% of the world's oil passed through the Strait of Hormuz.

#### Economic Impact of Mine Clearance

The economic implications of mine clearance in the Strait of Hormuz are substantial. Any disruption to oil shipments through this waterway could lead to significant increases in oil prices, affecting the global economy. The partnership between France and Oman to clear mines from the Strait of Hormuz is, therefore, a critical step in ensuring the stability of global oil markets.
