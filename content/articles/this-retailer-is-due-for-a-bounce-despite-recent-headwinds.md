## On Holding's Current State
On Holding, a prominent retailer, has been navigating through challenging times recently, facing headwinds from a leadership shakeup and broader macroeconomic uncertainties. However, according to a recent analysis by Raymond James, the company is poised to bounce back from these setbacks. This prediction is based on the retailer's current valuation, which is seen as a discount compared to its peers and historical performance.

### Historical Context
To understand the potential for a bounce, it's essential to look at On Holding's historical performance and how it has handled similar challenges in the past. The company has demonstrated resilience in the face of adversity, consistently showing the ability to adapt and grow. For instance, during the COVID-19 pandemic, On Holding successfully pivoted its strategy to focus on e-commerce and direct-to-consumer sales, mitigating the impact of store closures.

### Technical Analysis
From a technical standpoint, On Holding's stock has been showing signs of a potential bounce. The stock price has been consolidating near a key support level, and indicators such as the Relative Strength Index (RSI) suggest that the stock is oversold, which could lead to a rebound. Furthermore, the Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) is indicating a buy signal, as the MACD line has crossed above the signal line, suggesting upward momentum.

#### Chart Patterns
The chart pattern for On Holding also reveals a potential inverse head and shoulders formation, which is a bullish reversal pattern. If the stock price can break through the neckline of this pattern, it could confirm the bullish outlook and lead to a significant price increase.

## Expert Opinions
Experts at Raymond James are not the only ones who see potential in On Holding. Other analysts have also pointed out the company's strong fundamentals and the potential for a rebound. The consensus estimate among analysts is for On Holding to see significant revenue and earnings growth in the coming years. This growth is expected to be driven by the company's continued focus on e-commerce, the expansion of its product lines, and the potential for new market entries.

In conclusion, despite the recent headwinds, On Holding is poised for a bounce, according to Raymond James. As the retail sector continues to evolve, On Holding's ability to adapt and innovate will be crucial in determining its success.
