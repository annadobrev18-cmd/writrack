## Fundamentals of Nvidia and Boeing
Nvidia and Boeing are two of the top Dow stocks that are currently near their buy points. Nvidia, a leader in the technology sector, has been experiencing significant growth due to the increasing demand for its graphics processing units (GPUs) and high-performance computing products. Boeing, on the other hand, is a major player in the aerospace and defense industry, with a diverse portfolio of commercial and military aircraft.

### Historical Performance
Both Nvidia and Boeing have demonstrated strong historical performance, with Nvidia's stock price increasing by over 50% in the past year, and Boeing's stock price rising by over 20%. This growth can be attributed to their innovative products and services, as well as their ability to adapt to changing market conditions.

## Valuation of Nvidia and Boeing
The valuation of Nvidia and Boeing is a critical aspect of their analysis. Both companies have a high price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio, indicating that they are trading at a premium to their earnings.

### Price-to-Earnings Ratio
The P/E ratio of Nvidia is currently around 50, while Boeing's P/E ratio is around 25. This suggests that investors are willing to pay a higher price for Nvidia's earnings compared to Boeing's. However, this also means that Nvidia's stock price may be more volatile and sensitive to changes in earnings.

### Enterprise Value-to-EBITDA Ratio
The enterprise value-to-EBITDA (EV/EBITDA) ratio is another important valuation metric. Nvidia's EV/EBITDA ratio is around 20, while Boeing's EV/EBITDA ratio is around 15. This indicates that Nvidia's stock price is trading at a higher multiple of its earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (EBITDA) compared to Boeing.

## Risk Factors
Both Nvidia and Boeing are exposed to various risk factors that could impact their stock prices.

### Regulatory Risks
Nvidia is subject to regulatory risks related to its business in China, where it generates a significant portion of its revenue. Boeing, on the other hand, is exposed to regulatory risks related to its commercial and military aircraft business, including potential changes in government regulations and policies.

### Competitive Risks
Nvidia faces intense competition in the technology sector, particularly from companies such as Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) and Intel Corporation. Boeing, similarly, faces competition from other aerospace and defense companies, including Airbus and Lockheed Martin.

### Economic Risks
Both Nvidia and Boeing are exposed to economic risks, including potential downturns in the global economy. Nvidia's business is highly dependent on consumer spending, which could be impacted by economic conditions. Boeing's business, on the other hand, is dependent on government spending and commercial airline demand, which could also be impacted by economic conditions.
