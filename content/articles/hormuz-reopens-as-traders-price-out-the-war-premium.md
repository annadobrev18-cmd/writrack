## Global Market Implications of the Hormuz Reopening
The reopening of the Strait of Hormuz has sent shockwaves throughout the global economy, particularly in the energy sector. As traders price out the war premium, oil prices have begun to fluctuate, impacting various industries and markets worldwide.

### Historical Context of the Hormuz Strait
The Strait of Hormuz, a vital waterway connecting the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman, has long been a point of contention in global politics. Its strategic location makes it a crucial chokepoint for international oil trade, with approximately 20% of the world's oil supply passing through it. The strait's closure or disruption has historically led to significant increases in oil prices, as was seen during the Iran-Iraq War in the 1980s.
