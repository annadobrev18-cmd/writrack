## Geopolitical Uncertainty and Precious Metals
The recent surge in silver and gold prices can be attributed to the shifting bets on Iran, highlighting the significant impact of geopolitical tensions on the precious metals market. As investors and traders closely monitor the situation, they are seeking safe-haven assets to mitigate potential risks. This trend is not new, as precious metals have historically served as a hedge against uncertainty and inflation.

### Historical Context
To understand the current market dynamics, it is essential to examine the historical relationship between geopolitical events and precious metals prices. The 1979 Iranian Revolution, for instance, led to a significant increase in gold prices, as investors sought safe-haven assets amidst the chaos. Similarly, the 1990-1991 Gulf War saw a surge in gold prices, as the global economy was impacted by the conflict.
